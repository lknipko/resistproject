# Demand AI Accountability

## Quick Summary

AI systems are making consequential decisions about your job applications, healthcare, and interactions with law enforcement — often without your knowledge. Congress **again rejected a moratorium on state AI laws**, and Trump is preparing a **"ONE RULE" executive order** to preempt them administratively. The bipartisan **"Great American AI Act"** advanced in committee (June 25), while **Texas opened an investigation into Character.AI and Meta** over chatbots marketed to children. Over 100 state AI laws have passed in 2026. Below you'll find call scripts, email templates, and concrete ways to fight AI discrimination and protect your rights.

### Submit a Public Comment on the HUD Equal Access Rule

The **FTC and FCC** are developing AI-related policies per EO 14365. Monitor [Regulations.gov](https://www.regulations.gov/) for comment periods.

**How to submit effective comments:**
1. Visit [Regulations.gov](https://www.regulations.gov)
2. Search for AI-related rulemaking by FTC, FCC, or FDA
3. Explain how AI systems affect you personally (hiring, healthcare, policing)
4. Be specific about which provisions you support or oppose
5. Cite evidence — personal experience counts

## Sustained Actions

### Support State AI Laws

States are leading on AI accountability. Support your state's efforts:

- **Colorado (SB 24-205):** Takes effect **June 30, 2026**. Requires reasonable care to prevent AI discrimination. Contact your state legislators to ensure implementation stays on track.
- **Illinois (HB 3773):** Already in effect. If an employer uses AI in hiring and you're in Illinois, you have the right to be notified.
- **California (SB 942, SB 53, AB 325):** Multiple AI transparency laws taking effect in 2026. Support state AG enforcement.
- **Your state:** Check [Transparency Coalition AI Legislation Tracker](https://transparencycoalition.ai) for pending AI bills in your state.

### Know Your Rights: AI in Hiring

If you are a job applicant in 2026:

- **NYC:** Employers must tell you if AI is used in hiring decisions and disclose audit results (Local Law 144)
- **Illinois:** Employers must notify you of AI use in employment decisions (HB 3773)
- **Colorado (after June 30):** You have the right to be told when an AI system makes an adverse decision about you

**If you suspect AI discrimination:**
1. Document the interaction (screenshots, dates, job posting)
2. File a complaint with the [EEOC](https://www.eeoc.gov/filing-charge-discrimination) (federal)
3. File a complaint with your state attorney general
4. Contact the organizations listed below

### Advocate for Local Facial Recognition Bans

Cities have led on banning facial recognition. Push your local government to follow:

- **Models to cite:** San Francisco, Boston, Portland (OR), Somerville (MA), Santa Cruz (CA)
- **Attend city council meetings** when police surveillance tools are on the agenda
- **Request transparency** from local police about what AI tools they use
- **File public records requests** about facial recognition and predictive policing contracts

### Report AI-Generated Election Deepfakes

The 2026 midterms face unprecedented deepfake threats:

- **Report unlabeled AI-generated political content** to your state election officials
- **Check your state's law:** [Public Citizen Deepfake Tracker](https://www.citizen.org/article/tracker-legislation-on-deepfakes-in-elections/)
- **Verify before sharing:** Use reverse image search and media literacy tools
- **Report to platforms:** Flag AI-generated content on social media platforms
