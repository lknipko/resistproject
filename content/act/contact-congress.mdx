## Email Your Representatives

### Email Your Senators - Demand DOGE Oversight

<EmailTemplate
  subject="Demand congressional oversight of DOGE"
  repType="senator"
  body={`Dear Senator {repName},

I am writing as your constituent from {zipCode} to urge you to demand congressional oversight of the Department of Government Efficiency (DOGE).

DOGE has fired over 260,000 federal employees, accessed Treasury systems controlling $6.8 trillion in annual payments, and operates with zero transparency. Elon Musk's $15 billion in government contracts create an unprecedented conflict of interest.

The Constitution grants Congress the power to determine executive branch structure and spending. DOGE is bypassing this authority entirely.

I urge you to:
- Support congressional hearings on DOGE operations
- Demand GAO audits of all DOGE actions and contracts
- Restore congressional oversight of federal workforce decisions

Thank you for your attention to this urgent matter.

Sincerely,
{firstName} {lastName}
{zipCode}`}
/>

## Sustained Actions

### [+] Join Advocacy Organizations

- **Indivisible** - Local groups organizing calls, town halls, and actions
- **5 Calls** - Daily call scripts on urgent issues
- **Resistbot** - Text-based tool for contacting representatives
- **Common Cause** - Democracy and voting rights advocacy

### [+] Attend Town Halls

Check your representative's website for town hall schedules. Prepare questions in advance and attend with allies.

### [+] Write Letters to the Editor

Local newspapers reach your representative's constituents. Keep under 200 words, reference recent news, and include your town.

## Resources

### [+] Call Tips

**Best times to call:**
- 9-11 AM local time (staff are fresh)
- Avoid Mondays and Fridays (busiest days)

**What to expect:**
- You'll speak to a staffer (not the representative)
- Calls are tallied by issue
- Keep it brief (under 2 minutes)
- Be polite but firm

**What to say:**
1. Your name and zip code (proves you're a constituent)
2. The issue you're calling about
3. What action you want them to take
4. Thank them for their time

### [+] Email Tips

**Subject lines matter:**
- Be specific: "Vote NO on H.R. 1234"
- Not generic: "Important issue"

**Body structure:**
1. Who you are (constituent from [zip])
2. What you want (specific bill or action)
3. Why it matters (personal impact or principle)
4. Thank them for consideration

### [+] Follow Up

- Call/write multiple times on the same issue
- Mention previous contacts: "I called last week about..."
- Track your representatives' votes on [GovTrack](https://www.govtrack.us)
