## Quick Actions

### Start Contributing Today

**For New Contributors (Tier 1):**
1. Browse [Learn pages](/learn) or [Act pages](/act) to find content to improve
2. Click "Suggest Edit" on any page
3. Make your changes, write a clear summary
4. Submit for community review
5. Your edit needs 10 approval points from the community

**First Edit Bonus:** Your first approved edit automatically promotes you to Tier 2, making future edits easier to approve (only 5 points needed).

### Propose a New Page

Don't see coverage of an important issue?

1. Visit the [Learn](/learn) or [Act](/act) landing page
2. Click the "Propose New Page" card at the bottom
3. Use the provided template
4. Follow the content principles below
5. Submit for community review

## How the Edit Proposal System Works

### The Review Process

**1. You Propose an Edit**
- Click "Suggest Edit" on any page
- Make changes in the MDX editor
- Write a clear edit summary explaining your changes
- Submit your proposal

**2. Community Reviews**
- Your proposal appears in the [Review Queue](/review)
- Community members vote to approve or reject
- Votes are weighted by tier (see below)
- Voters can leave comments with feedback

**3. Automatic Resolution**
- **Tier 1 proposals:** Approved at +10 points, rejected at -10 points
- **Tier 2 proposals:** Approved at +5 points, rejected at -5 points
- **Tier 3+ proposals:** Instantly approved (no review needed)
- You earn reputation for approved edits
- You gain tier progression for quality contributions

### What Gets Approved?

**Good Edit Proposals:**
- ✅ Add primary source citations
- ✅ Update facts with new government documents
- ✅ Fix factual errors with proper sourcing
- ✅ Improve clarity and organization
- ✅ Add missing context or analysis (properly labeled)
- ✅ Fix typos or formatting issues

**Edit Proposals That Get Rejected:**
- ❌ Unsourced claims or opinions
- ❌ News media as primary sources (use for context only)
- ❌ Mixing facts and analysis without clear separation
- ❌ Partisan framing or inflammatory language
- ❌ Spam, vandalism, or off-topic content
- ❌ Plagiarism or copyright violations

## The Tier System

### Tier 1: New Contributor
- **Starting tier** for all new users
- Can propose edits (needs 10 vote points for approval)
- Can vote on proposals (1 point per vote)
- **Daily limits:** 3 edits, 10 votes
- **Promotion:** 1 approved edit → Tier 2

### Tier 2: Contributor
- **Auto-promoted** after first approved edit
- Proposals need only 5 vote points for approval
- Votes worth 2 points each
- **Daily limits:** 10 edits, 30 votes
- **Promotion:** 5 approved edits + 100 reputation → Tier 3

### Tier 3: Trusted Contributor
- **Auto-promoted** after 5 approved edits + 100 reputation
- **All edits instantly approved** (no review needed)
- Votes worth 3 points each
- Can access [Moderator Dashboard](/admin/review-edits)
- **Daily limits:** 50 edits, 100 votes
- **Promotion:** Manual promotion to Tier 4 (by administrators)

### Tier 4: Moderator
- Manual promotion by administrators
- Can instantly approve or reject any proposal
- Can moderate other users' proposals
- Trusted with platform governance

### Tier 5: Administrator
- Manual promotion for core maintainers
- Full system access
- Can manage users and system settings

## Reputation & Badges

### How You Earn Reputation

- **Edit approved:** +10 reputation
- **Edit rejected:** -5 reputation
- **Vote cast:** +1 reputation
- **Vote with majority:** +2 bonus reputation
- **Review completed:** +2 reputation

### Badge System

Earn badges for milestones:
- **Tier Badges:** Automatically awarded when you reach each tier
- **Contribution Badges:** 1st edit, 10th edit, 100th edit, etc.
- **Engagement Badges:** Voting, reviewing, community participation
- **Quality Badges:** High approval rates, trusted sources

## Content Principles

Follow these principles when creating or editing content:

### 1. Primary Sources Only

**Always use:**
- Federal Register notices
- Executive orders (WhiteHouse.gov)
- Court filings and decisions (PACER, CourtListener)
- Congressional bills and records (Congress.gov)
- Official agency statements (.gov domains)
- Inspector General reports

**Avoid:**
- News media as primary sources (use for context only)
- Opinion pieces or editorials
- Social media posts (except official government accounts)
- Unverified claims or rumors

### 2. Separate Facts from Analysis

**LEARN pages structure:**
- **Facts section:** Objective, verifiable, primary-sourced information
  - Timeline of events with dates
  - Direct quotes from source materials
  - Links to primary source documents
- **Analysis section:** Interpretation, context, and explanation
  - What this means
  - Who's affected
  - Legal/policy context
  - Expert perspectives (attributed)

**Clear labeling prevents misinformation.**

### 3. Non-Partisan Framing

Focus on:
- Constitutional rights and principles
- Legal standards and precedents
- Material impacts on people
- Factual accuracy over political narratives

Avoid:
- Partisan language or framing
- "Us vs. them" rhetoric
- Party politics (focus on actions, not parties)
- Emotional manipulation

### 4. Actionable Information

Every LEARN page should link to relevant ACT pages.

**ACT pages should provide:**
- **Quick Actions** (under 5 minutes): Call scripts, email templates, petitions
- **Sustained Actions**: Organizations to join, events, volunteer opportunities
- **Resources**: Downloadable materials, talking points, guides

### 5. Credibility Is Paramount

**One bad source undermines everything.**

- Double-check every link
- Verify dates and facts
- Use archive.org for sources that might disappear
- Link directly to source documents, not news articles about them

## Rate Limits & Moderation

### Daily Limits

Rate limits prevent spam and ensure quality:
- **Tier 1:** 3 edits/day, 10 votes/day
- **Tier 2:** 10 edits/day, 30 votes/day
- **Tier 3+:** 50 edits/day, 100 votes/day

Limits reset daily at midnight UTC.

### Validation Rules

All edits must:
- Change at least 3 words
- Pass profanity filter
- Include an edit summary
- Be valid MDX syntax

### Audit Logging

All actions are logged:
- Edit proposals and votes
- Tier promotions
- Reputation changes
- Moderation actions

This ensures transparency and accountability.

## Sustained Actions

### Join the Community

**Get Involved:**
- Regularly review proposals in the [Review Queue](/review)
- Vote on edits to help maintain quality
- Provide constructive feedback in comments
- Help new contributors improve their submissions

**Stay Updated:**
- Check for new proposals daily
- Follow issues you care about
- Help keep content current with new developments

### Quality Over Quantity

Focus on:
- Thorough research before proposing edits
- Clear, well-sourced contributions
- Constructive feedback on others' proposals
- Building consensus in the community

**Remember:** One high-quality, well-sourced edit is worth more than ten rushed submissions.

### Personalized Action Components

When creating or editing **Act pages**, you can add personalized email and call components that automatically identify users' representatives and pre-fill contact information.

**Available Components:**
- `<RepresentativeCard />` - Shows user's 2 senators + 1 house rep
- `<EmailTemplate>` - Pre-filled email templates with mailto: links
- `<CallRepButton>` - Click-to-call buttons with optional scripts
- `<EmailMessage>` and `<CallScript>` - For multiple topics per page

**Single Message Example:**
```jsx
<EmailTemplate
  subject="Subject line with {repName} variable"
  repType="senator"
  body={`Dear Senator {repName},

I am writing from {zipCode} to urge you to...

Sincerely,
{firstName} {lastName}`}
/>
```

**Multiple Topics Example:**
```jsx
<EmailTemplate repType="all">
  <EmailMessage
    topic="Budget Concerns"
    subject="Oppose budget cuts"
    body={`Dear {repName},...`}
  />
  <EmailMessage
    topic="Constitutional Issues"
    subject="Protect constitutional rights"
    body={`Dear {repName},...`}
  />
</EmailTemplate>
```

**Available Variables:**
- `{firstName}` - User's first name
- `{lastName}` - User's last name
- `{zipCode}` - User's zip code
- `{repName}` - Representative's name (auto-filled per rep)

**repType Options:**
- `"senator"` - Show only senators (2 people)
- `"representative"` - Show only house rep (1 person)
- `"all"` - Show all reps (default)

**When to use multiple topics:**
- If you have 2+ related messages on the same issue
- A topic selector appears, letting users switch between messages
- Keep topic labels short (under 20 characters)

See `templates/personalized-action.md` for detailed documentation and examples.
