# Support Supreme Court Reform

## Quick Summary

**Goal:** Demand enforceable ethics rules for Supreme Court justices, support legislation for term limits and court reform, and hold elected officials accountable for their positions on judicial accountability.

The Supreme Court operates under a voluntary ethics code with no enforcement mechanism -- the only branch of government without binding ethics rules. Justices have accepted millions in undisclosed gifts, refused to recuse from cases involving personal conflicts, and faced zero consequences. Congress has the power to change this, but only if constituents demand it.

## Sustained Actions

### Support Reform Organizations

These organizations are leading the fight for judicial accountability and court reform:

**Fix the Court**
- **Website:** [fixthecourt.com](https://fixthecourt.com/)
- **Mission:** Nonpartisan organization advocating for Supreme Court transparency and accountability, including term limits, ethics reform, and live audio of oral arguments.
- **How to help:** Sign up for action alerts, donate, share their research.

**Demand Justice**
- **Website:** [demandjustice.org](https://demandjustice.org/)
- **Mission:** Progressive organization focused on court reform, including court expansion, ethics enforcement, and opposition to extreme judicial nominees.
- **How to help:** Join their campaigns, phone banking, donate.

**American Constitution Society (ACS)**
- **Website:** [acslaw.org](https://www.acslaw.org/)
- **Mission:** Progressive legal organization and counterweight to the Federalist Society. Develops legal talent and progressive judicial philosophy.
- **How to help:** Join a local chapter, attend events, support their judicial nominations work.

**People for the American Way**
- **Website:** [pfaw.org](https://www.pfaw.org/)
- **Mission:** Monitors judicial nominations and advocates for an independent judiciary. Tracks nominees' records and organizes opposition to extreme appointees.
- **How to help:** Sign up for action alerts on judicial nominations, donate.

**Take Back the Court**
- **Website:** [takebackthecourt.org](https://takebackthecourt.org/)
- **Mission:** Advocates specifically for court expansion as a structural reform to restore balance.
- **How to help:** Join their campaign, attend events, contact legislators.

**Alliance for Justice**
- **Website:** [afj.org](https://www.afj.org/)
- **Mission:** Advocates for a fair and independent judiciary. Tracks judicial nominees and provides resources on court reform.
- **How to help:** Use their judicial nomination toolkit, attend events.

### Attend Judicial Nomination Hearings

**Why it matters:** After *Trump v. CASA* ended universal injunctions, every federal judge matters more than ever. Lower court judges are where most cases are decided.

**How to participate:**
1. **Senate Judiciary Committee hearings** are public and can be attended in person or watched live.
   - Schedule: [judiciary.senate.gov](https://www.judiciary.senate.gov/hearings)
   - Live stream: [senate.gov/committees](https://www.senate.gov/committees/hearings_meetings.htm)

2. **Submit written testimony** -- The Judiciary Committee accepts written statements from the public on judicial nominees.
   - Contact the committee through their [website](https://www.judiciary.senate.gov/contact)

3. **Track nominees** -- Know who is being nominated and their record.
   - [Alliance for Justice - Judicial Nominations](https://www.afj.org/our-work/judicial-nominations/)
   - [Ballotpedia - Federal Judicial Nominations](https://ballotpedia.org/Federal_judicial_nominations)

4. **Demand ethics questions** -- Call your senators before hearings and demand they ask nominees about:
   - Gift disclosure policies
   - Recusal standards
   - Views on judicial ethics enforcement
   - Federalist Society membership and activities

### Demand State Action

**State Attorneys General:**
- State AGs can file amicus briefs in Supreme Court cases.
- State AGs can challenge federal policies in court (especially important post-*CASA*).
- Pressure your AG to take action: [State AG Directory](https://www.naag.org/attorneys-general/the-attorneys-general/)

**State Legislatures:**
- States can pass resolutions calling for Supreme Court reform.
- States can pass Article V convention applications for a constitutional amendment on term limits.
- Contact your state legislators about court reform resolutions.

**Call Script for State AG:**

> "I'm calling to urge Attorney General [NAME] to file amicus briefs in pending Supreme Court cases that affect [STATE] residents. After *Trump v. CASA* ended universal injunctions, state attorneys general are among the most important advocates for residents' rights in federal court. I urge the AG to use every available legal tool to protect our state's interests."

### Support Investigative Journalism

The public knows about Supreme Court ethics violations because of investigative journalism, particularly ProPublica's Pulitzer Prize-winning reporting. Support the organizations doing this work:

- **ProPublica** - [propublica.org/donate](https://www.propublica.org/donate) -- Nonprofit investigative journalism
- **The Marshall Project** - [themarshallproject.org](https://www.themarshallproject.org/) -- Criminal justice reporting
- **The Trace** - [thetrace.org](https://www.thetrace.org/) -- Gun violence journalism
- **Your local newspaper** -- Subscribe and support local court coverage

## When to Act: Key Moments

**Judicial Nomination Hearings:**
- Call before hearings to demand ethics questions
- Submit written testimony
- Contact senators on Judiciary Committee

**Supreme Court Decision Days:**
- Typically June-July for major cases
- Respond immediately with calls and emails about reform
- Share decisions on social media with reform messaging

**Legislative Windows:**
- When ethics or reform bills are introduced or move through committee
- During budget debates (funding for ethics enforcement)
- During election seasons (demand candidates' positions on reform)

**Ethics Revelations:**
- When new reporting reveals gifts, conflicts, or violations
- Use the moment to renew pressure for enforceable rules
- Share the facts widely
