# Defend Public Education

## Quick Summary

The Department of Education is being dismantled from within — **50% of staff cut**, **90% of civil rights complaints dismissed**, special education programs defunded, and the **$1.7 trillion student loan portfolio** transferred to Treasury. H.R. 899 would terminate the entire department by December 31, 2026. **7.5 million students with disabilities**, **1.4 million LGBTQ+ students**, and **2.8 million students** in Title I schools are losing federal protections. You can fight back: contact Congress, file OCR complaints, engage your school board, and support advocacy organizations.

### File an OCR Complaint

If you or your child has experienced discrimination in a school or educational program, you can file a complaint with the Office for Civil Rights — even in its current diminished state, filing creates a record and contributes to legal challenges.

**How to file:**
1. **Online:** [ocrcas.ed.gov](https://ocrcas.ed.gov/)
2. **Email:** ocr@ed.gov
3. **Deadline:** Within 180 days of the discriminatory act
4. Anyone can file on behalf of another person or group

**OCR covers:**
- Race, color, or national origin discrimination (Title VI)
- Sex discrimination, including sexual harassment (Title IX)
- Disability discrimination (Section 504, ADA)
- Age discrimination (Age Discrimination Act)

[source: ED.gov — How to File a Complaint](https://www.ed.gov/laws-and-policy/civil-rights-laws/file-complaint/how-file-discrimination-complaint-ocr)

## Sustained Actions

### Engage Your School Board

Your local school board is the front line of defense for public education:

- **Attend school board meetings** and ask how federal funding cuts affect your district
- **Ask the superintendent** for a district-level impact analysis of ED cuts
- **Advocate for contingency plans** if Title I or IDEA funding is reduced
- **Monitor** whether your state is opting into the Federal Tax Credit Scholarship Program and what accountability measures are in place
- **Support special education parents** who are navigating reduced federal oversight

### Support Advocacy Organizations

- **[National Education Association (NEA)](https://www.nea.org/advocating-for-change/action-center)** — Largest educators' union; advocacy campaigns to protect public education
- **[American Federation of Teachers (AFT)](https://www.aft.org/action)** — Educator union with active litigation against ED cuts
- **[ProtectPublicEd.org](https://www.protectpubliced.org/take-action)** — Coalition organizing against Department elimination
- **[National PTA](https://www.pta.org/home/advocacy)** — Parent advocacy for education funding and policy
- **[The Arc](https://thearc.org/policy-advocacy/)** — Advocacy for people with intellectual and developmental disabilities
- **[Disability Rights Education & Defense Fund (DREDF)](https://dredf.org/)** — Legal advocacy for disability rights in education
- **[National Disability Rights Network](https://www.ndrn.org/)** — Protection and advocacy for people with disabilities
- **[ACLU](https://www.aclu.org/issues/student-rights)** — Litigation defending student civil rights

### Protect Student Loan Borrowers

- **File FAFSA early** — the transition to Treasury may cause delays for 2026-27 aid disbursement
- **Monitor [studentaid.gov](https://studentaid.gov)** for updates on loan servicer changes
- **Contact your loan servicer** if you experience disruptions during the transfer
- **Know your repayment options** — OBBBA changes take effect July 1, 2026
- **Support borrower advocacy organizations** like the [Student Borrower Protection Center](https://protectborrowers.org/)

### Support Students with Disabilities

- **Learn your rights under IDEA** — [Understood.org IDEA Guide](https://www.understood.org/en/articles/individuals-with-disabilities-education-act-idea-what-you-need-to-know)
- **Connect with your state's Parent Training and Information Center** — [Center for Parent Information and Resources](https://www.parentcenterhub.org/)
- **Document any violations** of your child's IEP or 504 plan
- **File OCR complaints** when schools fail to provide required services
