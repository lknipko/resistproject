Career prosecutors are being purged. Judges are under attack. The rule of law depends on citizens demanding accountability from Congress and supporting the institutions fighting back.

### Support Capitol Police Officers

The officers who defended the Capitol on January 6th -- and whose attackers have been pardoned -- deserve direct support.

**Capitol Police Memorial Fund:**
- [U.S. Capitol Police Memorial Fund](https://www.uscp.gov/) -- Supports families of fallen officers and officers injured in the line of duty

**Officer Advocacy:**
- Amplify the stories and testimony of officers like **Harry Dunn**, **Michael Fanone**, **Aquilino Gonell**, and **Daniel Hodges** who testified publicly about their experiences
- Support legislation providing enhanced benefits for officers injured on January 6th
- Attend events where officers speak publicly about the attack and its aftermath

**The Officers' Own Words:**

Officer **Michael Fanone** (MPD, retired): Suffered a heart attack and traumatic brain injury defending the Capitol. Tasered and beaten by rioters. Has been vocal about the pardons.

Officer **Harry Dunn** (USCP): Testified before the January 6th Committee about being called racial slurs during the attack. Ran for Congress in 2024. Continues to advocate for accountability.

Officer **Aquilino Gonell** (USCP): Suffered injuries requiring multiple surgeries. Has spoken publicly about the impact of the pardons on officer morale.

Officer **Daniel Hodges** (MPD): Pinned in a doorway and crushed by rioters in a video that became iconic footage of the attack. His assailant (Patrick McCaughey III, sentenced to 7.5 years) was pardoned.

### Attend Judicial Nomination Hearings

Federal judicial nominations are confirmed by the Senate Judiciary Committee. Attending hearings sends a message that citizens are watching.

**How to attend:**
- Senate Judiciary Committee hearings are held in the Dirksen Senate Office Building, Room 226
- Check [judiciary.senate.gov](https://www.judiciary.senate.gov/) for hearing schedules
- Hearings are also livestreamed on the committee website
- Members of the public can attend in person (first-come, first-served)

**What to watch for:**
- Whether nominees are asked about DOJ independence
- Whether nominees commit to following the law over political direction
- Whether nominees acknowledge the legitimacy of the January 6th investigation
- Whether nominees have prior connections to Trump legal matters

### Support State-Level Accountability

While federal DOJ is compromised, state attorneys general and local district attorneys retain independent authority.

**Support state-level prosecutors:**
- **Fani Willis** (Fulton County DA, Georgia) -- Brought RICO charges against Trump for election interference; now under DOJ investigation for prosecuting the president
- **Alvin Bragg** (Manhattan DA, New York) -- Secured Trump conviction on 34 felony counts; now under political attack
- State attorneys general bringing cases on immigration, environmental, and civil rights issues

**Contact your state attorney general:**
- [National Association of Attorneys General](https://www.naag.org/) -- Find your state AG
- Urge state AGs to maintain independent investigations and resist federal pressure

### Monitor and Report DOJ Actions

**Track pardons and DOJ actions:**
- [DOJ Capitol Breach Cases](https://www.justice.gov/usao-dc/capitol-breach-cases) -- Official case database
- [DOJ Office of the Pardon Attorney](https://www.justice.gov/pardon) -- Pardon records
- [CREW Pardon Tracker](https://www.citizensforethics.org/) -- Tracks all presidential pardons and their implications
- [PACER](https://pacer.uscourts.gov/) -- Federal court records (small fees apply)

**Report concerning DOJ actions:**
- Contact the DOJ Inspector General: [oig.justice.gov](https://oig.justice.gov/)
- Contact the Senate Judiciary Committee: [judiciary.senate.gov](https://www.judiciary.senate.gov/)
- Contact the House Judiciary Committee: [judiciary.house.gov](https://judiciary.house.gov/)
