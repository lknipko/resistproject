# Economic Resistance

## Quick Summary

The Tesla boycott drove a 30% stock decline and 71% drop in profits -- proof that coordinated economic pressure works. Consumer spending is two-thirds of U.S. GDP. Below you'll find boycott targets, credit union alternatives to the big four banks, economic blackout dates, and shareholder proxy tools.

## Quick Actions

<StartHere>

### Join the Tesla Boycott

Tesla stock is down 30%, profits fell 71% year-over-year, and European sales dropped 40%. The Tesla Takedown is the most measurable economic pressure campaign targeting Musk's political influence through DOGE.

**What you can do:**
- **If you own a Tesla:** Consider selling it, or at minimum remove Tesla branding and badges
- **If you're shopping for an EV:** Choose an alternative — Hyundai, Kia, Ford, Chevy, Rivian, and others offer competitive models
- **If you hold Tesla stock:** Divest it from your portfolio and retirement accounts
- **Join organized actions:** Attend protests at Tesla dealerships and showrooms

**Links:**
- [boycotttesla.org](https://boycotttesla.org/) — Central hub for the boycott campaign
- [Tesla Takedown on Action Network](https://actionnetwork.org/event_campaigns/teslatakedown) — Find local events and dealership protests

</StartHere>

### Move Your Money to a Credit Union

JPMorgan Chase, Citi, Wells Fargo, and Bank of America are the top financiers of fossil fuels and maintain significant lobbying operations. Moving your deposits to a credit union or community bank reduces their capital base and political leverage.

**What you can do:**
- Open a credit union account and transfer your checking, savings, and direct deposit
- Move your credit card to a credit union-issued card
- If you have a mortgage with a big bank, refinance with a local lender when rates allow

**Links:**
- [MyCreditUnion.gov](https://mycreditunion.gov) — Find a credit union near you
- [Banking On A Better Future](https://bankingonabetterfuture.org/how-it-works-1) — Take the pledge to move your money
- [Mazaska Talks](https://mazaskatalks.org/divest-your-community) — Community divestment toolkit for organizations and local governments

### Participate in Economic Blackouts

The 50501 movement organizes coordinated days of reduced spending to create measurable dips in consumer activity. These spending freezes generate economic data points that are tracked by markets and media.

**What you can do:**
- Join the 50501 movement's coordinated no-spend days
- Shop local and independent instead of national chains
- Support the "We Ain't Buying It" targeted boycotts of Amazon, Target, and Home Depot

**Links:**
- [FiftyFifty.One](https://www.fiftyfifty.one/) — Coordinated economic blackout campaigns
- Find local independent businesses through your city's small business directory

### Check Your 401k Proxy Votes

Log into your retirement account and check for pending proxy votes. Vote YES on ESG resolutions, board accountability measures, and climate disclosure proposals. The 2026 proxy season (April-June) is when your shareholder power matters most. Most 401(k) participants never vote their proxies, giving corporate management unchecked power. Ask your plan administrator whether proxy voting pass-through is available.

### Vote Your Shareholder Proxies

If you own stocks — even through index funds, ETFs, or a 401(k) — you have voting rights on corporate resolutions. The 2026 proxy season (spring/summer) includes ESG resolutions on climate disclosure, political spending transparency, and executive compensation.

**What you can do:**
- Check your brokerage account for proxy materials (usually emailed April-June)
- Vote on every resolution — most shareholders don't, which gives management outsized control
- Ask your 401(k) administrator whether the plan allows proxy voting pass-through

**Links:**
- [Clean Yield proxy voting guide](https://cleanyield.com/2026/02/proxy-voting-a-critical-tool-for-individuals-and-investors-to-influence-corporate-behavior/) — Issue-by-issue voting recommendations
- [As You Sow proxy voting guidelines](https://www.asyousow.org/) — Track corporate accountability resolutions

## Sustained Actions

### [+] Push for Pension Fund Divestment

Public pension funds hold trillions in assets. Pressuring your city, county, or state pension fund to divest from fossil fuels, defense contractors, or companies enabling administration policies creates large-scale capital shifts.

**Precedents:**
- New York City divested $3 billion from fossil fuels
- Maine mandated state pension divestment by January 2026
- University of California divested $13.4 billion from fossil fuels

**What you can do:**
- Contact your city council member about introducing a divestment resolution
- Attend public pension board meetings and submit public comment
- Work with local advocacy groups to build coalitions for divestment votes

**Links:**
- [Fossil Free California](https://fossilfreeca.org/municipal-resolutions/) — Model resolutions for municipal divestment
- [Climate Safe Pensions](https://climatesafepensions.org/what-we-do/our-impact/) — Track divestment impact and find resources

### [+] Support Worker-Owned Alternatives

Building economic infrastructure outside corporate control strengthens community resilience. Cooperatives, worker-owned businesses, and community land trusts keep money circulating locally and provide alternatives to corporate supply chains.

**What you can do:**
- Find and shop at local cooperatives — food co-ops, credit unions, and worker-owned businesses
- Support community land trusts working on affordable housing in your area
- If you run a business, explore conversion to worker ownership

**Links:**
- [Cooperative Economics Alliance](https://www.ceanyc.org/) — Find cooperatives and worker-owned businesses
- [Democracy at Work Institute](https://institute.coop/) — Resources for worker cooperative development
- [U.S. Federation of Worker Cooperatives](https://www.usworker.coop/) — National directory of worker co-ops
