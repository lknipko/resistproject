## Quick Actions

### Call Your Representative About Corruption

**Why it works:** Congressional staff tally calls by issue. High call volume on corruption signals constituent priority and builds pressure for oversight hearings. Your call takes 2 minutes.

**Script -- General Corruption/Conflicts:**

<CallScript>
"Hello, my name is [NAME] and I'm a constituent from [ZIP CODE]. I'm calling to urge [Senator/Representative NAME] to demand oversight hearings on the president's financial conflicts of interest. The president launched a cryptocurrency that earned his affiliated entities billions while simultaneously signing executive orders benefiting the crypto industry. Foreign governments continue spending millions at Trump properties. I urge [NAME] to support legislation requiring presidential divestiture and to demand subpoena power for investigating these conflicts. Thank you."
</CallScript>

**Script -- Crypto Conflicts Specifically:**

<CallScript>
"Hello, my name is [NAME] from [ZIP CODE]. I'm calling about the president's cryptocurrency conflicts of interest. Three days before inauguration, Trump launched a memecoin where his entities control 80% of the supply. He then signed pro-crypto executive orders and hosted a dinner for top token holders at his golf club. This is selling presidential access. I urge [NAME] to support the bipartisan ETHICS Act to ban elected officials from launching financial products. Thank you."
</CallScript>

**Script -- Saudi Arabia/Foreign Payments:**

<CallScript>
"Hello, my name is [NAME] from [ZIP CODE]. I'm calling about foreign government payments to the Trump family. Jared Kushner received $2 billion from Saudi Arabia's sovereign wealth fund after serving as a senior White House advisor. Saudi-backed LIV Golf holds tournaments at Trump properties. I urge [NAME] to support the Foreign Emoluments Accountability Act and demand financial disclosure from all senior officials and their family members. Thank you."
</CallScript>

**Who to call:**
- Find your Representative: [House.gov](https://www.house.gov/representatives/find-your-representative)
- Find your Senators: [Senate.gov](https://www.senate.gov/senators/senators-contact.htm)
- **House Switchboard:** 202-225-3121
- **Senate Switchboard:** 202-224-3121

**Key Congressional Committees to contact:**
- Senate Committee on Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs (oversight)
- House Committee on Oversight and Accountability
- Senate Banking Committee (crypto regulation)
- Senate Finance Committee (tax/financial disclosure)

### File Ethics Complaints

You can file formal ethics complaints about specific conflicts of interest. Here is how:

**Office of Government Ethics (OGE)**

- Website: [oge.gov](https://www.oge.gov/)
- OGE oversees the executive branch ethics program
- Can receive complaints about financial disclosure violations
- Contact: 202-482-9300

**Office of Congressional Ethics (OCE)**

- Website: [oce.house.gov](https://oce.house.gov/)
- Reviews allegations of misconduct by House members and staff
- File complaints online or by mail
- Particularly relevant for members who block oversight of corruption

**State Ethics Commissions**

- If you live in New York, Florida (Mar-a-Lago), New Jersey (Bedminster), or Virginia (Trump Winery), your state ethics commission may have jurisdiction over Trump Organization activities in your state
- Find your state ethics commission: [National Conference of State Legislatures - Ethics](https://www.ncsl.org/ethics)

**SEC Whistleblower Program**

- If you have information about securities violations related to $TRUMP, $MELANIA, WLFI, or DJT stock
- Awards 10-30% of sanctions exceeding $1 million
- File a tip: [sec.gov/whistleblower](https://www.sec.gov/whistleblower)
- Can file anonymously through an attorney

### Support Campaign Finance Reform

Presidential corruption is enabled by weak ethics laws. Support organizations pushing for structural reform:

**Key Legislation to Support:**

- **ETHICS Act** -- Would ban elected officials and their families from launching or promoting financial products (directly targets memecoin conflicts)
- **Presidential Tax Transparency Act** -- Would require presidential candidates to release tax returns
- **Foreign Emoluments Accountability Act** -- Would create enforcement mechanisms for the Emoluments Clause
- **STOCK Act Strengthening** -- Would close loopholes in congressional trading disclosure

**How to support:**
1. Call your representatives and ask them to co-sponsor these bills
2. Support organizations lobbying for passage ([Common Cause](https://www.commoncause.org/), [Public Citizen](https://www.citizen.org/))
3. Write letters to the editor of your local newspaper about the need for ethics reform

### Monitor and Document

**Track Trump Organization Activity**

You can independently monitor financial conflicts using public data:

- **[SEC EDGAR](https://www.sec.gov/edgar/searchedgar/companysearch)** -- Search for Trump Media (DJT) filings, insider transactions, quarterly reports
- **[ProPublica - Paying the President](https://projects.propublica.org/paying-the-president/)** -- Database of spending at Trump properties
- **[FARA.gov](https://www.fara.gov/)** -- Foreign Agent Registration Act filings showing foreign lobbying
- **[OpenSecrets](https://www.opensecrets.org/)** -- Campaign finance and lobbying data
- **Blockchain explorers** -- [Solscan](https://solscan.io/) and [Etherscan](https://etherscan.io/) for tracking $TRUMP and WLFI token movements
- **[Federal Register](https://www.federalregister.gov/)** -- Executive orders and regulatory changes benefiting Trump's business interests

**Submit FOIA Requests**

You can file Freedom of Information Act requests for government records about:
- Secret Service spending at Trump properties
- Government contracts with Trump Organization entities
- Communications between federal agencies and Trump businesses
- Visitor logs at Trump properties during government business

**How to file a FOIA request:**
1. Identify the federal agency that holds the records you want
2. Write a letter describing the records with reasonable specificity
3. Submit via the agency's FOIA portal (most accept online submissions)
4. Free for most requests; fees may apply for large document productions
- Guide: [FOIA.gov](https://www.foia.gov/)
- FOIA template generators: [MuckRock](https://www.muckrock.com/) (free tool that helps draft and track FOIA requests)
