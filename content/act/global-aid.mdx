# Support Global Humanitarian Aid

## Quick Summary

The dismantling of USAID and the freeze on foreign aid has put millions of lives at immediate risk. Over 20 million people face interruption of lifesaving HIV treatment, tens of millions face food insecurity, and maternal health programs serving millions of women have been halted. You can take action right now: call Congress to demand restoration of appropriated funds, donate directly to organizations stepping in where USAID left off, and support legal challenges to the aid freeze. Every action matters -- these are real people whose lives depend on whether the world responds.

## Quick Actions

### Call Your Representatives

Congressional offices tally every call by issue. High call volume on foreign aid signals constituent priority and is harder to ignore than emails. Members of the Appropriations and Foreign Relations/Affairs committees have the most direct power to act.

Withholding these funds violates the Impoundment Control Act. I urge {repName} to use every tool available -- including hearings, legislation, and the power of the purse -- to restore these lifesaving programs. Thank you.`} />

### Support Legal Challenges

Organizations challenging the USAID dismantling and aid freeze in court:

**Federal Employee Legal Defense:**
- [American Federation of Government Employees (AFGE)](https://www.afge.org/) -- Leading the fight to reinstate fired USAID employees
- [American Foreign Service Association (AFSA)](https://www.afsa.org/) -- Representing Foreign Service officers affected by the dismantling
- [Government Accountability Project](https://whistleblower.org/) -- Supporting whistleblowers within USAID who exposed DOGE overreach

**Constitutional and Legal Challenges:**
- [Democracy Forward](https://democracyforward.org/) -- Filing legal challenges to the aid freeze as an illegal impoundment
- [ACLU](https://www.aclu.org/) -- Constitutional challenges to executive overreach
- [Public Citizen](https://www.citizen.org/) -- Challenging violations of the Administrative Procedure Act

**How You Can Help:**
- Donate to legal defense funds
- Sign up for action alerts from these organizations
- If you are a lawyer, volunteer for pro bono representation
- If you are a former USAID employee, contact AFGE or AFSA about joining litigation

### Advocate for PEPFAR Restoration

PEPFAR was created by a Republican president (George W. Bush) and has had bipartisan support for over 20 years. Use this history to make the case to representatives across the political spectrum.

**Key Talking Points:**

1. **PEPFAR saved 25 million lives** -- it is the most successful global health initiative in history
2. **Bipartisan legacy** -- Created by Bush, expanded by Obama, maintained by Trump (first term), continued by Biden
3. **National security** -- Stable, healthy populations are less likely to generate conflict, terrorism, or mass migration
4. **Cost-effective** -- PEPFAR costs ~$7 billion/year; the U.S. defense budget is ~$886 billion/year. Development aid prevents far more expensive military interventions
5. **Constitutional issue** -- Congress appropriated these funds. Withholding them may violate the Impoundment Control Act
6. **American jobs** -- Thousands of American employees and contractors depend on USAID programs

**Organize a Constituent Meeting:**
- Request a meeting with your representative's staff (district office)
- Bring 3-5 constituents who care about global health
- Share personal stories about why PEPFAR matters
- Bring printed materials (fact sheets from [ONE Campaign](https://www.one.org/) or [KFF](https://www.kff.org/))
- Follow up with a thank-you note and continue relationship
