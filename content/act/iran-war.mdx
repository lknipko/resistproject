# Iran War Response

## Quick Summary

The US-Iran war that began February 28, 2026, is now in its 4th week. **Over 1,750 people have been killed in Iran**, the Strait of Hormuz has nearly shut down (3,000+ vessels stranded), and 47% of Americans oppose the war. The US is now deploying **82nd Airborne paratroopers and additional Marines**, with a **ground invasion reportedly under consideration**. Iran rejected Trump's peace proposal and he stated "I don't care about reaching a deal." Congress has twice narrowly rejected War Powers resolutions. The 60-day War Powers clock is running — **Congress must act by approximately April 29, 2026** or legally allow the war to continue.

<Callout variant="warning" title="The 60-Day War Powers Clock">

The War Powers Act of 1973 requires the president to seek congressional authorization within 60 days of committing forces to hostilities, or begin withdrawing them. The February 28 strikes started that clock.

**The deadline is approximately April 29, 2026.**

Congress has the power to demand withdrawal, block war funding, or formally authorize the conflict. Two votes have already failed — narrowly. Constituent pressure on swing-vote members matters now.

## Quick Actions

### Contact Congress: War Powers

Congress voted on the Massie-Khanna War Powers Resolution on March 4–5 and it failed 212–219. The margin was 7 votes. Four Democrats voted against it; two Republicans voted for it. Another vote is possible — and constituent pressure on those swing members is the most direct action available.

**What to say:**

- The Constitution gives Congress alone the power to declare war (Article I, Section 8)
- The president launched a full-scale war without a declaration or AUMF
- The War Powers Act requires authorization within 60 days — the deadline is April 29
- Vote YES on any new War Powers resolution; vote NO on any supplemental war funding

### Contact Congress: War Funding

Even if another War Powers vote fails, Congress has the power of the purse. The ACLU is pressing Congress to block any supplemental appropriations for the war.

**What to say:**

- Congress must not write a blank check for an unauthorized war
- Oppose any supplemental Pentagon funding for Iran operations without a concurrent vote to authorize the conflict
- Demand a public debate and vote before any new funding is approved

### Legal Support

**ACLU constitutional challenge:**
The ACLU is the primary organization pursuing the constitutional argument against the unauthorized war. Donate to support their work:
- [ACLU.org — Donate](https://www.aclu.org/donate)
- Their core argument: the Constitution requires specific congressional authorization; no existing AUMF covers the Iran conflict

**The 60-day War Powers deadline:**
The War Powers Act creates a legal mechanism — if the president does not seek and receive congressional authorization by approximately April 29, 2026, he is legally required to begin withdrawing forces. Whether this administration complies is uncertain. Citizen pressure on Congress to enforce the Act — through funding restrictions and public accountability — is the most actionable path.

**Monitor litigation:**
- Follow Just Security ([justsecurity.org](https://www.justsecurity.org)) for analysis of legal challenges and international law questions
- Follow the Brennan Center for Justice ([brennancenter.org](https://www.brennancenter.org)) for war powers constitutional analysis

### [+] Talking Points for Calls and Emails

Use these when contacting your representatives:

- **"The Constitution gives Congress the power to declare war — not the president."** (Article I, Section 8)
- **"The War Powers Act requires congressional authorization within 60 days — the deadline is April 29, 2026."**
- **"The House War Powers vote failed by just 7 votes on March 5. Your vote matters."**
- **"At least 7 US service members have already been killed. Congress owes them — and the American people — a real debate."**
- **"An unauthorized war sets a precedent that any future president can use. This is not a partisan issue."**
- **"The ACLU, legal scholars, and international law experts agree: this war lacks legal authorization."**
- **"If Congress won't vote to authorize the war, it should at minimum refuse to fund it."**

### [+] For Republican Constituents

These arguments may be effective when contacting Republican representatives:

- **Reagan invoked the War Powers Act himself** — he notified Congress about the Lebanon deployment in 1982. Respecting the Act is not weakness, it is constitutional order.
- **No existing AUMF covers Iran.** The 2001 and 2002 AUMFs targeted al-Qaeda and Iraq. Congress should vote explicitly on Iran — that is its constitutional role.
- **The 60-day clock is the law.** Congress passed the War Powers Act with bipartisan support specifically to prevent a president from committing the nation to war alone. That principle applies regardless of which party holds the White House.
- **An unauthorized war precedent is dangerous.** A future Democratic president could cite this precedent to launch military operations without congressional approval.
