# Join Litigation

## Quick Summary

After Trump v. CASA ended universal injunctions, your participation in lawsuits became essential. If a federal policy harms you, joining a lawsuit may be the only way to receive legal protection. The government can continue enforcing policies against everyone who isn't a party to a case.

## Quick Actions

### Understand Why This Matters

**Before *Trump v. CASA* (June 2025):**
- One lawsuit could block a policy nationwide
- Courts issued "universal injunctions" protecting everyone

**After *Trump v. CASA*:**
- Courts can only protect people who are parties to the lawsuit
- The government can continue enforcing policies against everyone else
- If you're not in a lawsuit, you may not be protected

**This means:** If a federal policy harms you, joining a lawsuit may be the only way to receive legal protection.

## How Class Actions Work

### What is a Class Action?

A class action allows many people with similar claims to sue together. One or more "named plaintiffs" represent the entire "class" of affected people.

**Benefits:**
- Efficiency - one case instead of thousands
- Resources - legal organizations fund the litigation
- Strength in numbers - courts take large classes seriously

### How to Join a Class Action

1. **Determine if you qualify** - Check the class definition
2. **Contact the legal team** - Organizations recruiting plaintiffs list contact info
3. **Provide documentation** - Evidence of how you were harmed
4. **Sign retainer/participation agreement** - Authorizing lawyers to represent you

### What Class Members Do

**As a named plaintiff:**
- Provide testimony/declarations
- May be deposed (questioned by opposing lawyers)
- Your name may become public
- May need to appear in court

**As an unnamed class member:**
- Benefit from any court order
- Usually no active participation required
- May receive notice of settlement

## Sustained Actions

### Become a Named Plaintiff

Named plaintiffs are essential to post-*CASA* litigation. If you've been harmed:

**What to expect:**
- Legal team will interview you about your experience
- You'll provide documents and declarations
- You may be deposed (questioned under oath)
- Your name may appear in court filings
- The process can take months or years

**Protections:**
- Attorneys can request protective orders for sensitive information
- Some cases allow pseudonymous plaintiffs (e.g., "Jane Doe")
- Retaliation for participating in litigation is illegal

### Help Recruit Plaintiffs

Legal teams need affected people in every federal judicial district.

**How to help:**
- Share information about active cases in your community
- Connect affected individuals with legal organizations
- Translate materials for non-English speakers
- Accompany people to legal intake appointments

### Support State Attorney General Lawsuits

71 lawsuits across 22 states are challenging federal policies. Maryland's Federal Accountability Unit alone recovered $100 million in education funding. State AGs may have broader standing than individual plaintiffs after *Trump v. CASA*.

**How to help:**
- Contact your state AG to express support for existing litigation: [State AG Directory](https://www.naag.org/attorneys-general/the-attorneys-general/)
- Track all active lawsuits: [Just Security Litigation Tracker](https://www.justsecurity.org/107087/tracker-litigation-legal-challenges-trump-administration/)
- Donate to your state AG's legal defense fund where available — search "[Your State] Attorney General legal defense fund"

### Support Litigation Financially

Public interest law organizations accept donations:

- [ACLU Foundation](https://www.aclu.org/)
- [NAACP Legal Defense Fund](https://www.naacpldf.org/)

## Active Litigation by Issue

### Birthright Citizenship

**Lead Case:** *Trump v. Barbara* (Supreme Court Docket 25-365)

- **Status:** Before Supreme Court
- **Class:** Parents of U.S.-born children denied citizenship recognition
- **Legal teams:** Multiple organizations coordinating

### OBBBA / Medicaid

**Lead Case:** *Planned Parenthood v. Kennedy*

- **Status:** District court
- **Challenging:** Section 71113 "Prohibited Entities" provision
- **Legal teams:** Reproductive rights organizations

### Immigration Enforcement

**Multiple cases in various districts**

- **Alien Enemies Act:** Federal judge ordered return of Venezuelan migrants deported to CECOT (February 12, 2026)
- **Abrego Garcia:** Supreme Court unanimously ruled his deportation unlawful; contempt proceedings possible against Trump administration
- **Contact:** [ACLU Immigrants' Rights Project](https://www.aclu.org/issues/immigrants-rights)
- **Contact:** [National Immigration Law Center](https://www.nilc.org/)

### Schedule F / Civil Service

**Expected Case:** AFGE challenge to OPM final rule

- **Status:** AFGE expected to file lawsuit challenging the Schedule F final rule (took effect March 8-9, 2026)
- **Class:** Federal employees reclassified under Schedule Policy/Career
- **Legal teams:** [AFGE](https://www.afge.org/), federal employee unions
- **Also:** NTEU v. Trump — ongoing challenge to the original EO 14171

### EPA Endangerment Finding

**Active Case:** Coalition challenge to Endangerment Finding repeal

- **Status:** Coalition of health and environmental groups sued EPA (filed March 2026, D.C. Circuit)
- **Challenging:** EPA's rescission of the 2009 Endangerment Finding, eliminating legal basis for all federal GHG regulation
- **Legal teams:** [Earthjustice](https://earthjustice.org/), environmental health organizations
- **24 states + 10 cities** filed a second, larger lawsuit on March 20, 2026, in the D.C. Circuit — led by Massachusetts, California, New York, and Connecticut. Santa Clara County and additional jurisdictions joined the following week. This is one of the largest state-led environmental lawsuits in history [source: MPR News](https://www.mprnews.org/story/2026/03/20/states-cities-sue-epa-over-repeal-of-endangerment-finding-central-to-climate-fight)

## Your State Attorney General

### Why AGs Matter

After *Trump v. CASA*, State Attorneys General may have broader standing to seek relief for state residents than individual plaintiffs.

**Check if your AG has filed suit:**
- [State AG Directory](https://www.naag.org/attorneys-general/the-attorneys-general/)
- Search "[Your State] Attorney General lawsuit [issue]"

### Pressure Your AG

If your AG hasn't joined litigation:

> "I urge Attorney General [NAME] to join litigation challenging [POLICY]. After *Trump v. CASA*, state AGs are among the few actors who can seek broad relief for state residents. [STATE] residents deserve the protection that comes from our state joining these cases."
