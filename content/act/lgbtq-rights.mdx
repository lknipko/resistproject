## Know Your Rights

### Protections That Still Exist

Despite the executive orders, some legal protections remain:

**Federal:**
- ***Bostock v. Clayton County* (2020)** - Title VII still prohibits employment discrimination based on sexual orientation and gender identity. If you are fired, demoted, or harassed at work because you are LGBTQ+, you can file a complaint with the EEOC. [EEOC Filing](https://www.eeoc.gov/filing-charge-discrimination)
- **Marriage equality** - *Obergefell v. Hodges* (2015) remains the law. Same-sex marriages are legally recognized nationwide. The Respect for Marriage Act (2022) provides additional statutory protections.
- **Fair Housing Act** - HUD previously interpreted sex discrimination to include sexual orientation and gender identity. While enforcement has weakened, the statute itself still applies per *Bostock* reasoning.

**State-Level:**
- **24 states + DC** have laws explicitly prohibiting discrimination based on sexual orientation and gender identity in employment, housing, and public accommodations
- **Sanctuary states** (CA, CO, CT, IL, MD, MA, MN, NJ, NM, NY, OR, VT, WA) have shield laws protecting gender-affirming care

**If You Experience Discrimination:**
1. **Document everything** - Save emails, texts, records of incidents with dates and witnesses
2. **File a complaint** - [EEOC](https://www.eeoc.gov/) (employment), [HUD](https://www.hud.gov/program_offices/fair_housing_equal_opp/online-complaint) (housing), your state civil rights agency
3. **Contact legal help** - [Lambda Legal Help Desk](https://www.lambdalegal.org/helpdesk): 1-866-542-8336

## Sustained Actions

### Join LGBTQ+ Legal Defense Organizations

These organizations are leading the legal fight against anti-LGBTQ+ policies and need sustained support:

**Lambda Legal** - [lambdalegal.org](https://www.lambdalegal.org/)
- Lead litigator on LGBTQ+ civil rights cases since 1973
- Currently challenging EO 14168 and state healthcare bans
- **Help Desk:** 1-866-542-8336 (if you've experienced discrimination)
- **Volunteer:** Legal observer program, community education

**ACLU LGBTQ & HIV Rights** - [aclu.org/issues/lgbtq-rights](https://www.aclu.org/issues/lgbtq-rights)
- Coordinates multi-state litigation strategy
- Active in every circuit challenging anti-trans laws
- **Your state affiliate** needs volunteers: [Find yours](https://www.aclu.org/affiliates)

**National Center for Lesbian Rights (NCLR)** - [nclrights.org](https://www.nclrights.org/)
- Specializes in cases involving LGBTQ+ families and youth
- Born Perfect campaign against conversion therapy
- **Legal helpline:** Available through website

**Transgender Law Center** - [transgenderlawcenter.org](https://transgenderlawcenter.org/)
- Largest trans-led legal organization
- Focus on trans people of color and low-income communities
- Community-based legal clinics

**GLAD (GLBTQ Legal Advocates & Defenders)** - [glad.org](https://www.glad.org/)
- Focus on New England states
- Legal InfoLine: 1-800-455-GLAD
- Brought the original marriage equality cases

### Support Transgender People Directly

**Practical ways to help trans people in your community:**

1. **Mutual aid funds** - Contribute to local trans mutual aid networks
   - [Trans Lifeline Microgrants](https://translifeline.org/microgrants/) - Emergency financial help
   - [Point of Pride](https://www.pointofpride.org/) - Binders, hormones, surgery fund
   - [Jim Collins Foundation](https://jimcollinsfoundation.org/) - Surgery grants
   - [For the Gworls](https://www.forthegworls.party/) - Black trans housing and healthcare

2. **Healthcare navigation** - Help connect trans people with affirming providers
   - [GLMA Provider Directory](https://www.glma.org/find-a-provider) - LGBTQ+ affirming healthcare providers
   - [Trans in the South](https://southernequality.org/transinthesouth/) - Southern states healthcare guide
   - [Planned Parenthood](https://www.plannedparenthood.org/learn/gender-identity/transgender/what-do-i-need-know-about-trans-health-care) - Gender-affirming care at many locations
   - [FOLX Health](https://www.folxhealth.com/) - Telehealth hormone therapy
   - [Plume](https://www.getplume.co/) - Telehealth gender-affirming care

3. **Housing support** - Trans people face disproportionate housing instability
   - [Ali Forney Center](https://www.aliforneycenter.org/) - NYC LGBTQ+ youth housing
   - [True Colors United](https://truecolorsunited.org/) - LGBTQ+ youth homelessness
   - [Local LGBTQ+ centers](https://www.lgbtcenters.org/) - Find your nearest center

4. **Be a visible ally** - Especially in workplaces, schools, and public spaces
   - Use correct names and pronouns
   - Speak up when you witness discrimination
   - Display visible support (safe space stickers, pronoun badges)
   - Educate yourself: [PFLAG resources](https://pflag.org/resource/)

### School and Education Advocacy

**Protect LGBTQ+ students in your school district:**

- **Start or support a GSA (Gender-Sexuality Alliance):**
  - [GSA Network](https://gsanetwork.org/) - Resources for starting and running GSAs
  - [GLSEN](https://www.glsen.org/) - Safe schools toolkit and training

- **Attend school board meetings:**
  - Speak in favor of inclusive anti-bullying policies
  - Oppose book bans targeting LGBTQ+ content
  - Support transgender student inclusion
  - See [School Board Advocacy](/act/school-boards) for detailed guidance

- **Support LGBTQ+ youth directly:**
  - Volunteer with the [Trevor Project](https://www.thetrevorproject.org/volunteer/) as a crisis counselor (40-hour training)
  - Become a PFLAG chapter leader
  - Mentor LGBTQ+ youth through local organizations

### State and Local Advocacy

**Your state legislature is where many anti-LGBTQ+ bills are introduced:**

1. **Track your state's legislation:**
   - [ACLU Anti-Trans Legislation Tracker](https://www.aclu.org/legislative-attacks-on-lgbtq-rights) - Updated weekly
   - [Freedom for All Americans](https://freedomforallamericans.org/) - State legislation tracker
   - [Equality Federation State Partners](https://www.equalityfederation.org/members/) - Find your state equality org

2. **Contact your state legislators:**
   - Find them: [OpenStates.org](https://openstates.org/)
   - Use the same call script format as federal calls, adapted for state bills
   - State legislators receive far fewer calls -- your call has outsized impact

3. **Pressure your Attorney General:**
   - If your AG hasn't joined federal lawsuits challenging anti-LGBTQ+ orders:
   > "I urge Attorney General [NAME] to join litigation challenging the federal executive orders that discriminate against LGBTQ+ residents of [STATE]. After *Trump v. CASA*, state AGs are among the few actors who can seek broad relief. Our LGBTQ+ residents deserve protection."
   - [Find your AG](https://www.naag.org/attorneys-general/the-attorneys-general/)

4. **Support state-level Equality Acts:**
   - Many states are considering their own versions of the Equality Act
   - Contact your state equality organization to learn how to help
