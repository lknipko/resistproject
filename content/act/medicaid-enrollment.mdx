# Medicaid Enrollment Defense

## Quick Summary

The OBBBA now requires Medicaid reverification every 6 months instead of 12 months. Combined with provider tax bans forcing state cuts, millions risk losing coverage. Update your contact information immediately and help neighbors navigate the process.

## Quick Actions

<StartHere>

### Check Your Enrollment Status (2 minutes)

**Step 1: Find Your State Medicaid Portal**
- [Healthcare.gov State Directory](https://www.healthcare.gov/medicaid-chip/medicaid-chip-program-names/)
- Most states have online portals where you can check your enrollment status

**Step 2: Update Your Contact Information**
Make sure your state has your current:
- Mailing address
- Phone number
- Email address

*Missing a reverification notice = automatic disenrollment*

</StartHere>

### Know Your Deadlines

Under the OBBBA, states now require reverification every **6 months** instead of 12 months.

**What to watch for:**
- Official mail from your state Medicaid office
- Requests for income verification documents
- Notices of upcoming coverage end dates

### Report Coverage Problems

If you've been wrongly disenrolled or can't get through to your state:
- [Healthcare.gov Help Line](https://www.healthcare.gov/contact-us/) - 1-800-318-2596
- [CMS Complaint Portal](https://www.cms.gov/Medicare/CMS-Forms/CMS-Forms/CMS-Forms-Items/CMS017339)

## Sustained Actions

### Become a Medicaid Navigator

**What Navigators Do:**
- Help neighbors complete reverification paperwork
- Translate documents for non-English speakers
- Accompany people to in-person appointments
- Track deadlines for multiple households

**Get Trained:**
- [Families USA Navigator Resources](https://familiesusa.org/)
- [Community Catalyst Training](https://communitycatalyst.org/)
- Contact your state's Health Insurance Marketplace for local trainings

### Monitor Your State's Response

States are responding differently to the OBBBA provider tax ban:

| State Response | What It Means |
|----------------|---------------|
| **Raising other taxes** | Coverage may be maintained |
| **Cutting reimbursement rates** | Providers may stop accepting Medicaid |
| **Reducing benefits** | Some services may no longer be covered |
| **Tightening eligibility** | Income thresholds may drop |

**Track your state:**
- [KFF State Medicaid Tracker](https://www.kff.org/medicaid/)
- [Georgetown CCF State Updates](https://ccf.georgetown.edu/)

### Comment on Medicaid Rule Changes

Comment on proposed Medicaid work requirement rules at [Regulations.gov](https://www.regulations.gov). Federal agencies are legally required to consider substantive public comments, and courts have struck down rules where agencies failed to respond to them. Search for active CMS dockets related to Medicaid eligibility and work requirements.

### Support Legal Challenges

Organizations challenging OBBBA provisions:
- [National Health Law Program](https://healthlaw.org/) - Litigation and advocacy
- [Community Catalyst](https://communitycatalyst.org/) - State-level organizing

## If You Lose Coverage

### Immediate Steps

1. **Don't panic** - You may be eligible for reinstatement
2. **Contact your state Medicaid office immediately** - Ask about the appeals process
3. **Request a fair hearing** - You have the right to appeal disenrollment
4. **Document everything** - Keep copies of all notices and correspondence

### Alternative Coverage Options

| Option | Who Qualifies | Where to Apply |
|--------|---------------|----------------|
| **Marketplace plans (ACA)** | Income 100-400% FPL | [Healthcare.gov](https://www.healthcare.gov/) |
| **CHIP** | Children in families above Medicaid limits | [InsureKidsNow.gov](https://www.insurekidsnow.gov/) |
| **Community Health Centers** | Anyone, sliding scale fees | [FindAHealthCenter.hrsa.gov](https://findahealthcenter.hrsa.gov/) |

### Emergency Care Rights

**Remember:** Hospitals cannot refuse emergency care regardless of insurance status (EMTALA law).
