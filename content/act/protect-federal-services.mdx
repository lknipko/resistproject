Congress has the power to stop this. Your calls, your pressure, and your support for legal challenges are the most effective tools available right now.

## Quick Actions

### Call Your Representatives NOW

**Phone calls are the single most effective form of constituent pressure.** Congressional staff tally calls and report volume to members. High call volume on an issue directly influences votes.

**Find your representatives:**
- [House.gov - Find Your Representative](https://www.house.gov/representatives/find-your-representative)
- [Senate.gov - Find Your Senators](https://www.senate.gov/senators/senators-contact.htm)
- **Fastest:** Text your zip code to [Resistbot](https://resist.bot/) at **50409**

**Congressional Switchboard:**
- **House:** 202-225-3121
- **Senate:** 202-224-3121

**Pro Tips:**
- Call in the morning (9-10 AM Eastern) for shortest wait times
- Call the DC office for policy issues
- Be brief: state your name, zip code, and specific ask
- Ask to be "logged on the issue"
- Call both senators AND your House representative -- that's 3 calls
- If you get voicemail, leave a message -- they still count those

[Your Name]

### [+] File FOIA Requests

The Freedom of Information Act (FOIA) is a powerful tool for forcing transparency. You do not need to be a journalist or lawyer to file a FOIA request.

**What to Request**

Effective FOIA requests related to DOGE:
- DOGE team rosters and security clearance status at specific agencies
- Communications between DOGE staff and agency leadership
- Records of DOGE access to Treasury payment systems
- Data extraction logs from OPM personnel databases
- Cost-benefit analyses for proposed DOGE cuts
- Communications about CFPB or USAID shutdown decisions

**How to File**

1. **Identify the agency** that holds the records you want
2. **Write a specific request** -- narrow requests get faster responses
3. **Submit through the agency's FOIA portal** -- every agency has one:
   - [FOIA.gov - Submit Requests](https://www.foia.gov/)
   - Find any agency's FOIA office at [FOIA.gov](https://www.foia.gov/)
4. **Track your request** -- agencies must respond within 20 business days
5. **Appeal denials** -- you have the right to appeal; many denials are overturned

**Template FOIA Request:**

```
Dear FOIA Officer,

Pursuant to the Freedom of Information Act, 5 U.S.C. Section 552,
I request access to and copies of:

[SPECIFIC DESCRIPTION OF RECORDS]

covering the period from January 20, 2025 to the present.

I am willing to pay fees up to $25.00. If estimated fees exceed
this amount, please contact me before proceeding.

If any portion of this request is denied, please cite the specific
exemption(s) and notify me of my right to appeal.

Thank you for your prompt attention to this request.

[Your Name]
[Your Address]
[Your Email]
```

**Organizations Filing FOIA for You**

If you do not want to file yourself, these organizations are actively filing FOIA requests about DOGE:
- [American Oversight](https://www.americanoversight.org/) -- Files hundreds of FOIA requests; publishes results
- [Democracy Forward](https://democracyforward.org/) -- FOIA litigation
- [MuckRock](https://www.muckrock.com/) -- Helps anyone file FOIA requests for free

### [+] Attend Town Halls

**Why Town Halls Matter**

In-person events are the highest-impact form of constituent pressure. Representatives remember faces and stories. Local media covers town halls, amplifying your message.

**Find Town Halls:**
- [Town Hall Project](https://townhallproject.com/) -- National database of upcoming events
- Check your representatives' websites for scheduled events
- Follow your reps on social media for event announcements

**At the Town Hall:**

1. **Arrive early** -- Get a seat near the front or microphone
2. **Prepare one clear question** -- Focus on a single DOGE issue
3. **Tell a personal story** -- How does the service cut affect you or your community?
4. **Be respectful but firm** -- Demand a specific answer, not vague reassurances
5. **Record the response** -- Share on social media to hold them accountable
6. **Follow up** -- Call their office the next week referencing the town hall

**Sample Town Hall Question:**

> "[Representative NAME], DOGE has fired over 260,000 federal employees. Social Security disability claims now take over a year. VA hospitals are understaffed. What specific actions are you taking to restore funding and staffing to these agencies? Will you commit to holding oversight hearings on DOGE?"

### [+] Track and Report DOGE Impacts

If you experience or witness the impact of DOGE cuts on federal services, document and report it:

**How to Document**

1. **Keep a log** -- Date, agency, issue, wait time, outcome
2. **Take screenshots** -- Error messages, closed-office notices, wait time screens
3. **Save correspondence** -- Letters about benefit delays, service changes
4. **Note staff comments** -- If federal workers mention staffing shortages, document it (with permission)

**Where to Report**

- **Your representative's office** -- Personal constituent stories are the most powerful advocacy tool
- **Your state attorney general** -- If your state is part of anti-DOGE litigation, they want impact evidence
- **Inspector General hotlines** -- Report waste, fraud, or abuse: [CIGIE IG list](https://www.ignet.gov/)
- **Government Accountability Project** -- If you witness government wrongdoing: [whistleblower.org](https://whistleblower.org/)
- **Local media** -- Journalists covering DOGE impacts want real stories from real people
