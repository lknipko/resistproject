# Protect Social Security & Medicare

## Quick Summary

Social Security faces **insolvency by 2032** with automatic **28% benefit cuts** for 70.6 million Americans. Medicare's Hospital Insurance fund lost **12 years of solvency** in under one year. DOGE cut SSA staffing to a **60-year low** — 47 field offices closing, disability waits at 236 days. A typical retired couple faces an **$18,400/year cut**. Reform proposals exist that would fix the shortfall. Congress needs to hear from you **now** — every year of inaction makes the fix more painful.

## Sustained Actions

### Attend Town Halls

When your member of Congress holds a town hall, attend and ask this specific question:

> "Social Security faces insolvency by 2032 with automatic 28% benefit cuts. What is your specific plan to restore solvency — and when will you vote on it?"

AARP hosts free tele-town halls on Social Security topics — check [aarp.org](https://www.aarp.org/advocacy/aarp-fight-for-social-security-2026/) for upcoming events.

### Report SSA Service Problems

If you experience long wait times, office closures, or processing delays, contact your Congressional office and request a **formal Congressional inquiry** with SSA. Congressional offices have dedicated staff for constituent casework with federal agencies. This creates a paper trail that demonstrates the impact of staffing cuts.

### Support Key Organizations

- **[AARP](https://www.aarp.org/advocacy/aarp-fight-for-social-security-2026/)** — The largest advocacy organization for Social Security and Medicare. Actively lobbying Congress and running public awareness campaigns
- **[National Committee to Preserve Social Security and Medicare](https://www.ncpssm.org/)** — Nonpartisan advocacy focused exclusively on protecting these programs
- **[Center for Medicare Advocacy](https://medicareadvocacy.org/)** — Legal assistance and policy advocacy for Medicare beneficiaries; filed amicus brief in DOGE/SSA lawsuit
- **[Social Security Works](https://socialsecurityworks.org/)** — Advocacy for expanding (not just protecting) Social Security benefits
- **[The Arc](https://thearc.org/)** — Advocates for people with disabilities, including SSDI recipients facing 236-day waits

### Monitor Rule Changes

When SSA proposes changes to benefit calculations, disability determinations, or office procedures, submit public comments through [regulations.gov](https://www.regulations.gov/). Search for "Social Security Administration" to find open comment periods.

### Know the Reform Options

SSA publishes a complete list of solvency proposals and their actuarial effects at [ssa.gov/oact/solvency](https://www.ssa.gov/oact/solvency/index.html). Familiarize yourself with these so you can have informed conversations with your representatives about specific solutions.

Key fact: **Eliminating the taxable maximum entirely** (currently $184,500) would fix **67% of the long-range shortfall**. This single change — asking high earners to pay the same rate as everyone else — would be the largest single step toward solvency.
