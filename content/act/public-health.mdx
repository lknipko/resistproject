# Protect Public Health

## Quick Summary

Public health infrastructure is being dismantled at every level: WHO withdrawal, CDC interference, NIH funding cuts, FDA weakening, and pandemic preparedness gutted. These changes affect everyone. You can fight back by contacting Congress, supporting science advocacy organizations, strengthening state-level public health, and ensuring your family stays protected.

### Protect Yourself and Your Family

While advocating for systemic change, take immediate steps to protect your health:

**Vaccination:**
- Ensure all family members are up-to-date on vaccinations
- Check [Vaccines.gov](https://www.vaccines.gov/) for locations near you
- If your state has restricted pharmacy access, use [FindAHealthCenter.hrsa.gov](https://findahealthcenter.hrsa.gov/) to find community health centers
- See our [Pharmacy Access Guide](/act/pharmacy-access) for state-specific information

**Emergency Preparedness:**
- Maintain a 30-day supply of prescription medications
- Keep a basic emergency medical kit
- Know your nearest community health center: [FindAHealthCenter.hrsa.gov](https://findahealthcenter.hrsa.gov/)
- Save your local health department contact: [NACCHO Directory](https://www.naccho.org/membership/lhd-directory)

**Stay Informed:**
- Follow trusted sources: [CDC](https://www.cdc.gov/), [WHO](https://www.who.int/), [KFF](https://www.kff.org/)
- Sign up for your state health department alerts
- Bookmark [Outbreak.info](https://outbreak.info/) for disease tracking

### Strengthen State-Level Public Health

With federal infrastructure being dismantled, states are the frontline defense. Advocate for your state to:

**1. Decouple from Federal Vaccine Guidance**
- Contact your governor and state legislators
- Use New York's Executive Order 52.5 as a model
- See [Pharmacy Access Guide](/act/pharmacy-access) for details

**2. Increase State Health Department Funding**
- Attend state budget hearings
- Advocate for increased state funding to replace lost federal grants
- Support state public health laboratory capacity

**3. Join or Create a Local Public Health Advisory Board**
- Many counties and municipalities have citizen advisory boards
- Contact your local health department: [NACCHO Directory](https://www.naccho.org/membership/lhd-directory)
- Volunteer for community health initiatives

**State Advocacy Script:**

<CallScript>
"I'm calling to urge [Governor/State Legislator] to strengthen [STATE]'s public health infrastructure in response to federal cuts. Specifically, I urge you to: [1] Decouple state pharmacy laws from federal vaccine recommendations, [2] Increase state funding for the [STATE] Department of Health, and [3] Protect state-level disease surveillance and emergency preparedness programs. [STATE] cannot rely on a federal government that is dismantling public health protections."
</CallScript>

### Fight Health Misinformation

The same administration policies that dismantle infrastructure also flood the public with misinformation about vaccines, fluoride, and medical treatments. Counter this by:

**Personal actions:**
- Share evidence-based health information with your community
- When you see misinformation on social media, report it and share corrective information
- Discuss vaccination with hesitant family members and friends using empathy, not judgment

**Community actions:**
- Volunteer with local health literacy programs
- Support your public library's health information resources
- Attend school board meetings to support evidence-based health education

**Trusted Information Sources:**
- [CDC](https://www.cdc.gov/) - Disease prevention and health data
- [WHO](https://www.who.int/) - Global health information
- [KFF](https://www.kff.org/) - Health policy analysis
- [HealthNewsReview](https://www.healthnewsreview.org/) - Evaluating health claims
- [ScienceBasedMedicine](https://sciencebasedmedicine.org/) - Evidence-based medical analysis

### Talking Points

**On WHO Withdrawal:**
- The U.S. contributed $700M/year to WHO, roughly 15-18% of its budget
- WHO coordinates disease surveillance in 194 countries
- Without WHO membership, we lose early warning of emerging pandemics
- The previous withdrawal in 2020 was reversed in 2021

**On NIH Funding Cuts:**
- NIH funds 50,000 research grants at 2,500+ institutions in all 50 states
- NIH-funded research generates $103 billion in annual economic activity
- The proposed 40% cut would be the largest in NIH history
- Cuts threaten cancer, Alzheimer's, and infectious disease research

**On CDC Interference:**
- ACIP has set vaccine recommendations since 1964 based on science, not politics
- The January 2026 vaccine reclassification bypassed ACIP entirely
- Career scientists are being fired, taking decades of expertise with them
- Measles cases surged in 2025 as vaccination guidance was weakened

**On Pandemic Preparedness:**
- COVID-19 killed 1.2 million Americans with preparedness systems in place
- ASPR, BARDA, and Global Health Security programs are being gutted
- The U.S. is now less prepared than it was before COVID
- Climate change and global travel increase pandemic risk
