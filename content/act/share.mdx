## Quick Actions

### Share via Text/Message

Copy and send one of these messages to friends, family, or group chats. Texts from someone you know are far more likely to be opened than any social media post.

**Short and direct:**

> Check out resistproject.com — 120+ ways to take action on what's happening in government right now. Every fact links to primary sources. Most actions take less than a minute.

**Issue-specific (customize the brackets):**

> Concerned about [Iran / ICE raids / healthcare cuts / DOGE]? resistproject.com has the facts straight from government documents and concrete actions you can take right now. No opinions, just sources and steps.

**After a conversation about politics:**

> You mentioned feeling like there's nothing you can do. I found this site — resistproject.com — that has 120+ specific actions, from 30-second emails to sustained campaigns. Every claim links to government documents, not news articles. Worth a look.

### Share on Social Media

Copy any of these posts. Each is tailored to the platform and stays within character limits.

**Twitter/X:**

> Feeling overwhelmed by the news? resistproject.com breaks it down: verified facts from primary sources + 120 concrete actions you can take. Most take less than a minute. Bookmark it. Share it.

**Instagram (caption for post or story):**

> Not sure what to do about everything happening right now? Same. Then I found resistproject.com. It's a free, community-built site with 120+ specific civic actions — from contacting your reps to joining active lawsuits. Every single claim links to government documents, not headlines. No opinions. Just facts, sources, and steps you can take today. Link in bio or just type resistproject.com into your browser.

**Facebook:**

> I want to share a resource that's been genuinely useful. resistproject.com is a free civic action site with 120+ things you can actually do about what's happening in government. What makes it different: every fact links directly to government documents and court filings, not news articles. There are quick actions that take 30 seconds and sustained campaigns for people who want to go deeper. If you've been looking for something concrete, this is it. resistproject.com

**Threads:**

> If you want to do something but don't know where to start: resistproject.com. 120+ civic actions. Every fact sourced to government documents. Most actions take under a minute. Free, community-built, no paywall.

**Bluesky:**

> resistproject.com — a free civic action resource with 120+ concrete things you can do. Every claim links to primary sources (government docs, court filings, executive orders). Not awareness. Action.

### Share the QR Code

If you received a flyer or saw a sign with a QR code at an event, you can share it further:

- **Screenshot it** from the sign or flyer and text it to people
- **Save it** to your camera roll and post it to your stories
- **Show your phone** to the person next to you and let them scan directly

The QR code points to resistproject.com and works from any phone camera.

### Tell Someone In Person

You don't need a script. Here are a few ways to describe the site in conversation:

- **"It's like Wikipedia but for civic action."** It covers what's happening in government, sourced entirely from government documents, and pairs every issue with concrete things you can do about it.

- **"Every claim links to government documents, not news articles."** The site uses executive orders, court filings, and federal register notices — not opinion pieces or social media.

- **"There are 120+ things you can do, and most take less than a minute."** From pre-written emails to your representatives to joining active lawsuits. Quick actions and sustained campaigns.

- **"It's free, community-built, and nonpartisan."** Focuses on constitutional rights, legal standards, and material impacts — not party politics.

## Sustained Actions

### Bring It to Your Community

The people most likely to act are the ones who hear about it from someone they trust. Share resistproject.com in spaces where you already have relationships:

- **Book clubs and discussion groups** — Suggest it as a resource for staying informed
- **Faith communities** — Share with your church group, synagogue, mosque, or meeting house
- **Parent groups** — PTA meetings, school board networks, parenting groups
- **Labor organizations** — Union halls, professional associations, worker groups
- **Neighborhood networks** — Local Facebook groups, Nextdoor, community listservs
- **Professional channels** — Slack workspaces, team channels, professional networks

A one-line message is enough: "Found this useful — resistproject.com. 120+ civic actions sourced from government documents."

### Print and Post

Physical materials reach people who aren't plugged into social media:

- **Sticky notes** — Write "resistproject.com" and stick them on community bulletin boards
- **Business cards** — Print simple cards with the URL and a one-line description
- **Flyers** — Post at coffee shops, libraries, laundromats, community centers
- **Handwritten notes** — Leave one with a tip at a restaurant, tuck one into a library book

Keep it simple. The URL is short and memorable: **resistproject.com**

### Start a Local Action Group

Individual action is good. Collective action is better.

- **Gather 5-10 friends** who want to take action but don't know where to start
- **Use the site as your agenda** — Pick a few actions each week and do them together
- **Meet regularly** — Weekly or biweekly, in person or on a call
- **Hold each other accountable** — Check in on who completed what
- **Celebrate progress** — Track how many actions your group has completed together

Accountability partners make sustained action dramatically more likely. A group that meets weekly will accomplish more in a month than most individuals do in a year.
