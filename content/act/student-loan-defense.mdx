# Student Loan Survival Guide

## Quick Summary

This isn't just an advocacy page — it's a **survival guide**. The One Big Beautiful Bill Act rewrote federal student aid, and **July 1, 2026** is the critical deadline. The SAVE plan is gone. Grad PLUS loans end. Parent PLUS gets capped. If you have federal student loans, you need to act **now** — not after July 1 when servicer systems will be overwhelmed and options will be limited.

[← Learn More: Full facts on the OBBBA student loan overhaul](/learn/student-loans)


## Quick Actions

### Step 1: Check Your Loans NOW

Log into [StudentAid.gov](https://studentaid.gov/) and review:
- Your current repayment plan
- Your total loan balance and loan types
- Your servicer contact information
- Whether you have Parent PLUS loans that need consolidation

This takes 5 minutes and is the essential first step.

---

### Step 2: If You're on SAVE — Evaluate Alternatives Now

Don't wait for the July 1 servicer notice. Use the [Federal Loan Simulator](https://studentaid.gov/loan-simulator/) to compare your options:

- **RAP (Repayment Assistance Plan):** Income-driven. Payments based on AGI. 30-year forgiveness. Qualifies for PSLF. Best for lower-income borrowers.
- **IBR (Income-Based Repayment):** Still available for pre-July 2026 loans. 20-25 year forgiveness. May have lower payments than RAP depending on income.
- **Tiered Standard:** Fixed payments. No forgiveness. Best for higher-income borrowers who want to pay off quickly.

**If you do nothing:** You'll be auto-enrolled in the Standard Plan after 90 days — potentially the most expensive option.

---

### Step 3: Parent PLUS Borrowers — Consolidate BY JUNE 30

**This is the most urgent deadline.** If you have Parent PLUS loans and have NOT consolidated into a Direct Consolidation Loan:

1. Go to [StudentAid.gov](https://studentaid.gov/) and apply for a **Direct Consolidation Loan** immediately
2. Processing takes 4–6 weeks — the recommended April 1 deadline has passed, but applications are still being processed
3. After June 30, 2026, you **permanently lose access to ALL income-driven repayment plans**
4. After consolidation, enroll in ICR before July 1, 2028 to preserve IDR eligibility

---

### Step 4: If You're Pursuing PSLF — Verify Now

- Confirm your employer qualifies under the new rules at the [PSLF Help Tool](https://studentaid.gov/manage-loans/forgiveness-cancellation/public-service)
- Submit an Employment Certification Form
- Note: The new "illegal purpose" employer rule may affect some government and nonprofit employers
- For new loans after July 1, RAP is the **only** PSLF-qualifying IDR plan

## Sustained Actions

### File Complaints About Servicer Issues

If your servicer mishandles your account, provides wrong information, or fails to process your plan change:

- **CFPB (Consumer Financial Protection Bureau):** [consumerfinance.gov/complaint](https://www.consumerfinance.gov/complaint/) or call **1-855-411-2372**
- **FSA Ombudsman:** [studentaid.gov/feedback-ombudsman](https://studentaid.gov/feedback-ombudsman/) or call **1-877-557-2575**
- **Your state's student loan ombudsman** (if your state has one): California DFPI, New York AG, Massachusetts AG

### Support Organizations Fighting for Borrowers

- **[Protect Borrowers](https://protectborrowers.org/)** — Advocacy, litigation, and congressional contact tools
- **[TICAS (The Institute for College Access & Success)](https://ticas.org/)** — Policy research and borrower FAQs
- **[Student Loan Borrowers Assistance (NCLC)](https://studentloanborrowerassistance.org/)** — Free legal information and borrower guides
- **[NASFAA](https://www.nasfaa.org/ob3)** — Financial aid professional resources and OBBBA web center

### Prepare for the IDR Tax "Bomb"

If you're on a long-term IDR plan and expect forgiveness in future years:

- Start saving now for the potential tax bill
- Consult a tax professional about the **IRS insolvency exclusion** (Form 982)
- Learn about **offer in compromise** (Form 656) and **installment agreements** (Form 9465)
- PSLF forgiveness is still tax-free — consider whether public service employment is an option

### Check Your State's Resources

Some states have their own student loan protections and assistance:

- **California:** [DFPI Student Loan Resources](https://dfpi.ca.gov/)
- **Massachusetts:** [Mass.gov Student Loan Assistance](https://www.mass.gov/info-details/student-loan-assistance)
- **New York:** [EDCAP — Free Counseling for NY Borrowers](https://www.edcapny.org/)
