# Fight Back Against Harmful Tariffs

## Quick Summary

**Goal:** Demand that Congress reclaim its constitutional authority over trade and oppose tariffs that function as a hidden tax on American families. The tariffs imposed since February 2025 cost the average household $2,100-$3,800 per year in higher prices. Congress has the power to end this -- but only if constituents demand it.

**Who pays tariffs?** American importers pay tariffs to U.S. Customs at the border. Not foreign countries. The cost is passed to you through higher prices on groceries, cars, electronics, clothing, and building materials.

**Congress can stop this.** Article I, Section 8 of the Constitution gives Congress -- not the president -- the power to set tariffs. Multiple bills have been introduced to reclaim that authority.

|--------------|---------------|-----|
| **Avocados, tomatoes, berries** | 10-25% higher | 25% tariff on Mexico, the primary source |

| **Gas and heating oil** | Variable increase | 10% tariff on Canadian energy imports |
| **Lumber for home projects** | 15-25% higher | 25% tariff on Canadian lumber |
| **Phone, laptop, electronics** | 10-30% higher | Tariffs on Chinese electronics and components |
| **Clothing** | 10-20% higher | Tariffs on Chinese and Vietnamese textiles |
| **Appliances** (washer, dryer, fridge) | 10-20% higher | Steel/aluminum tariffs plus finished goods tariffs |

## Sustained Actions

### Support Trade Reform Legislation

Several bills have been introduced to reclaim congressional trade authority:

**Trade Review Act**
- Would require congressional approval for tariffs imposed under IEEPA
- Bipartisan support in both chambers
- Contact your representatives to urge co-sponsorship

**Reclaiming Congressional Trade Authority Act**
- Would terminate existing IEEPA tariff emergency declarations
- Would prohibit future use of IEEPA for tariffs without congressional authorization

**How to support:**
1. Call your representatives and ask them to co-sponsor these bills by name
2. Write letters to the editor supporting congressional trade authority
3. Attend town halls and raise trade authority as a constitutional issue
4. Track bill progress at [Congress.gov](https://www.congress.gov/)

### Support Affected Communities

#### Farmers

American farmers have been among the hardest hit by retaliatory tariffs. Ways to help:
- **Buy local produce** at farmers markets to support farmers struggling with lost export markets
- **Contact your state Farm Bureau** to learn about advocacy efforts: [fb.org](https://www.fb.org/)
- **Urge Congress** to provide meaningful relief beyond temporary aid packages

#### Small Businesses

Small businesses cannot absorb tariff costs the way large corporations can:
- **Shop local** at small businesses that are absorbing tariff costs to keep prices competitive
- **Support small business advocacy** through your local chamber of commerce
- **Share small business tariff stories** with your representatives

#### Workers in Affected Industries

Workers in agriculture, manufacturing, and retail face job losses from tariff impacts:
- **Support worker retraining programs** -- urge Congress to fund Trade Adjustment Assistance
- **Advocate for extended unemployment benefits** for tariff-displaced workers

### Attend Town Halls and Public Events

**Town halls are the single most effective way to pressure elected officials in person.** Tips:
1. Find your representative's town hall schedule at [Town Hall Project](https://townhallproject.com/)
2. Prepare a question about tariffs and congressional trade authority
3. Bring specific data: "$2,100-$3,800 per household" or "685,000 jobs at risk"
4. Be respectful but persistent -- elected officials remember constituents who show up
5. Record the response (with permission) and share on social media

### [+] Key Facts for Advocacy

Use these in calls, emails, letters, and conversations:

- **"Tariffs are a tax on American consumers, not foreign countries."** -- Federal Reserve Chair Jerome Powell
- **$2,100-$3,800** per household per year in higher prices (CBO estimate)
- **685,000** full-time equivalent jobs at risk (Tax Foundation)
- **0.7%** reduction in long-run GDP (Tax Foundation)
- **$1.4 trillion** in tariff revenue collected from American importers over 10 years (CBO) -- proving tariffs are a domestic tax
- **Article I, Section 8** of the Constitution gives Congress the exclusive power to set tariffs
- The U.S. Court of International Trade **ruled IEEPA tariffs unconstitutional** in April 2025
- IEEPA was **never used for tariffs before 2025** -- it was designed for sanctioning hostile nations
