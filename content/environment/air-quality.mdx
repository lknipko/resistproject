# Air Quality & Emissions Standards

## Quick Summary

**Key Point:** EPA is rolling back air quality protections on multiple fronts. The value of human life has been removed from regulatory cost-benefit analysis, making it mathematically impossible to justify new health regulations. Vehicle emission standards are being weakened. The Clean Power Plan replacement has been repealed. PM2.5 fine particulate matter standards are under review for weakening. Scientific advisory boards have been dissolved and reconstituted with industry-aligned members.

Air pollution kills an estimated 100,000-200,000 Americans annually. These rollbacks will increase that number.

## Facts

### Eliminating the Value of Human Life (VSL)

EPA Administrator Lee Zeldin directed the agency to stop using the "Value of a Statistical Life" (VSL) -- currently approximately $11.8 million per life -- in regulatory cost-benefit analyses. The VSL is not a statement about what a life is "worth." It is an economic metric that quantifies how much regulatory health benefits are worth in dollar terms.

**How VSL works in practice:**
- A proposed rule to reduce particulate matter costs industry $2 billion/year
- The rule prevents 300 premature deaths/year
- Benefits: 300 lives x $11.8 million = $3.54 billion
- Since benefits ($3.54B) exceed costs ($2B), the rule is justified

Without VSL, only the $2 billion cost appears. Health benefits are described qualitatively but not monetized, making it nearly impossible to justify any regulation that imposes costs on industry.

[source: EPA Mortality Risk Valuation](https://www.epa.gov/environmental-economics/mortality-risk-valuation) | [source: EPA Guidelines for Economic Analyses](https://www.epa.gov/environmental-economics/guidelines-preparing-economic-analyses)

### Regulations That Relied on VSL

| Regulation | Deaths Prevented/Year | Monetized Benefit |
|-----------|----------------------|-------------------|
| Clean Air Act PM2.5 standards | ~13,000 | ~$150 billion |
| Mercury and Air Toxics Standards | ~11,000 | ~$130 billion |
| Clean Power Plan (2015) | ~3,600 | ~$42 billion |
| Lead and Copper Rule | ~1,000 | ~$12 billion |
| Cross-State Air Pollution Rule | ~2,700 | ~$32 billion |

Without VSL, none of these could have been justified under cost-benefit analysis.

[source: EPA Benefits and Costs of the Clean Air Act](https://www.epa.gov/clean-air-act-overview/benefits-and-costs-clean-air-act)

### PM2.5 Standards Under Review

Fine particulate matter (PM2.5) is the deadliest form of air pollution, responsible for heart attacks, strokes, lung cancer, and premature death. EPA's 2024 revision tightened the annual standard from 12 to 9 micrograms per cubic meter. This revision is being reconsidered with potential weakening.

[source: EPA PM2.5 NAAQS](https://www.epa.gov/pm-pollution/national-ambient-air-quality-standards-naaqs-pm)

### Vehicle Emission Standards Rolled Back

EPA has moved to weaken Biden-era vehicle emission standards that would have required approximately 56% of new car sales to be electric by 2032. The rollback gives automakers more flexibility and extends timelines.

[source: EPA Vehicle Emission Standards](https://www.epa.gov/regulations-emissions-vehicles-and-engines)

### Scientific Advisory Boards Dissolved

EPA's Science Advisory Board (SAB) and Clean Air Scientific Advisory Committee (CASAC) were defunded and reconstituted with industry-aligned members. These committees provide independent scientific review of EPA regulations. The requirement that members be independent of regulated industries was reversed.

[source: NPR - EPA Science Committees Dismissed](https://www.npr.org/2025/01/29/nx-s1-5278637/why-members-of-two-epa-science-advisory-committees-were-let-go)

### 10-for-1 Deregulation Mandate

Executive Order 14192 requires agencies to repeal 10 existing regulations for every new one. This effectively prevents EPA from issuing any new air quality protections.

[source: Executive Order 14192](https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2025/02/05/2025-02349/unleashing-prosperity-through-deregulation)

## Analysis

### What This Means

- New health-based regulations are effectively blocked by the VSL removal and 10-for-1 mandate
- Existing regulations face legal challenges without VSL-based justification for enforcement
- Industry-aligned advisory boards will recommend weaker standards
- Air quality improvements that have saved millions of lives over 50 years are being reversed
- The health costs will fall on taxpayers, not on polluters

### Who's Affected

- **Everyone:** Air pollution exposure affects all Americans, especially children, elderly, and those with respiratory conditions
- **Environmental justice communities:** Low-income neighborhoods and communities of color are disproportionately located near pollution sources
- **Urban areas:** Vehicle emission rollbacks will worsen urban air quality
- **Downwind communities:** Cross-state air pollution affects regions far from emission sources
- **Healthcare system:** Increased hospitalizations, ER visits, and chronic disease

## Sustained Actions

### Citizen Air Monitoring

- **Purchase a PurpleAir sensor** (~$250) to contribute to community air quality data
- **Join community monitoring programs** through your local health department
- **Document air quality events** -- photos, sensor readings, health impacts create evidence for litigation

### Support Organizations

- **American Lung Association** ([lung.org](https://www.lung.org)) -- Air quality advocacy and "State of the Air" reports
- **Clean Air Task Force** ([catf.us](https://www.catf.us)) -- Technology and policy solutions for air pollution
- **Moms Clean Air Force** ([momscleanairforce.org](https://www.momscleanairforce.org)) -- Parent-led advocacy for clean air

### Push for State-Level Standards

California and other states have historically adopted stricter air quality standards than federal requirements:
- Support your state's adoption of California emission standards (Section 177 states)
- Advocate for state-level PM2.5 monitoring and enforcement
- Push for state "right to clean air" legislation
