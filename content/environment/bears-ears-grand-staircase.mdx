# Bears Ears & Grand Staircase-Escalante

## Quick Summary

**Key Point:** Two of Utah's largest national monuments -- Bears Ears (1.36 million acres) and Grand Staircase-Escalante (1.87 million acres) -- face renewed threats from the Trump administration, which is considering redrawing their boundaries for energy development. These monuments protect over 100,000 archaeological sites, sacred Indigenous lands, world-class paleontological resources, and irreplaceable desert ecosystems. Congress is simultaneously moving to overturn Grand Staircase-Escalante's management plan through the Congressional Review Act. A pending Tenth Circuit decision could reshape presidential authority over national monuments for decades.

71% of Utah voters support Bears Ears National Monument. These are publicly owned lands with extraordinary cultural, scientific, and ecological value.

## Facts

### Key Points

- **Bears Ears** was designated by President Obama in December 2016 at 1.35 million acres, reduced 85% by Trump in 2017, restored by Biden in 2021 to 1.36 million acres, and now faces another potential reduction
- **Grand Staircase-Escalante** was designated by President Clinton in 1996 at 1.7 million acres, cut nearly in half by Trump in 2017, restored by Biden in 2021 to 1.87 million acres, and faces both boundary review and management plan rollback
- Interior Secretary Doug Burgum ordered a review of "withdrawn public lands" on February 3, 2025, with agency plans due by February 18
- Sen. Mike Lee and Rep. Celeste Maloy introduced House Joint Resolution 151 in March 2026 to use the Congressional Review Act to overturn Grand Staircase-Escalante's management plan
- The Tenth Circuit Court of Appeals heard oral arguments in September 2024 in consolidated cases challenging Biden's monument restorations; a ruling remains pending as of early 2026
- At least 14 new uranium and vanadium mining claims have been filed in the Bears Ears area since Trump's first reduction

[source: Executive Order 14174 - Unleashing American Energy](https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2025/01/29/2025-02054/unleashing-american-energy) | [source: NRDC - Bears Ears Litigation](https://www.nrdc.org/court-battles/nrdc-et-v-trump-bears-ears)

### [+] Bears Ears: History & Significance

**The Antiquities Act (1906):** The Antiquities Act gives presidents authority to designate national monuments on federal lands to protect "historic landmarks, historic and prehistoric structures, and other objects of historic or scientific interest." The Supreme Court has repeatedly upheld broad presidential discretion under this law. Whether a president can *reduce* or *eliminate* a monument is legally untested by the Supreme Court.

[source: National Park Service - Antiquities Act](https://www.nps.gov/subjects/archeology/antiquities-act.htm)

**Timeline:**

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| July 2015 | Bears Ears Inter-Tribal Coalition (Hopi Tribe, Navajo Nation, Ute Mountain Ute Tribe, Pueblo of Zuni, Ute Indian Tribe) formally proposes monument designation to President Obama |
| December 28, 2016 | President Obama designates Bears Ears National Monument (1.35 million acres) -- first monument requested by a tribal coalition |
| December 4, 2017 | President Trump signs proclamation reducing Bears Ears by 85%, from 1.35 million acres to approximately 202,000 acres in two smaller units |
| 2017-2021 | Multiple lawsuits filed challenging Trump's authority to reduce monuments; mining claims staked in formerly protected areas |
| October 8, 2021 | President Biden restores Bears Ears to 1.36 million acres (slightly larger than original) |
| June 2022 | BLM, Forest Service, and the five tribes of the Bears Ears Commission commit to historic co-management agreement |
| January 2025 | Bears Ears Resource Management Plan finalized by BLM and USFS in partnership with Bears Ears Commission |
| January 20, 2025 | Executive Order 14174 directs review of all national monument designations since 1996 |
| February 2025 | Interior Secretary Burgum orders review of withdrawn public lands |
| April 2025 | Reports confirm Trump administration is considering redrawing boundaries of six Western monuments including Bears Ears |

[source: Bears Ears Inter-Tribal Coalition - Timeline](https://www.bearsearscoalition.org/timeline/) | [source: DOI - Bears Ears Co-Management](https://www.doi.gov/pressreleases/blm-forest-service-and-five-tribes-bears-ears-commission-commit-historic-co-management)

**Archaeological and Cultural Resources:**

Bears Ears contains more than **100,000 cultural and archaeological sites** spanning 13,000 years of continuous human history, making it the most significant unprotected archaeological area in the United States when it was designated. The landscape includes:

- Ancient cliff dwellings and granaries
- Rock art panels (petroglyphs and pictographs)
- Ancestral Puebloan villages and kivas
- Historic trails and trade routes
- 117 culturally significant plant species used by Indigenous nations for food, medicine, ceremony, and daily life

[source: Bears Ears Inter-Tribal Coalition - Archaeological Significance](https://www.bearsearscoalition.org/archaeological-significance/) | [source: National Trust for Historic Preservation](https://savingplaces.org/places/ancestral-places-of-southeast-utah)

**Paleontological Resources:**

Bears Ears encompasses 1.3 million acres with exceptionally fossiliferous rock units spanning from the Pennsylvanian Period through the mid-Cretaceous Period -- a semi-continuous depositional record covering over 200 million years. Fossils reveal critical insights into major biological transitions and mass extinctions.

[source: Geology of the Intermountain West - BENM Paleontology](https://giw.utahgeology.org/giw/index.php/GIW/article/view/82) | [source: Natural History Museum of Utah](https://nhmu.utah.edu/articles/2023/08/fossils-bears-ears)

### [+] Grand Staircase-Escalante

**Timeline:**

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| September 18, 1996 | President Clinton designates Grand Staircase-Escalante National Monument (1.7 million acres) -- the first BLM-managed national monument |
| December 4, 2017 | President Trump reduces Grand Staircase-Escalante by nearly half, cutting approximately 800,000 acres |
| 2018 | Mining interests file claims in areas stripped of monument protection |
| October 8, 2021 | President Biden restores Grand Staircase-Escalante to 1.87 million acres |
| January 2025 | BLM finalizes updated 362-page management plan reflecting Biden-era boundaries |
| February 2026 | Sierra Club issues statement opposing Trump administration's attack on Grand Staircase-Escalante |
| March 4, 2026 | Sen. Mike Lee and Rep. Celeste Maloy introduce HJR 151 to use the Congressional Review Act to overturn the management plan |

[source: The Wilderness Society - Grand Staircase-Escalante](https://www.wilderness.org/wild-places/utah/national-monuments-grand-staircase-escalante) | [source: Inside Climate News - Congressional Threat](https://insideclimatenews.org/news/26012026/grand-staircase-escalante-national-monument-is-yet-again-under-threat-this-time-from-congress/)

**Paleontological Significance:**

Grand Staircase-Escalante is one of the most important paleontological sites in the world. The Kaiparowits Plateau contains unrivaled Late Cretaceous fossil beds. Scientists have identified **30 new species** from fossils found here, including:

- New tyrannosaur species
- The first known specimen of a Triceratops ancestor (Diabloceratops eatoni)
- Dinosaur skin impressions and tracks
- Marble-sized bird eggs
- Marine and brackish-water molluscs, turtles, crocodilians, lizards, fish, and mammals

Only a tiny fraction of the area has been excavated -- the scientific potential is enormous.

[source: BLM - GSENM Science & Research](https://www.blm.gov/programs/national-conservation-lands/utah/grand-staircase-escalante-national-monument/science-research) | [source: The Wilderness Society - Tyrannosaur Discovery](https://www.wilderness.org/articles/blog/tyrannosaur-fossil-find-grand-staircase-escalante-reminder-public-lands-scientific-importance)

**Congressional Review Act Threat:**

HJR 151, introduced in March 2026, would use the Congressional Review Act (CRA) to overturn the Biden-era management plan and replace it with the 2021 Trump-era version. If passed:

- The monument boundaries would remain, but protections on how the land is managed would be weakened
- More drilling, mining, and grazing could be permitted
- The CRA prevents the agency from issuing a "substantially similar" rule without new legislation
- This makes the CRA approach particularly dangerous -- it would lock in weaker protections indefinitely

[source: Deseret News - Utah Congressional Delegation](https://www.deseret.com/the-west/2026/03/05/utah-congressional-delegation-overturn-grand-staircase-escalante-land-management-plan/) | [source: Inside Climate News - CRA Maneuver](https://insideclimatenews.org/news/05032026/utah-grand-staircase-escalante-national-monument-drilling-mining/)

### [+] Current Threats

**Executive Review of Monuments:**

The Trump administration is reportedly considering shrinking the boundaries of six national monuments in the West for energy development, including both Bears Ears and Grand Staircase-Escalante. Interior Secretary Burgum's February 2025 order directed agencies to submit plans for reviewed public lands by February 18.

[source: NBC News - Redrawing Monument Boundaries](https://www.nbcnews.com/news/us-news/trump-administration-consider-redrawing-boundaries-national-monuments-rcna190740)

**Mining and Drilling:**

- **Uranium mining:** Atomic Minerals Corp. has authorization to drill 25 exploratory holes just outside Bears Ears in search of uranium. At least 14 new uranium and vanadium claims have been filed in the Bears Ears area, including the Easy Peasy Mine where 30 tons of ore were excavated in 2018.
- **Coal:** Grand Staircase-Escalante sits atop significant coal deposits -- the original monument designation in 1996 blocked a proposed coal mine.
- **Oil and gas:** Reduced monument boundaries would open lands to federal oil and gas leasing at $10/acre minimum bids.

[source: Grand Canyon Trust - Open for Business](https://www.grandcanyontrust.org/blog/bears-ears-and-grand-staircase-open-business/) | [source: Environment America - Uranium Threat](https://environmentamerica.org/articles/bears-ears-national-monument-threatened-by-uranium-mining/)

**Federal Funding Cuts:**

Federal research funding cuts have impacted scientific work in the monuments. A National Science Foundation grant supporting research on potential water contamination from uranium mines in the Bears Ears area ran out in March 2026, and the geologist leading the study was forced to crowdfund to continue.

[source: Moab Sun News - Crowdfunding for Research](https://moabsunnews.com/2026/03/02/geologist-crowdfunds-to-continue-critical-bears-ears-research-after-federal-funding-cuts/)

### [+] Tribal Sovereignty & Co-Management

**The Bears Ears Inter-Tribal Coalition** is composed of five sovereign tribal nations:

1. **Hopi Tribe** -- Ancestral homeland; clan migration routes pass through the area
2. **Navajo Nation** -- Surrounds the monument; thousands of Navajo families live on and near the land
3. **Ute Mountain Ute Tribe** -- Historical territory; the twin buttes "Bears Ears" are a sacred landmark
4. **Pueblo of Zuni** -- Ancestral connection through clan histories and ceremonial practices
5. **Ute Indian Tribe of the Uintah and Ouray Reservation** -- Historical territory and cultural connections

Bears Ears was the **first national monument in U.S. history designated at the request of a tribal coalition**. The 2022 co-management agreement between federal agencies and the five tribes was a landmark in federal-tribal relations, giving tribes formal authority in managing the monument for the first time.

The Coalition has operated the Bears Ears Conservation Corps since 2022, training participants in land management, cultural preservation, and environmental conservation. In 2025, the Coalition launched the Bears Ears Youth Council to empower the next generation of Indigenous leaders. The Coalition celebrated its 10th anniversary in 2025.

[source: Bears Ears Inter-Tribal Coalition](https://www.bearsearscoalition.org/) | [source: NARF - Tribal Legal Defense](https://narf.org/cases/bears-ears/)

### [+] Litigation Status

**Two parallel legal tracks** affect these monuments:

**1. Garfield County v. Biden / Dalton v. Biden (Tenth Circuit)**
- The State of Utah, Garfield County, and others sued in 2022 challenging President Biden's restoration of both monuments
- Plaintiffs argue the restorations exceed Antiquities Act authority because the monuments are too large to protect specific historic objects
- A federal district court dismissed the lawsuits in August 2023
- The Tenth Circuit Court of Appeals heard oral arguments on September 26, 2024
- **As of early 2026, a decision remains pending** -- this ruling could define the scope of presidential monument authority

[source: NRDC - Garfield County v. Biden](https://www.nrdc.org/court-battles/garfield-county-et-v-biden-et-and-dalton-et-v-biden-et-bears-ears-and-grand-staircase)

**2. NRDC v. Trump / Wilderness Society v. Trump (stayed)**
- Filed during Trump's first term challenging his authority to *reduce* monuments
- These cases were stayed after Biden restored the monuments
- As of June 2025, the cases remain stayed pending the Tenth Circuit's decision in the Garfield County case
- If Trump reduces the monuments again, these cases could be revived or new litigation filed

[source: NRDC - Bears Ears Case](https://www.nrdc.org/court-battles/nrdc-et-v-trump-bears-ears) | [source: NRDC - Grand Staircase Case](https://www.nrdc.org/court-battles/wilderness-society-et-v-trump-et-grand-staircase-escalante)

**Key Legal Question:** The Antiquities Act clearly grants presidents power to *create* monuments. Whether they can *reduce or eliminate* them has never been definitively resolved by the Supreme Court. The Tenth Circuit's pending decision may be the most significant test of this question in the Act's 120-year history.

## Analysis

### What This Means

- **Unprecedented convergence of threats:** These monuments face simultaneous pressure from executive review (boundary reduction), congressional action (CRA management plan rollback), and pending litigation -- a multi-front assault on public land protections
- **Tribal sovereignty at stake:** Reducing Bears Ears would undermine the historic co-management agreement and the principle that tribal nations should have a voice in managing their ancestral lands
- **Irreversible damage:** Archaeological sites, once disturbed by mining or road construction, cannot be restored. Paleontological resources destroyed by extraction are lost permanently.
- **Scientific loss:** Federal funding cuts and potential boundary reductions threaten ongoing research into uranium contamination, climate history, and species evolution
- **Economic tradeoff:** Utah's outdoor recreation economy generates billions annually; extractive industries in monument areas would produce comparatively small returns while potentially diminishing recreation and tourism value
- **Legal precedent:** If the Tenth Circuit or Supreme Court rules that presidents can reduce monuments, every national monument in the country becomes vulnerable to future administrations

### Who's Affected

- **Tribal nations:** Five sovereign nations with deep ancestral and spiritual connections to Bears Ears -- reducing the monument disrespects treaty obligations and tribal sovereignty
- **Scientists and researchers:** World-class archaeological, paleontological, and ecological research depends on intact landscapes; NSF funding cuts compound the problem
- **Tourism and recreation economy:** Southern Utah's economy increasingly depends on visitors drawn to public lands -- Kane County and San Juan County have seen income growth tied to monument tourism
- **Local communities:** Rural communities near the monuments depend on recreation jobs; uranium mining history in the region has left a legacy of contamination and health impacts, particularly for Navajo communities
- **Wildlife:** The monuments protect habitat for desert bighorn sheep, Mexican spotted owl, peregrine falcon, mountain lion, black bear, and hundreds of other species across diverse ecosystems from desert canyon to alpine forest
- **All Americans:** These publicly owned lands preserve irreplaceable cultural heritage and scientific resources for current and future generations

### Submit Public Comment on Monument Reviews

When the Department of Interior opens public comment periods on monument reviews, your voice matters:
- Watch for Federal Register notices at [federalregister.gov](https://www.federalregister.gov/) -- search for "national monument review"
- Comments citing specific resources (archaeological sites, endangered species, paleontological resources) carry the most weight
- Personal stories about visiting or researching in these monuments are powerful testimony
- The BLM ePlanning portal posts environmental assessments: [eplanning.blm.gov](https://eplanning.blm.gov/)

### Visit the Monuments

Visitation numbers directly demonstrate public support for monument protections:
- **Bears Ears:** Access via Utah Highway 95 and Highway 261 from Blanding, UT. Key sites include Moon House ruin, Mule Canyon, and House on Fire
- **Grand Staircase-Escalante:** Access from Escalante, Boulder, Kanab, or Big Water, UT. Key sites include Calf Creek Falls, Zebra Slot Canyon, and Devils Garden
- Practice responsible visitation -- stay on trails, do not touch rock art or archaeological sites, pack out all trash
