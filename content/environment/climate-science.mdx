# Climate Change: The Evidence

## Quick Summary

**Key Point:** Earth has warmed 1.47°C (2.65°F) above pre-industrial levels as of 2024 -- the hottest year in recorded history. Atmospheric CO2 has reached 430 ppm, higher than at any point in at least 800,000 years. Over 99.9% of peer-reviewed climate studies agree: humans are causing it. The evidence is not a matter of opinion -- it is measured, quantified, and independently verified by every major scientific institution on Earth.

This page compiles the primary-source data in one place. It is designed to be shared with anyone who has questions about whether climate change is real, whether humans are causing it, or whether scientists actually agree. Every claim links directly to NASA, NOAA, or peer-reviewed research.

### The Numbers

- **2024 was the hottest year ever recorded.** Global temperatures reached 1.47°C above pre-industrial levels (1850-1900). 2025 was the third-hottest. The 10 warmest years on record have all occurred since 2015. [source: NASA - 2024 Warmest Year on Record](https://www.nasa.gov/news-release/temperatures-rising-nasa-confirms-2024-warmest-year-on-record/)

- **Atmospheric CO2 hit 430.5 ppm in May 2025** -- the highest level in at least 800,000 years of ice core records. Before the Industrial Revolution, CO2 was 280 ppm. It is now over 50% higher. [source: NOAA Global Monitoring Laboratory](https://gml.noaa.gov/ccgg/trends/) | [source: Scripps - CO2 Milestone](https://scripps.ucsd.edu/news/annual-carbon-dioxide-peak-passes-another-milestone)

- **CO2 is rising 100 times faster** than any natural increase in the geological record. The 2024 annual increase of 3.58 ppm was the largest in the 67-year Keeling Curve record. [source: NOAA - CO2 Levels Surge](https://www.noaa.gov/news-release/during-year-of-extremes-carbon-dioxide-levels-surge-faster-than-ever)

- **Sea level has risen 4 inches (10 cm) since 1993.** The rate has more than doubled, from 0.07 inches/year in 1993 to 0.17 inches/year currently. In 2024, it rose even faster -- 0.23 inches in a single year. [source: NASA Sea Level](https://science.nasa.gov/earth/explore/earth-indicators/sea-leve/) | [source: NASA - 2024 Sea Level Rise](https://www.nasa.gov/missions/jason-cs-sentinel-6/sentinel-6-michael-freilich/nasa-analysis-shows-unexpected-amount-of-sea-level-rise-in-2024/)

- **Ice sheets are losing 400+ billion tons of ice per year.** Greenland loses ~266 billion tons/year, Antarctica ~135 billion tons/year. This is a sixfold increase from the 1990s (81 billion tons/year then vs. 475 billion tons/year in the 2010s). [source: NASA Ice Sheets](https://science.nasa.gov/earth/explore/earth-indicators/ice-sheets/)

- **Arctic sea ice is declining 12.2% per decade.** All 19 of the lowest September minimum ice extents have occurred in the last 19 years. In March 2025, Arctic winter sea ice hit the lowest maximum extent in the 47-year satellite record. [source: NSIDC - 2025 Arctic Minimum](https://nsidc.org/sea-ice-today/analyses/2025-arctic-sea-ice-minimum-squeezes-ten-lowest-minimums)

- **The ocean has absorbed so much CO2 that its pH has dropped 0.1 units** -- a 30% increase in acidity. This threatens coral reefs, shellfish, and marine ecosystems worldwide. [source: NOAA Ocean Acidification](https://www.noaa.gov/education/resource-collections/ocean-coasts/ocean-acidification)

- **Over 99.9% of peer-reviewed climate papers** that take a position agree: humans are causing climate change. This is based on analysis of 88,125 studies. [source: Lynas et al. 2021 - Environmental Research Letters](https://iopscience.iop.org/article/10.1088/1748-9326/ac2966) | [source: Cornell University](https://news.cornell.edu/stories/2021/10/more-999-studies-agree-humans-caused-climate-change)

- **The IPCC states it is "unequivocal"** that human influence has warmed the atmosphere, ocean, and land. This is the strongest possible language in scientific assessment. [source: IPCC AR6 Summary for Policymakers](https://www.ipcc.ch/report/ar6/wg1/chapter/summary-for-policymakers/)

### [+] Temperature Record in Detail

Earth's average surface temperature has increased by approximately 1.1°C (2.0°F) since the late 1800s, with most of that warming occurring in the past 50 years. Here is what the data shows, decade by decade.

**How temperature is measured:**

NASA's Goddard Institute for Space Studies (GISS) and NOAA's National Centers for Environmental Information independently analyze temperature data from thousands of weather stations, ship- and buoy-based ocean observations, and Antarctic research stations. Their results consistently agree.

[source: NASA - How Scientists Measure Global Temperature](https://science.nasa.gov/earth/measuring_global_temperature/)

**The warming timeline:**

| Period | Temperature vs. Pre-Industrial | Key Context |
|--------|-------------------------------|-------------|
| 1850-1900 | Baseline (0°C) | Pre-industrial reference period |
| 1980s decade | ~+0.3°C | Warming becomes clearly detectable |
| 1990s decade | ~+0.4°C | Accelerating trend established |
| 2000s decade | ~+0.6°C | Every decade warmer than the last |
| 2010s decade | ~+0.8°C | Sixfold increase in ice sheet loss |
| 2023 | +1.17°C (NOAA) | Second-hottest year on record |
| 2024 | +1.47°C above pre-industrial | Hottest year ever recorded |
| 2025 | +1.19°C above 1951-1980 avg | Third-hottest year on record |

[source: NASA Global Temperature](https://science.nasa.gov/earth/explore/earth-indicators/global-temperature/) | [source: NOAA Climate - Global Temperature](https://www.climate.gov/news-features/understanding-climate/climate-change-global-temperature)

**Key facts:**

- The 10 warmest years on record have all occurred since 2015
- 2024 was the first year the annual average approached 1.5°C above pre-industrial -- the threshold the Paris Agreement aimed to avoid
- For more than half of 2024, monthly temperatures exceeded the 1.5°C threshold
- Upper ocean heat content reached a record high in 2025
- Both Arctic and Antarctic annual sea ice extent ranked among the three lowest years on record in 2025

[source: NASA - 2024 Warmest Year](https://www.nasa.gov/news-release/temperatures-rising-nasa-confirms-2024-warmest-year-on-record/) | [source: NOAA - 2025 Climate Assessment](https://www.ncei.noaa.gov/news/global-climate-202511)

### [+] CO2: 800,000 Years of Context

The concentration of carbon dioxide in the atmosphere is the single most important driver of modern climate change. Ice cores drilled from Antarctic glaciers allow scientists to measure CO2 levels going back 800,000 years -- and what they show is stark.

**The ice core record:**

For 800,000 years, CO2 levels naturally fluctuated between about 180 ppm (during ice ages) and 280 ppm (during warm interglacial periods). CO2 never exceeded 300 ppm during that entire period. These cycles were driven by changes in Earth's orbit and tilt (Milankovitch cycles), with CO2 acting as an amplifying feedback.

[source: NOAA - CO2 in the Ice Core Record](https://www.climate.gov/teaching/resources/co2-ice-core-record) | [source: NOAA Paleoclimatology - EPICA Dome C 800K Data](https://www.ncei.noaa.gov/access/metadata/landing-page/bin/iso?id=noaa-icecore-6091)

**Then industrialization happened:**

- **Pre-industrial (before ~1750):** ~280 ppm
- **1958 (Keeling Curve begins):** 315 ppm
- **2024 annual average:** 422.7 ppm
- **May 2025 peak:** 430.5 ppm (NOAA) / 430.2 ppm (Scripps)

That means CO2 is now over 50% higher than at any point in 800,000 years. The increase from 280 to 430 ppm has happened in roughly 275 years -- an eyeblink in geological time.

[source: NOAA - CO2 Now 50% Higher Than Pre-Industrial](https://www.noaa.gov/news-release/carbon-dioxide-now-more-than-50-higher-than-pre-industrial-levels) | [source: NASA - Relentless Rise of CO2](https://science.nasa.gov/resource/graphic-the-relentless-rise-of-carbon-dioxide/)

**Rate of increase:**

- Natural CO2 changes between ice ages and warm periods occurred over thousands of years
- The current increase is 100 times faster than any natural increase in the geological record
- The 2024 annual increase of 3.58 ppm was the largest single-year jump in the 67-year Keeling Curve record
- CO2 has increased by roughly 100 ppm in just the last 60 years

[source: NOAA - Atmospheric CO2](https://www.climate.gov/news-features/understanding-climate/climate-change-atmospheric-carbon-dioxide) | [source: NOAA - CO2 Levels Surge](https://www.noaa.gov/news-release/during-year-of-extremes-carbon-dioxide-levels-surge-faster-than-ever)

**The Keeling Curve:**

Charles David Keeling began continuous CO2 measurements at Mauna Loa Observatory in Hawaii in 1958. His record -- the Keeling Curve -- is one of the most important datasets in all of science. It shows the unmistakable upward march of atmospheric CO2, with a sawtooth pattern caused by seasonal plant growth in the Northern Hemisphere.

[source: Scripps - The Keeling Curve](https://scripps.ucsd.edu/programs/keelingcurve/)

### [+] Scientific Consensus

The scientific consensus on human-caused climate change is not a matter of debate among researchers. It is as well-established as the consensus that smoking causes cancer or that the Earth orbits the Sun.

**What the studies show:**

- **Cook et al. (2013):** Examined 11,944 peer-reviewed abstracts from 1991-2011. Among papers expressing a position on the cause of warming, **97.1% endorsed human causation.** [source: Skeptical Science - The 97% Consensus](https://skepticalscience.com/print.php?r=18)

- **Lynas et al. (2021):** Analyzed 88,125 climate-related studies from 2012-2020. Found that **over 99.9% of peer-reviewed papers** that take a position agree humans are causing climate change. Only 28 papers out of 88,125 were skeptical. [source: Lynas et al. 2021 - Environmental Research Letters](https://iopscience.iop.org/article/10.1088/1748-9326/ac2966)

**What "consensus" means:**

Scientific consensus is not a vote. It is the convergence of independent lines of evidence, tested by thousands of researchers using different methods, across different disciplines, over decades. When 99.9% of published research points in the same direction, the question is settled.

**Every major scientific organization in the world agrees:**

- **NASA:** "Scientific evidence for warming of the climate system is unequivocal." [source: NASA Scientific Consensus](https://science.nasa.gov/climate-change/scientific-consensus/)

- **American Physical Society (2021):** "Earth's changing climate is a critical issue... the evidence is incontrovertible." Identifies human activities as the "dominant driver." [source: APS Statement on Climate](https://www.aps.org/about/governance/statements/changing-climate)

- **American Chemical Society:** "Rigorous scientific research concludes greenhouse gases emitted by human activities are the primary driver." [source: ACS Climate Statement](https://www.acs.org/policy/washington-science/2021-public-comments/2021-multisociety-public-comments/2021-acs-cswg-statement.html)

- **American Association for the Advancement of Science (AAAS):** Has repeatedly reaffirmed the scientific consensus. [source: AAAS Climate Statement](https://www.aaas.org/resources/aaas-reaffirms-statement-climate-change)

- **Royal Society & U.S. National Academy of Sciences (joint statement):** Confirmed the evidence and urgency. [source: Royal Society - Climate Change Evidence](https://royalsociety.org/news/2014/climate-change-evidence-causes/)

- **31 U.S. scientific societies** issued a joint letter to Congress in 2016 reaffirming the consensus. [source: 31 Scientific Societies Letter](https://esa.org/blog/2016/06/28/31-scientific-societies-letter-on-global-climate-change/)

There is no major scientific organization anywhere in the world that disputes human-caused climate change. Not one.

### [+] What We Can See

Climate change is not just in the data -- it is visible. These are physical changes measured by satellites, field researchers, and monitoring networks worldwide.

**Ice sheets and glaciers:**

- Greenland loses approximately 266 billion tons of ice per year. Antarctica loses approximately 135 billion tons per year. Combined, this is a sixfold increase from the 81 billion tons per year lost in the 1990s. [source: NASA Ice Sheets](https://science.nasa.gov/earth/explore/earth-indicators/ice-sheets/) | [source: NASA - Six Times Faster](https://sealevel.nasa.gov/news/183/greenland-antarctica-melting-six-times-faster-than-in-the-1990s)

- Glaciers in Arctic Scandinavia and Svalbard experienced their largest annual net ice loss on record between 2023 and 2024. [source: NOAA Arctic Report Card 2025](https://arctic.noaa.gov/report-card/report-card-2025/)

- Alaskan glaciers have lost an average of 125 vertical feet (38 meters) of ice since the mid-20th century. [source: NOAA Arctic Report Card 2025](https://arctic.noaa.gov/report-card/report-card-2025/)

**Sea ice:**

- September Arctic sea ice extent is declining 12.2% per decade compared to the 1981-2010 average. [source: NASA Arctic Sea Ice Minimum](https://science.nasa.gov/earth/explore/earth-indicators/arctic-sea-ice-minimum-extent/)

- March 2025 set the record for lowest Arctic winter maximum ice extent in the entire 47-year satellite record. [source: NSIDC - 2025 Arctic Minimum](https://nsidc.org/sea-ice-today/analyses/2025-arctic-sea-ice-minimum-squeezes-ten-lowest-minimums)

**Sea level:**

- Global sea level has risen 4 inches (10 cm) since satellite measurements began in 1993. [source: NASA Sea Level](https://science.nasa.gov/earth/explore/earth-indicators/sea-leve/)

- The rate of rise has more than doubled, from 0.07 in/year (1993) to 0.17 in/year currently -- and reached 0.23 in/year in 2024. [source: NASA - 2024 Sea Level Rise](https://www.nasa.gov/missions/jason-cs-sentinel-6/sentinel-6-michael-freilich/nasa-analysis-shows-unexpected-amount-of-sea-level-rise-in-2024/)

**Oceans:**

- Ocean surface pH has dropped 0.1 units since industrialization -- a 30% increase in acidity. By century's end it could be 150% more acidic, reaching levels not seen in 20 million years. [source: NOAA Ocean Acidification](https://www.noaa.gov/education/resource-collections/ocean-coasts/ocean-acidification)

- 90% of coral reefs could face severe annual bleaching by 2055 under high-emission scenarios. [source: NOAA - Coral and Climate](https://oceanservice.noaa.gov/facts/coralreef-climate.html)

**Species and ecosystems:**

- Marine species including Humboldt squid, blue whales, and bottlenose dolphins have shifted their ranges northward. [source: NOAA Fisheries - Ocean Acidification](https://www.fisheries.noaa.gov/insight/understanding-ocean-acidification)

- Upper ocean heat content reached a record high in 2025. [source: NOAA - 2025 Climate Assessment](https://www.ncei.noaa.gov/news/global-climate-202511)

### [+] How We Know It's Us

This is the question skeptics ask most often: how do scientists know the warming is caused by humans rather than natural cycles? The answer involves multiple independent lines of evidence that all point to the same conclusion.

**Carbon isotope fingerprinting:**

Different sources of carbon have different isotopic signatures. Fossil fuels are depleted in carbon-13 (^13C) and contain no carbon-14 (^14C) because they are millions of years old. As atmospheric CO2 has increased, the ratio of ^13C to ^12C in the atmosphere has decreased -- exactly what you would expect if the new CO2 is coming from burning fossil fuels, and not from volcanic activity or ocean outgassing.

[source: NOAA - How We Know CO2 Is From Humans](https://www.climate.gov/news-features/climate-qa/how-do-we-know-build-carbon-dioxide-atmosphere-caused-humans) | [source: NOAA - Stable Isotopes](https://gml.noaa.gov/education/isotopes/)

**It's not the Sun:**

- Solar output has been measured precisely by satellites since 1978. It has shown no upward trend -- in fact, solar activity has been slightly declining while temperatures have risen.
- If the Sun were causing the warming, we would expect the upper atmosphere (stratosphere) to warm along with the surface. Instead, the stratosphere is cooling while the surface warms -- exactly what greenhouse gas theory predicts.

[source: NASA - Causes of Climate Change](https://science.nasa.gov/climate-change/causes/)

**It's not volcanoes:**

- Major volcanic eruptions actually cause temporary cooling (1-2 years) by injecting reflective sulfur particles into the stratosphere.
- Human CO2 emissions are over 100 times larger than volcanic CO2 emissions annually.
- The warming trend has continued steadily regardless of volcanic activity.

[source: NASA - Causes of Climate Change](https://science.nasa.gov/climate-change/causes/)

**Pattern matching (fingerprinting):**

Scientists use climate models to simulate what would happen under different scenarios -- natural forces only, human forces only, or both. The observed warming pattern matches human-caused forcing and does not match natural-only forcing. This "fingerprinting" approach has been applied to surface temperature, ocean heat content, atmospheric moisture, and other variables. In every case, the human fingerprint is detected.

[source: IPCC AR6 Chapter 3 - Human Influence on the Climate System](https://www.ipcc.ch/report/ar6/wg1/chapter/chapter-3/)

**The IPCC conclusion:**

The Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change -- representing thousands of scientists from 195 countries -- states: "It is unequivocal that human influence has warmed the atmosphere, ocean and land." Human activities are responsible for approximately 1.1°C of warming since 1850-1900.

[source: IPCC AR6 Summary for Policymakers](https://www.ipcc.ch/report/ar6/wg1/chapter/summary-for-policymakers/) | [source: IPCC Press Release](https://www.ipcc.ch/2021/08/09/ar6-wg1-20210809-pr/)

## Analysis

### What This Means

The scientific evidence is not ambiguous. Earth is warming rapidly, the warming is caused by human greenhouse gas emissions, and the consequences are already measurable:

- **Health:** Heat-related deaths are increasing. Air quality is worsening in many regions due to more frequent and intense wildfires. Mosquito-borne diseases are expanding their range.

- **Economy:** Extreme weather events cost the U.S. over $90 billion annually in damages. Agricultural disruptions, infrastructure damage, and insurance costs are rising. Coastal property values face long-term decline as sea level rise accelerates.

- **National security:** The Department of Defense has identified climate change as a threat multiplier that exacerbates conflict, migration, and resource competition.

- **Ecosystems:** Coral reef collapse, species extinction, ocean acidification, and disrupted food webs are already underway. Many of these changes are irreversible on human timescales.

The 1.5°C threshold set by the Paris Agreement is not an arbitrary number -- it represents the point beyond which many climate impacts become significantly more severe and some become irreversible. In 2024, the global average temperature approached this threshold for the first time.

### Why Denial Persists

The scientific evidence has been clear for decades. Denial persists not because of scientific uncertainty, but because of a deliberate, well-funded campaign to manufacture doubt.

**What Exxon knew:**

- In 1977, Exxon senior scientist James Black told the company's management that "there is general scientific agreement that the most likely manner in which mankind is influencing the global climate is through carbon dioxide release from the burning of fossil fuels." [source: Scientific American - Exxon Knew](https://www.scientificamerican.com/article/exxon-knew-about-climate-change-almost-40-years-ago/)

- A 2023 Harvard-led analysis found that Exxon's internal climate models were remarkably accurate -- between 63% and 83% of their projections correctly predicted subsequent warming, matching or exceeding the accuracy of academic and government models. [source: Supran et al. 2023 - Science](https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/science.abk0063)

- Despite this internal knowledge, ExxonMobil publicly promoted doubt about climate science for decades. Internal documents show a coalition of fossil fuel companies pledged to launch a communications effort with the stated goal: "Victory will be achieved when the average person is uncertain about climate science." [source: Harvard Gazette - Exxon Predictions](https://news.harvard.edu/gazette/story/2023/01/harvard-led-analysis-finds-exxonmobil-internal-research-accurately-predicted-climate-change/)

This is not a conspiracy theory. It is documented in internal corporate memos, peer-reviewed research, and ongoing litigation by states and cities against fossil fuel companies.

The science was never in doubt. The doubt was manufactured.

### Talk About Climate (Really)

Climate silence is one of the biggest barriers to action. Research shows that most Americans are concerned about climate change but rarely discuss it.

- **Start with shared values** -- clean air for kids, protecting property values, energy independence -- not partisan framing
- **Lead with local impacts** -- Utah's inversions, Great Salt Lake, wildfire smoke -- not polar bears
- **Use "I" statements** -- "I worry about..." invites conversation; "You should..." shuts it down
- **Share this page** -- it exists specifically as a resource to share with skeptics

[source: Yale Program on Climate Change Communication](https://climatecommunication.yale.edu/publications/how-to-talk-about-climate-change/)

### Reduce Your Carbon Footprint (the changes that actually matter)

Not all actions are equal. These have the biggest per-person impact:

1. **Switch to renewable electricity** -- Many utilities offer green power options. In Utah, Rocky Mountain Power's Blue Sky program costs ~$2/month extra.
2. **Reduce car trips** -- Transportation is the #1 US emissions sector. Carpool, bike, or take transit when possible.
3. **Eat less beef** -- Beef produces 10x more emissions than chicken and 50x more than beans per gram of protein. Even one meatless day per week matters.
4. **Weatherize your home** -- An energy audit (often free from your utility) identifies the cheapest ways to cut heating/cooling emissions.
5. **Fly less** -- One round-trip cross-country flight equals ~2 months of driving. When possible, choose alternatives.
