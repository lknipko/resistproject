# Deforestation & Logging

## Quick Summary

**Key Point:** The Trump administration has declared an "emergency situation" across 113 million acres of national forest -- nearly 60% of the system -- to fast-track logging and bypass environmental review. An executive order targets 3.3 billion board feet of annual timber production. The Roadless Rule, which protected 58.5 million acres of wild forests for over two decades, is being rescinded. Globally, forests absorb roughly 16 billion metric tonnes of CO2 per year, making deforestation responsible for approximately 10% of all human carbon emissions. The four major commodities driving tropical deforestation -- beef, palm oil, soy, and timber -- are embedded in everyday consumer products.

## Facts

### Executive Order: "Immediate Expansion of American Timber Production"

In March 2025, President Trump signed an executive order directing a massive increase in commercial logging on federal lands. The order:

- Sets an annual target of **3.3 billion board feet** of timber from federal forests
- Directs the Forest Service and BLM to "streamline all processes" for logging approvals
- Orders agencies to use categorical exclusions to bypass NEPA environmental review
- Undermines Endangered Species Act protections for forest-dependent wildlife
- Targets a 25% increase in timber production on national forests

The USDA subsequently issued a memo declaring that more than **113 million acres** of the national forest system are in an "emergency situation," instructing the Forest Service to fast-track logging proposals across these lands.

[source: Executive Order 14225](https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2025/03/executive-orders) | [source: NRDC Analysis](https://www.nrdc.org/media/executive-orders-direct-massive-expansion-logging-public-lands)

### [+] Roadless Rule Rollback

The Roadless Rule, finalized in 2001, protected **58.5 million acres** of undeveloped national forest from road construction and commercial logging -- an area roughly the size of Oregon. In June 2025, the USDA announced intent to rescind the rule entirely.

- **Tongass National Forest** (Alaska): 17 million acres, the largest national forest in the U.S. and North America's largest intact temperate rainforest. Rescission would remove protections from over 9 million undeveloped acres and add nearly 190,000 acres to lands "suitable" for timber production.
- The Tongass is the ancestral homeland of the **Tlingit, Haida, and Tsimshian peoples**, and the Roadless Rule has long been supported by many Native Nations in Southeast Alaska.
- Over **500,000 public comments** were submitted defending the rule in just 21 days during the initial comment period.
- A draft environmental impact statement and proposed rule are expected by March 2026, with the final rule expected in late 2026.

[source: Federal Register - Roadless Area Conservation](https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2025/08/29/2025-16581/special-areas-roadless-area-conservation-national-forest-system-lands) | [source: Earthjustice - Tongass Roadless Rule](https://earthjustice.org/press/2025/tongass-defenders-blast-the-trump-administrations-rollback-of-roadless-rule-protections-on-americas-largest-forest)

### [+] The "Fix Our Forests Act"

The Fix Our Forests Act (H.R. 471 / S. 1462) claims to reduce wildfire risk through forest thinning and streamlined permitting. More than **100 conservation organizations** have opposed the bill, warning it would:

- Stifle citizen participation in land management decisions
- Facilitate large-scale rollbacks of the Endangered Species Act, NEPA, and National Historic Preservation Act
- Open millions of acres to commercial logging under the guise of fire management

Forest scientists note that ecological restoration requires targeting smaller, younger, fire-susceptible trees -- not commercial logging of the largest, most mature, most fire-resilient trees.

[source: Sierra Club - Fix Our Forests](https://www.sierraclub.org/oregon/blog/2025/05/executive-order-and-harmful-legislation-push-huge-logging-increase-public-lands) | [source: Earthjustice Statement](https://earthjustice.org/press/2025/earthjustice-statement-on-senate-fix-our-forests-act)

### [+] Old-Growth Forests: Why They Matter

Old-growth forests are irreplaceable ecosystems that took centuries to develop. They store vastly more carbon per acre than younger forests and provide critical habitat for endangered species like the northern spotted owl, marbled murrelet, and Pacific fisher.

- **U.S. old-growth** covers less than **5%** of original forest area in the lower 48 states
- Once cut, old-growth trees take **200-500+ years** to replace -- longer than most human lifetimes
- Old-growth forests store **2-3 times more carbon** per acre than managed timber plantations
- The Biden administration's 2024 old-growth protection rule is being reversed

[source: USDA Climate Hubs - Forest Carbon](https://www.climatehubs.usda.gov/hubs/northeast/topic/what-can-forest-managers-do-increase-carbon-storage-and-mitigate-climate)

### [+] Forests and Climate Change

Forests are the planet's largest terrestrial carbon sink, absorbing approximately **25% of human carbon emissions** annually:

- The world's forests absorb an average of **16 billion metric tonnes** of CO2 per year
- Deforestation and forest degradation emit approximately **8.1 billion metric tonnes** of CO2 per year
- Net: forests remove about **7.6 billion metric tonnes** more CO2 than they emit
- Deforestation accounts for roughly **10% of total global carbon emissions**

In the Amazon, warming and drying from deforestation creates a feedback loop, causing an additional **5.1%** loss of remaining forest biomass. Of the world's three largest tropical rainforests, only the Congo Basin still functions as a strong net carbon sink.

[source: World Resources Institute - Forest Carbon](https://www.wri.org/insights/forests-absorb-twice-much-carbon-they-emit-each-year) | [source: Nature Communications - Deforestation Climate Feedback](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41467-022-29601-0)

### [+] Global Deforestation: Supply Chain Drivers

An estimated **10.9 million hectares** of forest are lost each year globally (2015-2025). Four commodities drive **40% of tropical deforestation**:

| Commodity | Role in Deforestation | Found In |
|-----------|----------------------|----------|
| **Beef** | Largest single driver globally; forests cleared for cattle ranching | Meat, leather, gelatin, tallow |
| **Palm oil** | Found in ~50% of supermarket products | Soap, shampoo, snacks, bread, chocolate, cosmetics |
| **Soy** | 80% used for animal feed (poultry, pork, dairy) | Animal feed, cooking oil, processed foods |
| **Timber** | Illegal logging and unsustainable harvesting | Construction, furniture, paper, packaging |

**In the Congo Basin**, primary forest loss hit record highs in 2024, with the Republic of the Congo seeing a **150% increase** from 2023 to 2024. **In Brazil**, Amazon deforestation dropped 11% under renewed enforcement, reaching its lowest level in 11 years. **In Indonesia**, forest loss fell 11% but remains threatened by plantation and mining expansion.

[source: World Resources Institute - Deforestation Trends](https://gfr.wri.org/latest-analysis-deforestation-trends) | [source: Our World in Data - Drivers of Deforestation](https://ourworldindata.org/drivers-of-deforestation)

## Analysis

### What This Means

- The "emergency situation" declaration bypasses normal environmental review, allowing logging projects to proceed without assessing impacts on wildlife, water quality, or carbon storage
- Rescinding the Roadless Rule would open the largest remaining tracts of undeveloped national forest to road construction and industrial logging
- Targeting 3.3 billion board feet annually would be the highest federal timber harvest since the 1990s, when old-growth logging devastated Pacific Northwest forests
- Commercial logging of large, mature trees actually **increases** fire risk by replacing fire-resistant old trees with dense young growth
- Supply chain deforestation means U.S. consumer demand contributes to tropical forest loss thousands of miles away

### Who's Affected

- **Indigenous communities:** The Tongass is home to Tlingit, Haida, and Tsimshian peoples; many national forests contain sacred sites
- **Wildlife:** Over 3,000 species depend on old-growth habitat, including spotted owls, salmon, and grizzly bears
- **Rural communities:** Clean water, flood control, and recreation economies depend on intact forests
- **Climate:** Forests are our largest land-based carbon sink; logging them accelerates warming
- **Everyone who breathes:** Forests filter air pollution and regulate regional weather patterns

### Check Your Products for Deforestation Links (5 minutes)

Your purchasing decisions directly affect forests. Here is what to look for:

1. **Look for the FSC label** on all wood and paper products. The [Forest Stewardship Council](https://fsc.org/en) certifies responsibly managed forests. FSC 100% is the gold standard; FSC Mix is acceptable; avoid products with no certification.
2. **Check for palm oil** in ingredient lists -- it appears in ~50% of supermarket products under names like "palm kernel oil," "palmitate," "glyceryl stearate," and "stearic acid." Use the [Rainforest Alliance Palm Oil Shopping Guide](https://www.rainforest-alliance.org/everyday-actions/palm-oil-shopping-guide/) to find certified brands.
3. **Choose Rainforest Alliance Certified** coffee, chocolate, tea, and bananas -- look for the green frog seal.
4. **Reduce beef consumption** -- cattle ranching is the single largest driver of tropical deforestation globally.

### Plant Trees Locally (1-2 hours)

- **Join your city's tree planting program** -- most municipalities host spring and fall planting events. Search "[your city] tree planting volunteer."
- **Plant native trees in your yard** -- contact your county extension office for species recommendations suited to your area.
- **Donate to plant trees remotely:** [Arbor Day Foundation](https://www.arborday.org/programs/reforestation/) plants trees in areas recovering from wildfire, storms, and disease for as little as $1 per tree.
- **Join a community forest restoration project** -- check [American Forests](https://www.americanforests.org) for projects near you.

### File a FOIA Request for Local Logging Plans (15 minutes)

Use the Freedom of Information Act to find out what logging is planned in your national forest:

1. Identify your local national forest at [fs.usda.gov](https://www.fs.usda.gov/land-management/national-forests)
2. Email your request to **wo_foia@fs.fed.us** or submit via [FOIA.gov](https://www.foia.gov/)
3. Request: "All timber sale contracts, environmental assessments, and categorical exclusions for [National Forest name] from January 2025 to present"
4. FOIA requests are free (fees may apply for large requests; fee waivers available for public interest requests)
5. Expected response time: 20 working days

[source: USFS FOIA Site](https://www.fs.usda.gov/about-agency/foia)

## Sustained Actions

### Support Forest Protection Organizations

- **Earthjustice** ([earthjustice.org](https://earthjustice.org)) -- The nation's largest environmental law organization, actively litigating to protect the Roadless Rule and old-growth forests
- **Sierra Club Our Wild America Campaign** ([sierraclub.org](https://www.sierraclub.org/our-wild-america)) -- Forest protection advocacy and grassroots organizing
- **Rainforest Alliance** ([rainforest-alliance.org](https://www.rainforest-alliance.org)) -- Certification programs for sustainable forestry, agriculture, and supply chains. Volunteer opportunities include community education and advocacy events.
- **Arbor Day Foundation** ([arborday.org](https://www.arborday.org)) -- Plant trees, join the Rain Forest Rescue program, or become a member ($15) and receive 10 free native trees. The Foundation has supported planting nearly 500 million trees worldwide.
- **American Forests** ([americanforests.org](https://www.americanforests.org)) -- America's first national conservation nonprofit. Leads large-scale reforestation projects and community urban forestry programs.
- **Defenders of Wildlife** ([defenders.org](https://defenders.org)) -- Fights for endangered species that depend on forest habitat
- **Oregon Wild** ([oregonwild.org](https://oregonwild.org)) -- Leading Pacific Northwest old-growth and forest protection advocacy

### Join Local Conservation Efforts

- **Land trusts:** Find your local land trust at [findalandtrust.org](https://findalandtrust.org/) -- they permanently protect forests from development and logging
- **National forest volunteer programs:** The Forest Service has formal volunteer programs for trail maintenance, restoration, and monitoring -- visit [volunteer.gov](https://www.volunteer.gov)
- **Community forests:** Support community forest initiatives that keep forests in local hands for conservation, recreation, and sustainable use
- **Master Naturalist programs:** State-run programs that train volunteers in ecology and conservation -- search "[your state] master naturalist program"

### Make Deforestation-Free Consumer Choices (Ongoing)

- **Switch to FSC-certified paper, tissues, and toilet paper** -- brands like Seventh Generation, Who Gives A Crap, and Trader Joe's carry FSC options
- **Choose sustainable furniture and building materials** -- ask for FSC-certified wood at lumber yards and furniture stores
- **Download the Sustainable Palm Oil Shopping App** from [RSPO](https://rspo.org/) to scan product barcodes
- **Reduce meat consumption** -- even one meat-free day per week reduces demand for soy-fed livestock and cattle ranching that drives deforestation
- **Avoid products with uncertified tropical hardwoods** like teak, mahogany, and rosewood unless verified as sustainably sourced

### Engage Local Government

- Attend city council and county commission meetings to advocate for **urban tree canopy ordinances** that protect existing trees and require new plantings in development
- Push for **municipal purchasing policies** requiring FSC-certified paper and wood products in government offices
- Support local **tree protection ordinances** that require permits before removing mature trees on private land

### Monitor and Report

- Use [Global Forest Watch](https://www.globalforestwatch.org/) to track deforestation in real time anywhere in the world
- Monitor your local national forest using BLM ePlanning ([eplanning.blm.gov](https://eplanning.blm.gov/)) for upcoming timber sales and environmental assessments
- Report illegal logging or unpermitted tree removal to your state forestry agency and the Forest Service
