# Environmental Litigation Tracker

## Quick Summary

**Key Point:** As of April 2026, there are more than **200 active environmental lawsuits** filed by or against the Trump administration — the most in any comparable period in U.S. history. Environmental groups have filed suits challenging rollbacks of air and water rules, offshore drilling expansions, and public lands leasing. The federal government has counter-sued states trying to hold fossil fuel companies accountable for climate damages. Meanwhile, children's climate cases have produced the first constitutional climate victory in U.S. history — and the Montana legislature immediately tried to override it, triggering a sequel lawsuit. Courts remain one of the last reliable checks on executive environmental destruction.

This page is a living tracker. Last updated: April 4, 2026.

## Facts

### 1. Clean Air Act Cases

**California Vehicle Emissions Waiver (Multiple Cases)**

California's Advanced Clean Cars II (ACC II) rules require automakers to sell increasing percentages of zero-emission vehicles — reaching 100% of new car sales by 2035. The EPA granted California a waiver to enforce these rules in December 2024, but the Trump administration has fought back on multiple fronts.

- **DOJ v. California (March 2026):** The Justice Department sued California in March 2026 to block the state from enforcing its ZEV requirements, asking courts to declare the ACC II rules unlawful [source: Washington Times](https://www.washingtontimes.com/news/2026/mar/12/justice-department-sues-california-zero-emission-vehicle-targets/)
- **California v. United States (2025):** California and 10 other states filed a counter-suit challenging three Congressional Review Act resolutions that purported to rescind EPA's waiver grants for California's Advanced Clean Cars II, Advanced Clean Trucks, and Omnibus Low NOx rules [source: California Air Resources Board](https://ww2.arb.ca.gov/current-litigation)
- **Status:** Multiple overlapping cases working through federal courts as of April 2026; resolution likely to take 1–2 years
- **What's at stake:** Vehicle emissions rules across 12 states plus D.C. — covering roughly 40% of the U.S. auto market

**EPA Endangerment Finding Rescission**

The endangerment finding is the EPA's formal scientific determination that greenhouse gases endanger public health — the legal foundation for all federal climate regulation.

- **Case:** Coalition of 25+ state AGs plus cities, counties, and environmental organizations v. EPA, U.S. Court of Appeals for the D.C. Circuit (filed February 2026)
- **What's at stake:** If the EPA's February 2026 rescission stands, every federal climate regulation loses its legal basis
- **Status:** Pending in D.C. Circuit [source: California DOJ](https://oag.ca.gov/news/press-releases/president-trump-ignores-climate-science-law-will-hold-him-accountable-california)
- See the [EPA Rollbacks page](/environment/epa-rollbacks) for full background on the endangerment finding

**PM2.5 Air Quality Standards**

The Biden EPA tightened the annual fine particle (PM2.5) standard from 12 μg/m³ to 9 μg/m³ in 2024. The Trump EPA has moved to revise or weaken this standard.

- Industry groups filed challenges to the tightened standard even before the Trump EPA moved to roll it back
- Environmental groups, public health organizations, and state AGs are defending the stronger standard in court
- **What's at stake:** Fine particle pollution kills an estimated **100,000 Americans per year** and causes millions of asthma attacks and hospitalizations [source: Manko Gold Katcher & Fox](https://www.mankogold.com/publications-Climate-Change-PFAS-Loper-Chevron-RCRA-CERCLA.html)

### [+] Full Clean Air Litigation Details

**Vehicle Emissions — Truck and Bus Rules:**
- The Trump EPA also moved to revoke or revise California's Advanced Clean Trucks waiver, which requires increasing percentages of zero-emission trucks and buses
- California, truck manufacturers, and environmental groups are in overlapping litigation over these rules in the D.C. Circuit and Ninth Circuit

**Power Plant Emissions:**
- The EPA under Biden finalized carbon emission rules for existing coal plants and new natural gas plants in 2024 — among the strongest power plant rules ever issued
- Industry and Republican state AGs challenged the rules; the Trump EPA subsequently moved to repeal them
- Environmental groups are challenging the repeal, arguing EPA must regulate power plant carbon emissions under the endangerment finding [source: EPA Press Release](https://www.epa.gov/newsreleases/epa-proposes-repeal-biden-harris-epa-regulations-power-plants-which-if-finalized-would)

**Science Advisory Board Purge:**
- On January 28, 2025, EPA dismissed all members of both the Clean Air Scientific Advisory Committee (CASAC) and the Science Advisory Board (SAB) — the independent scientific bodies that review EPA standards
- Legal challenges to these dismissals are ongoing [source: NPR](https://www.npr.org/2025/01/29/nx-s1-5278637/why-members-of-two-epa-science-advisory-committees-were-let-go)

### [+] 2. Clean Water Act Cases

**WOTUS: Waters of the United States (Post-Sackett)**

The Supreme Court's May 2023 decision in *Sackett v. EPA* (598 U.S. ___ (2023)) drastically narrowed which wetlands receive federal Clean Water Act protection, requiring a "continuous surface connection" between a wetland and a regulated water body.

- **Immediate aftermath:** EPA stripped federal protections from approximately **60 million acres** of wetlands and streams after the ruling [source: NRDC](https://www.nrdc.org/stories/what-you-need-know-about-sackett-v-epa)
- **November 2025 rule:** The Trump EPA issued a new WOTUS definition even more restrictive than what the Supreme Court required — potentially removing protections from a majority of wetlands in states like Pennsylvania [source: Energy Law Blog](https://www.theenergylawblog.com/2025/03/articles/industrial-project-development/epa-and-army-corps-issue-new-guidance-for-implementing-wotus-definition-under-supreme-courts-sackett-decision/)
- **Pending:** A public comment period on the new rule closed January 5, 2026; litigation against a final rule is expected
- **What's at stake:** Federal protection for hundreds of millions of acres of wetlands, streams, and other water bodies that filter drinking water, reduce flooding, and provide habitat

**PFAS Drinking Water Standards**

The Biden EPA finalized the first-ever federal drinking water standards for PFAS "forever chemicals" in April 2024, setting limits for six PFAS compounds including PFOA and PFOS.

- **Industry challenge:** Chemical and water utility industry groups challenged the standards in the D.C. Circuit Court of Appeals
- **Status (2026):** Litigation ongoing; the Trump EPA has signaled it may revise or delay the standards
- **What's at stake:** PFAS contamination affects an estimated **45% of U.S. tap water supplies** [source: Manko Gold Katcher & Fox](https://www.mankogold.com/publications-Climate-Change-PFAS-Loper-Chevron-RCRA-CERCLA.html)
- For full background, see the [PFAS Forever Chemicals page](/environment/pfas-forever-chemicals)

### [+] 3. Public Lands Cases

**Bears Ears and Grand Staircase-Escalante Monuments**

Federal courts have been central to the back-and-forth on national monument designations in Utah.

- A lawsuit by conservation groups challenged the Trump administration's 2017 reductions of Bears Ears and Grand Staircase-Escalante monuments; the Biden administration restored the monuments' boundaries in 2021
- The Trump administration has again targeted monument boundaries; litigation is expected [source: Bears Ears page](/environment/bears-ears-grand-staircase)
- See the [Bears Ears & Grand Staircase page](/environment/bears-ears-grand-staircase) for the full tracker

**ANWR Drilling — Arctic National Wildlife Refuge**

The Trump administration moved quickly to resume oil leasing in the Arctic National Wildlife Refuge's coastal plain.

- Environmental groups and Alaska Native organizations (including the Gwich'in Nation) have challenged ANWR leasing in the Ninth Circuit
- The Biden administration had suspended ANWR leases; the Trump administration re-reinstated them
- **Status:** Litigation ongoing in the Ninth Circuit as of 2026
- **What's at stake:** 1.5 million acres of the Arctic coastal plain and the calving grounds of the Porcupine caribou herd

**BLM Oil and Gas Leasing**

The Biden administration paused new oil and gas leasing on federal lands in January 2021; industry groups immediately sued.

- Courts partially blocked the leasing moratorium; the Biden BLM then issued a rule prioritizing conservation over fossil fuel development
- The Trump administration reversed the conservation rule and expanded leasing in 2025; environmental challenges are pending

### [+] 4. Endangered Species Cases

**Gray Wolf**

The gray wolf has been the subject of repeated listing-and-delisting battles, with courts repeatedly finding that delistings violated the Endangered Species Act.

- **August 2025:** A Montana federal judge ruled that the U.S. Fish and Wildlife Service violated the ESA when it removed protections from the gray wolf, finding the delisting was not founded on science [source: Deseret News](https://www.deseret.com/the-west/2025/08/12/federal-judge-gray-wolf-delisting-broke-law-endangered-species-act/)
- **December 2025:** The House voted to delist gray wolves nationwide via legislation — a move that would bar judicial review [source: Earthjustice](https://earthjustice.org/press/2025/earthjustice-responds-to-house-vote-to-strip-gray-wolves-of-endangered-species-act-protections)
- **February 2026:** The Center for Biological Diversity filed a new lawsuit demanding the Fish and Wildlife Service develop a national gray wolf recovery plan [source: Center for Biological Diversity](https://biologicaldiversity.org/w/news/press-releases/new-lawsuit-demands-national-gray-wolf-recovery-plan-2026-02-10/)
- **What's at stake:** Gray wolves remain absent from most of their historic range; ESA protection is the main tool for recovery

**Grizzly Bear and Greater Sage-Grouse**

- Congress has introduced legislation — H.R. 281, "Grizzly Bear State Management Act" — to delist grizzly bears in the Greater Yellowstone Ecosystem and block judicial review of the delisting
- Legislation has also been introduced to block ESA protections for greater sage-grouse, which would affect energy and grazing policy across millions of acres in the West
- Center for Biological Diversity and Earthjustice are the lead litigants defending both species [source: Four Paws USA](https://www.fourpawsusa.org/our-stories/blog-news/legislative-attacks-on-endangered-species-in-2025)
- See the [Endangered Species page](/environment/endangered-species) for full background

**ESA "Harm" Definition**

- The Trump administration is moving to rescind the regulatory definition of "harm" under the ESA, which currently includes habitat destruction that kills or injures wildlife
- If rescinded, logging, development, and agriculture in critical habitat would face far fewer ESA restrictions
- A final rule and litigation are anticipated in 2026 [source: National Agricultural Law Center](https://nationalaglawcenter.org/top-environmental-law-topics-to-watch-in-2026/)

### [+] 5. Climate Accountability Cases

**State Climate Superfund Laws**

New York's Climate Change Superfund Act (signed 2024) requires fossil fuel companies to collectively pay **$75 billion** into a state fund for climate adaptation. Vermont has a similar law. More states are considering similar legislation.

- **DOJ v. New York and DOJ v. Vermont (May 2025):** The Trump DOJ filed federal suits to block both laws, arguing they are preempted by the Clean Air Act and interfere with federal foreign affairs authority [source: CNN](https://www.cnn.com/2025/05/02/climate/trump-climate-lawsuits-hawaii-michigan-new-york-vermont)
- **DOJ v. Hawaii and DOJ v. Michigan (April–May 2025):** The DOJ preemptively sued Hawaii and Michigan to block them from pursuing their own climate lawsuits against fossil fuel companies before those suits were even filed
- Hawaii went ahead and filed its climate lawsuit in state court anyway
- Most environmental law experts view the federal preemption arguments as legally weak
- **Status:** All four cases in early stages of federal court litigation as of April 2026

**State AG Lawsuits Against Fossil Fuel Companies**

More than 16 states and cities have filed or announced intent to file suits holding fossil fuel companies liable for climate damages.

- Active state AG suits include: California, Minnesota, Hawaii, Delaware, New Jersey, and Michigan [source: Center for Climate Integrity](https://climateintegrity.org/lawsuits)
- Hawaii's suit, filed in state court, points to the devastating 2023 Maui wildfires as climate-attributable damage caused by fossil fuel company deception
- The American Petroleum Institute has made defeating these lawsuits one of its top 2026 lobbying priorities [source: Morrison Foerster](https://www.mofo.com/resources/insights/250529-climate-and-carbon-litigation-trends)

**Children's Climate Cases**

- **Held v. Montana:** In August 2023, a Montana judge ruled that the state's policy of ignoring greenhouse gas emissions in energy permitting violated the Montana Constitution's guarantee of a clean and healthful environment — the first successful constitutional climate case in U.S. history. The Montana Supreme Court affirmed on December 18, 2024. [source: Our Children's Trust](https://www.ourchildrenstrust.org/montana)
- **Held v. Montana II (December 2025):** The 2025 Montana legislature passed new laws directly contradicting the Held ruling, preventing agencies from considering GHG emissions. Youth plaintiffs filed a new petition with the Montana Supreme Court to enforce compliance with the original ruling [source: Our Children's Trust](https://heldvmontana.ourchildrenstrust.org/)
- **Juliana v. United States:** The landmark federal youth climate lawsuit was effectively ended when the U.S. Supreme Court declined in March 2025 to hear the plaintiffs' appeal after the Ninth Circuit dismissed for lack of standing [source: KLCC](https://www.klcc.org/crime-law-justice/2025-03-24/supreme-court-denial-effectively-ends-federal-climate-lawsuit-brought-by-teens)
- **Juliana internationally:** 15 of the original Juliana plaintiffs have taken their case to the Inter-American Commission on Human Rights [source: Our Children's Trust](https://www.ourchildrenstrust.org/juliana-v-us)

### [+] 6. Offshore Drilling Cases

**Challenging Trump's Ocean Drilling Orders**

President Trump revoked Biden-era protections for large swaths of U.S. ocean and moved to open them to oil and gas leasing.

- **Earthjustice et al. v. Trump (February 2025):** Environmental groups filed the first environmental lawsuit against the new Trump administration, challenging his order to undo ocean protections from offshore drilling [source: Earthjustice](https://earthjustice.org/press/2025/groups-file-first-environmental-lawsuit-vs-new-trump-administration-challenging-illegal-order-to-undo-ocean-protections-from-offshore-drilling)
- The legal argument: Once a president permanently withdraws ocean areas from oil and gas leasing under the Outer Continental Shelf Lands Act, that protection cannot be undone by a subsequent president
- **What's at stake:** 2.8 million acres of the Beaufort Sea, 44 million acres off the Alaskan coast, and 125 million acres in the Chukchi and Beaufort Seas protected under Obama [source: Earthjustice](https://earthjustice.org/brief/2025/trump-lawsuit-offshore-drilling)
- Trump's "One Big Beautiful Bill" would force 30 massive oil sales in the Gulf of Mexico over 15 years, plus over 20 sales in Alaskan offshore areas including the High Arctic
- **Status:** Multiple cases in federal courts; more litigation expected as lease sales proceed

## Analysis

### What This Means

- Courts are the most effective short-term check on environmental rollbacks — but litigation takes years, and courts cannot force agencies to pursue environmental protection, only stop them from acting unlawfully
- The *Sackett* decision and the Supreme Court's broader skepticism of agency authority (see *Loper Bright*, which overturned Chevron deference in 2024) have made it easier for industry to challenge environmental rules and harder for agencies to defend them
- Children's climate litigation is establishing new legal theories — constitutional environmental rights at the state level — that could survive federal court hostility
- The preemption battle over state climate laws is the most consequential unresolved legal question of 2026: if courts allow the federal government to preempt state climate accountability, it eliminates the most promising avenue for holding fossil fuel companies liable for decades of deception

### Who's Affected

- **All Americans:** Clean air, clean water, and climate stability depend on the outcome of these cases
- **Communities near fossil fuel infrastructure:** Lose the most if offshore drilling, ANWR, and WOTUS protections are eliminated
- **Residents of states with climate laws:** New York, Vermont, California, and others face federal legal attack on their climate programs
- **Wildlife dependent on ESA:** Gray wolves, grizzly bears, sage-grouse, and hundreds of other species depend on courts enforcing the Endangered Species Act

### Track a Case on CourtListener

Go to [courtlistener.com](https://courtlistener.com), search for a case listed above (try "Earthjustice" or "California v. United States"), and sign up for email alerts on new filings. Knowing when decisions drop lets you share accurate information and respond quickly.

### Submit a Public Comment on Proposed Rules

Many of the regulatory rollbacks underlying these lawsuits went through public comment periods. The next round of rule changes will too. Sign up for EPA and agency mailing lists at [epa.gov](https://www.epa.gov) and [regulations.gov](https://www.regulations.gov) to be notified when comment periods open.

### Share Accurate Case Updates on Social Media

Misinformation about court outcomes spreads fast. When a case decision comes down, share accurate summaries from Earthjustice, Sabin Center, or NRDC — not just headlines. Follow [@earthjustice](https://twitter.com/earthjustice), [@SabinCenter](https://twitter.com/SabinCenter), and [@CenterBioDiv](https://twitter.com/CenterBioDiv) for reliable updates.

### Align Your Purchases and Banking with Your Values

The fossil fuel industry that's fighting environmental protections in court depends on continued consumer demand and financial flows:

- Use [Fossil Free Funds](https://fossilfreefunds.org) to check whether your retirement accounts hold fossil fuel stocks, and ask your HR department or broker for ESG screened alternatives
- Move your checking and savings to a credit union or community bank — the five largest US banks financed over **$1 trillion** in fossil fuels between 2016 and 2022
- Avoid buying products from companies that are currently suing to block environmental rules — track corporate litigation positions through [Center for Climate Integrity](https://climateintegrity.org/lawsuits)

### File a FOIA Request on EPA or Agency Rollbacks

Many of the regulatory rollbacks underlying these lawsuits involve internal agency deliberations that are public records. You can request them:

1. Go to [efoia.epa.gov](https://efoia.epa.gov) (EPA) or [regulations.gov](https://www.regulations.gov) for the agency involved
2. Request internal communications, cost-benefit analyses, or scientific review documents related to any of the rollbacks described above
3. Organizations like the Natural Resources Defense Council and Environmental Defense Fund often publicize what they find — share what you receive

### Volunteer or Intern with an Environmental Legal Organization

Environmental legal organizations need volunteer researchers, paralegals, and community liaisons, especially for cases with local impacts:

- **Earthjustice** lists volunteer and intern opportunities at [earthjustice.org/about/careers](https://earthjustice.org/about/careers)
- **Our Children's Trust** accepts youth ambassadors and volunteers — especially in states where youth climate cases are active
- Contact your local Sierra Club chapter about legal action monitoring and public comment organizing volunteers
- Law students can apply for clinical placements through Earthjustice, NRDC, or state environmental law clinics
