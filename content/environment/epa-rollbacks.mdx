# EPA Gutting & Regulatory Rollbacks

## Quick Summary

**Key Point:** The EPA is being dismantled on every front. The FY2026 budget proposal cuts funding by 54% (from $9.14 billion to $4.16 billion). Over 4,000 employees have been fired or pushed out -- one-third of the workforce -- dropping staffing to levels not seen since the Nixon era. At least 15 major environmental rules have been rolled back or weakened. Civil enforcement cases have dropped to a 20-year low, with 71% of new cases incurring zero penalties. The endangerment finding -- the legal foundation for all federal climate regulation -- was repealed in February 2026, triggering the largest environmental lawsuit in U.S. history.

This is not trimming bureaucracy. It is the systematic elimination of the federal government's ability to protect Americans from pollution.

### Key Points

- **54% budget cut proposed** -- from $9.14B (FY2025) to $4.16B (FY2026), the deepest cut in EPA history [source: EPA FY2026 Budget in Brief](https://www.epa.gov/system/files/documents/2025-05/fy-2026-epa-bib.pdf)
- **4,000+ employees eliminated** -- through layoffs, DOGE-driven reductions in force, and voluntary departures, cutting the workforce from 16,155 to approximately 9,700 [source: Government Executive](https://www.govexec.com/workforce/2025/09/trump-administration-track-cut-1-3-epa-staffers-end-2025/408455/)
- **Endangerment finding repealed** -- the scientific basis for all federal greenhouse gas regulation was rescinded on February 12, 2026, eliminating the legal foundation for climate rules [source: EPA Final Rule](https://www.epa.gov/regulations-emissions-vehicles-and-engines/final-rule-rescission-greenhouse-gas-endangerment)
- **Civil enforcement at 20-year low** -- only 16 DOJ legal actions against polluters in 2025, an 87% drop from Obama's second term and 81% lower than Trump's own first term [source: Environmental Integrity Project](https://environmentalintegrity.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/EIP_Report_2025EnvironmentalEnforcement_2.5.26.pdf)
- **71% of new cases had zero penalties** -- polluters face almost no financial consequences [source: Union of Concerned Scientists](https://blog.ucs.org/science-blogger/despite-what-this-epa-says-enforcement-under-trump-has-dropped/)
- **All vehicle greenhouse gas standards repealed** -- the single largest deregulatory action in U.S. history, removing emission limits for light, medium, and heavy-duty vehicles [source: EPA Press Release](https://www.epa.gov/newsreleases/epa-proposes-repeal-biden-harris-epa-regulations-power-plants-which-if-finalized-would)
- **Science advisory boards purged** -- all members of the Science Advisory Board (SAB) and Clean Air Scientific Advisory Committee (CASAC) dismissed on January 28, 2025 [source: NPR](https://www.npr.org/2025/01/29/nx-s1-5278637/why-members-of-two-epa-science-advisory-committees-were-let-go)
- **24 states plus DC suing EPA** -- joined by cities, counties, and 20+ environmental organizations in the largest environmental lawsuit the administration faces [source: Earthjustice](https://earthjustice.org/press/2026/earthjustice-and-partners-sue-epa-for-illegal-repeal-of-climate-protections)
- **Endangerment Finding rescission took effect April 20** -- permanently eliminating federal authority to regulate greenhouse gases under the Clean Air Act. 24 states plus DC are suing. [source: Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-04-08/lee-zeldin-touts-epa-rollbacks-at-washington-dc-climate-denial-conference)
- **PFAS drinking water protections rolled back** -- on May 18, 2026, EPA announced it will repeal limits for four PFAS compounds and delay enforcement of remaining standards to 2029 [source: Waste Dive](https://www.wastedive.com/news/epa-rescind-pfas-drinking-water-regulations-2026/820595/)
- **Methane rules weakened** -- on April 4, EPA finalized revisions to methane rules for oil and gas industry, saving the industry an estimated $2.5 billion [source: EPA](https://www.epa.gov/controlling-air-pollution-oil-and-natural-gas-operations/2026-final-rule-reduce-burden-oil-and)
- **Superfund Solutions initiative launched** -- on June 3, EPA announced a new initiative to "accelerate" cleanups of 1,340+ Superfund sites by "streamlining decision-making and cutting red tape" [source: EPA](https://www.epa.gov/newsreleases/trump-epa-announces-new-superfund-cleanup-initiative-prioritize-faster-remediation-and)

### [+] Staffing Reductions

**Timeline of workforce destruction:**

| Date | Action | Employees Affected |
|------|--------|-------------------|
| Jan 2025 | Hiring freeze across EPA | All open positions frozen |
| Jan 28, 2025 | Environmental justice and DEI offices eliminated | ~200 employees |
| Feb 2025 | Deferred resignation program ("Fork in the Road") | ~1,000 accepted buyouts |
| Mar 2025 | Environmental justice staff briefly recalled, then eliminated again | ~200 employees |
| May 2025 | EPA announces plan to cut to Reagan-era staffing levels | Target: ~12,000 FTEs |
| Jul 2025 | EPA confirms 23% reduction (3,707 of 16,155 employees gone) | 3,707 employees |
| Sep 2025 | Projected 33% total reduction by end of 2025 | ~5,300 total |
| Oct 2025 | Additional layoffs announced (20-30 more) | Ongoing |
| Mar 2026 | EPA hits 40-year staffing lows | ~9,700 remaining |

**What was lost:**

- **Environmental scientists** who monitored air and water quality
- **Enforcement attorneys** who brought cases against polluters
- **Research staff** from the Office of Research and Development (entire office eliminated)
- **Regional office expertise** -- staff who knew local contamination sites and pollution sources
- **Institutional knowledge** -- decades of expertise in chemical safety, toxicology, and risk assessment that cannot be quickly replaced

The remaining workforce of approximately 9,700 is the lowest since the Ford administration in the mid-1970s -- when the U.S. population was 216 million (versus 340 million today) and the number of regulated facilities was a fraction of what exists now.

### [+] Enforcement Decline

EPA enforcement of environmental laws dropped to historic lows in 2025, the first full year of the current administration.

**Key enforcement statistics (2025 vs. prior years):**

| Metric | 2024 (Biden) | 2025 (Trump) | Change |
|--------|-------------|-------------|--------|
| DOJ legal actions against polluters | ~123 | 16 | **-87%** |
| Federal penalties assessed | $68 million | $54 million | **-21%** |
| Cases with zero penalties | — | 71% of new cases | — |
| Toxic substance inspections | Baseline | Down 36% | **-36%** |
| Drinking water inspections | Baseline | Down 15% | **-15%** |
| Air pollution inspections | Baseline | Down 14% | **-14%** |
| Water pollution inspections | Baseline | Down 5% | **-5%** |
| Civil judicial cases filed | ~48 | Lowest in 20 years | Historic low |

**"Compliance First" policy:** In December 2025, EPA formalized a policy of working with suspected violators to "correct problems" before any formal enforcement action. Critics call this a signal to polluters that violations carry no consequences.

**Lost enforcement capacity:** At least one-third of lawyers in the DOJ's environment division have left. Hundreds of EPA monitoring staff who tracked pollution affecting human health were laid off. The combination of fewer inspectors, fewer attorneys, and a policy of leniency has created what environmental groups describe as "open season for polluters."

**Context:** The 16 legal actions in 2025 compare to 87% fewer than Obama's second term, 76% fewer than Biden's first year, and even 81% fewer than Trump's own first year in 2017.

[source: Environmental Integrity Project Report](https://environmentalintegrity.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/EIP_Report_2025EnvironmentalEnforcement_2.5.26.pdf) | [source: NPR](https://www.npr.org/2026/02/05/nx-s1-5699511/epa-trump-enforcement) | [source: Inside Climate News](https://insideclimatenews.org/news/05022026/trump-epa-polluter-enforcement-collapses/) | [source: Union of Concerned Scientists](https://blog.ucs.org/science-blogger/despite-what-this-epa-says-enforcement-under-trump-has-dropped/)

### [+] Scientific Advisory Changes

On January 28, 2025, EPA dismissed all members of two critical scientific advisory bodies:

**Science Advisory Board (SAB)**
- Provides independent scientific review of EPA research and regulations
- All members removed; EPA announced a "reset" to reverse alleged "politicization"
- New nominations solicited with criteria that critics say favor industry-aligned scientists

**Clean Air Scientific Advisory Committee (CASAC)**
- Mandated by the Clean Air Act to review air quality science
- Reviews the scientific basis for National Ambient Air Quality Standards (PM2.5, ozone, etc.)
- All members dismissed simultaneously

**What this means for regulation:**
- Without independent scientific review, EPA rules lack the expert validation courts have traditionally required
- Industry-aligned replacements are more likely to support weaker pollution standards
- CASAC's reviews of PM2.5 science directly affect whether air quality standards are strengthened or weakened
- Legal challenges to EPA rules often cite SAB/CASAC reviews -- stacking these boards weakens future regulatory defenses

**Additional science attacks:**
- EPA's Office of Research and Development was eliminated entirely
- Air quality research budget cut 65% (from $96M to $33M)
- Greenhouse gas reporting requirements delayed or eliminated

[source: NPR](https://www.npr.org/2025/01/29/nx-s1-5278637/why-members-of-two-epa-science-advisory-committees-were-let-go) | [source: Sabin Center for Climate Change Law](https://climate.law.columbia.edu/content/epa-scientific-advisory-board-members-dismissed) | [source: Earthjustice](https://earthjustice.org/press/2025/earthjustice-condemns-the-new-epas-dismissal-of-critical-science-advisory-boards) | [source: Inside Climate News](https://insideclimatenews.org/news/29012025/trump-epa-dismisses-agency-science-advisers/)

### [+] Key Litigation

Multiple lawsuits are challenging EPA rollbacks. The endangerment finding repeal has generated the largest environmental legal battle in years.

| Case | Plaintiffs | Target | Court | Status |
|------|-----------|--------|-------|--------|
| **Endangerment Finding Challenge** | 24 states + DC + U.S. Virgin Islands + 12 cities/counties + 20 orgs (Earthjustice, NRDC, EDF, CATF, Sierra Club, and others) | Repeal of 2009 GHG endangerment finding | D.C. Circuit Court of Appeals | Filed Feb-Mar 2026; consolidation expected |
| **Youth Climate Case** | 18 young plaintiffs (Our Children's Trust) | Endangerment finding repeal violates constitutional rights | D.C. Circuit Court of Appeals | Filed Feb 2026 |
| **Power Plant Standards** | Expected multi-state coalition | Repeal of GHG standards for power plants | Expected D.C. Circuit | Awaiting final rule |
| **Methane Rule Delays** | EDF, Clean Air Task Force | Suspension of oil & gas methane compliance | Federal court | Ongoing |
| **MATS Repeal** | Expected environmental group challenge | Repeal of 2024 mercury standards strengthening | Expected D.C. Circuit | Awaiting legal filings |

**Endangerment finding litigation -- what to know:**

This is the most consequential case. The endangerment finding, established in 2009 following the Supreme Court's *Massachusetts v. EPA* (2007) ruling, found that greenhouse gas emissions endanger public health and welfare. It is the legal foundation for every federal greenhouse gas regulation.

Senior Earthjustice attorney Hana Vizcarra has said the litigation "will take a few years" and is "going to be a relatively large, complicated piece of litigation, because there's so many players involved." The case is likely to reach the Supreme Court.

If the repeal is upheld, the federal government will have no legal basis to regulate greenhouse gas emissions from any source -- vehicles, power plants, factories, or oil and gas operations.

[source: Earthjustice](https://earthjustice.org/press/2026/earthjustice-and-partners-sue-epa-for-illegal-repeal-of-climate-protections) | [source: NRDC](https://www.nrdc.org/press-releases/nrdc-coalition-sue-endangerment-rollback-climate-protections) | [source: EDF](https://www.edf.org/media/epa-sued-over-illegal-repeal-climate-protections) | [source: Bloomberg Law](https://news.bloomberglaw.com/environment-and-energy/epa-endangerment-finding-rollback-draws-first-legal-challenges) | [source: Clean Air Task Force](https://www.catf.us/2026/02/us-epa-sued-over-illegal-repeal-climate-protections/)

## Analysis

### What This Means

- **The regulatory safety net is gone.** Between the endangerment finding repeal, the 10-for-1 mandate, and the VSL removal, new health-based environmental regulations are effectively impossible at the federal level. Existing regulations face legal vulnerability without their scientific and economic justifications.

- **Enforcement is voluntary.** With a "compliance first" policy, 87% fewer legal actions, and one-third of enforcement attorneys gone, polluters face negligible risk of federal consequences. Companies that invest in pollution controls are now at a competitive disadvantage.

- **State capacity cannot compensate.** The 70% cut to state environmental grants means the states that are supposed to pick up federal slack are losing their own funding. States with weaker environmental agencies will see the biggest impacts.

- **Science has been sidelined.** Dissolved advisory boards, eliminated research offices, and a 65% cut to air quality research mean that future regulations -- whenever they come -- will lack the scientific foundation needed to survive legal challenges.

- **The health costs are real and measurable.** The Clean Air Act alone prevents an estimated 230,000 premature deaths annually. Weakening air quality standards, mercury protections, and vehicle emissions rules will reverse decades of public health gains. These costs fall on taxpayers and families, not on polluters.

### Who's Affected

- **Communities near industrial facilities:** Reduced inspections and enforcement mean more unmonitored pollution. Fenceline communities -- disproportionately low-income and communities of color -- face the greatest risk.
- **Children:** More vulnerable to air pollution, lead exposure, and PFAS contamination. Weaker PM2.5 standards and PFAS rollbacks directly increase childhood health risks.
- **Elderly and people with respiratory/cardiovascular conditions:** Fine particulate matter (PM2.5) causes heart attacks, strokes, and premature death. Weakened air quality standards will increase hospitalizations and mortality.
- **Rural communities:** Dependent on federal grants for water infrastructure, Superfund cleanup, and drinking water monitoring. A 75% cut to the Clean Water SRF leaves these communities without alternatives.
- **Workers at chemical plants:** Rollback of the Chemical Accident Prevention Rule reduces safety requirements at facilities handling dangerous chemicals.
- **Everyone who breathes air or drinks water:** These are not abstract policy changes. They affect the quality of the air in your lungs and the water in your tap.

### Track Open Comment Periods

Many EPA rollbacks go through a public comment process before being finalized. Your comments become part of the legal record and can be cited in court challenges.

1. **Visit** [regulations.gov](https://www.regulations.gov)
2. **Search** for "EPA" and filter by "Open for Comment"
3. **Key dockets to watch:**
   - Power plant GHG standards repeal
   - Chemical Accident Prevention Rule rescission
   - PFAS reporting rule amendments
4. **Tips:** Be specific, cite personal impacts, reference scientific evidence. Form letters carry less weight than individual comments.
