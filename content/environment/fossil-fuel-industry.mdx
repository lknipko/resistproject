# Fossil Fuel Industry: Who Pollutes Most

## Quick Summary

**Key Point:** Just 20 fossil fuel companies have produced 480 billion tonnes of CO2 and methane since 1965 -- 35% of all global emissions. Their own scientists accurately predicted global warming decades ago. Instead of acting on that knowledge, they spent billions on lobbying, campaign contributions, and climate denial to block action. Over 30 lawsuits are now working through U.S. courts to hold them accountable.

The fossil fuel industry receives roughly $35 billion per year in U.S. federal subsidies while spending over $150 million per year lobbying Congress to keep those subsidies flowing.

### [+] They Knew: The Industry's Own Research

The fossil fuel industry's own scientists accurately predicted global warming decades before the public understood the threat. Rather than act on this knowledge, the industry launched a multi-decade campaign to manufacture doubt.

**Exxon's Internal Research (1977-2003):**

- **1977:** Exxon senior scientist James Black told the company's management committee that "the most likely manner in which mankind is influencing the global climate is through carbon dioxide release from the burning of fossil fuels." He warned that doubling CO2 could raise temperatures 2-3 degrees Celsius. [source: Scientific American](https://www.scientificamerican.com/article/exxon-knew-about-climate-change-almost-40-years-ago/)

- **1977-2003:** Exxon scientists produced climate models projecting 0.20°C of warming per decade (±0.04°C). A 2023 Harvard peer-reviewed study found 63-83% of these projections matched actual observed temperatures. [source: Science (journal)](https://www.science.org/doi/10.1126/science.abk0063)

- **1982:** Exxon's internal projections correctly predicted the current level of atmospheric CO2 and global temperature rise. Their scientists accurately predicted that human-caused global warming would first be detectable around the year 2000. [source: Harvard Gazette](https://news.harvard.edu/gazette/story/2023/01/harvard-led-analysis-finds-exxonmobil-internal-research-accurately-predicted-climate-change/)

- **1978-present:** Despite this research, Exxon publicly cast doubt on climate science for decades, emphasizing "uncertainty" to delay regulatory action. [source: InsideClimate News](https://insideclimatenews.org/news/22102015/exxon-sowed-doubt-about-climate-science-for-decades-by-stressing-uncertainty/)

**Industry-Wide Knowledge:**

- **1968:** API's own research (the Robinson Report) warned that rising CO2 levels could cause melting ice caps and rising sea levels. [source: InsideClimate News](https://insideclimatenews.org/news/22122015/exxon-mobil-oil-industry-peers-knew-about-climate-change-dangers-1970s-american-petroleum-institute-api-shell-chevron-texaco/)

- **1989:** The industry created the Global Climate Coalition (GCC), a lobbying group specifically designed to challenge the scientific basis for climate action. Exxon was a founding member. [source: Wikipedia - GCC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Global_Climate_Coalition)

- **1998:** A leaked API memo declared: "Victory will be achieved when average citizens 'understand' (recognize) uncertainties in climate science." The plan budgeted millions for a disinformation campaign. [source: Climate Files - API Memo](https://www.climatefiles.com/trade-group/american-petroleum-institute/1998-global-climate-science-communications-team-action-plan/)

- **2001:** The GCC's campaign contributed to the U.S. withdrawal from the Kyoto Protocol under George W. Bush. [source: PBS Frontline](https://www.pbs.org/wgbh/pages/frontline/hotpolitics/reports/manipulation.html)

The fossil fuel industry maintains its position through massive political spending, trade group lobbying, and a revolving door between industry and government.

|----------|--------|--------|

[source: Yale Climate Connections](https://yaleclimateconnections.org/2025/01/the-fossil-fuel-industry-spent-219-million-to-elect-the-new-u-s-government/) | [source: OpenSecrets - Oil & Gas](https://www.opensecrets.org/industries/totals?ind=E01++)

**Lobbying:**

- **2024:** The oil and gas industry spent $151.1 million on federal lobbying -- the second-largest annual expenditure after 2009 (the year of the cap-and-trade bill). [source: OpenSecrets](https://www.opensecrets.org/news/2025/02/federal-lobbying-set-new-record-in-2024/)
- **First half of 2025:** Nearly $240 million poured in, with ~2,200 lobbyists representing the sector -- nearly half of whom are former government employees. [source: InsideClimate News](https://insideclimatenews.org/news/08092025/energy-sector-lobbying-spending/)
- **1998-2025 cumulative:** $3.1 billion in lobbying by oil and gas, vs. $487 million by environmental groups -- a 6:1 spending ratio. [source: OpenSecrets](https://www.opensecrets.org/federal-lobbying/industries/summary?id=E01)

**Trade Groups:**

- **American Petroleum Institute (API):** Spent over $750 million on PR, advertising, and communications between 2008-2019. Created astroturf front groups to simulate grassroots support for the industry. [source: Climate Investigations Center](https://climateinvestigations.org/trade-association-pr-spending/american-petroleum-institute/)
- **U.S. Chamber of Commerce:** Spends millions lobbying against climate legislation while keeping its fossil fuel donor list private. [source: Gizmodo](https://gizmodo.com/the-chamber-of-commerces-sordid-history-of-climate-deni-1847194757)
- A 2024 Senate/House joint investigation found the industry shifted from outright climate denial to "deception, disinformation, and doublespeak" using dark money and phony front groups. [source: Senate Budget Committee Report (PDF)](https://www.budget.senate.gov/imo/media/doc/fossil_fuel_report1.pdf)

**Federal Subsidies:**

Despite record profits, the fossil fuel industry receives approximately $35 billion per year in federal subsidies, including tax breaks, below-market drilling leases on public lands, and regulatory loopholes. In 2025, the "One Big Beautiful Bill" added over $4 billion in new fossil fuel subsidies. Fossil fuel subsidies have more than doubled since 2017.

[source: Oil Change International](https://oilchange.org/news/us-fossil-fuel-subsidies/) | [source: Yale E360](https://e360.yale.edu/digest/republican-spending-bill-fossil-fuel-subsidies)

**Greenwashing:**

Oil majors have made and broken a series of climate pledges:

- **BP** pledged in 2020 to cut oil and gas production by 40% -- reduced to 30% in 2023, then 23% in 2024, then effectively abandoned. [source: Euronews](https://www.euronews.com/2026/03/21/you-cant-live-without-us-have-fossil-fuel-giants-gone-back-on-their-green-promises)
- **Shell** promised to cut emissions 30% by 2030 -- revised downward and deprioritized, while expanding LNG production. [source: ClientEarth](https://www.clientearth.org/projects/the-greenwashing-files/shell/)
- A peer-reviewed PLOS One study found a "mismatch between discourse, actions and investments" across BP, Chevron, ExxonMobil, and Shell -- clean energy claims are contradicted by actual spending. [source: PLOS One](https://journals.plos.org/plosone/article?id=10.1371/journal.pone.0263596)
- By 2024, messaging shifted to framing fossil fuels as "permanent, indispensable and essential" rather than making transition pledges. [source: Grist](https://grist.org/language/oil-companies-greenwashing-exxon-bp-shell-research/)

### [+] Lawsuits & Accountability

Over 30 lawsuits from states and cities are pursuing fossil fuel companies for climate-related damages and deception, modeled on the successful litigation against the tobacco industry.

**Key Cases:**

- **Honolulu v. Sunoco et al.:** Filed 2020. In January 2025, the U.S. Supreme Court denied oil companies' petition to block the case, allowing it to proceed in state court. Trial in 2026 is possible -- potentially the first climate deception case to reach trial. [source: Climate Case Chart](https://www.climatecasechart.com/collections/city-county-of-honolulu-v-sunoco-lp_25ac49)

- **California cities and AG:** In February 2025, the California Supreme Court denied fossil fuel companies' attempts to dismiss lawsuits brought by the state Attorney General and California localities. [source: CalMatters](https://calmatters.org/environment/climate-change/2025/03/climate-change-california-oil-industry-legal-strategy/)

- **Nine states + dozens of municipalities:** San Francisco, New York, Baltimore, Honolulu, Maui, and others have filed suits alleging the industry knew about climate risks and misled the public. [source: Stateline](https://stateline.org/2024/04/04/after-a-long-slog-climate-change-lawsuits-will-finally-put-big-oil-on-trial/)

- **Hawaii state lawsuit:** After Hawaii announced plans to sue fossil fuel companies, the Trump administration preemptively sued the state in April 2025 to block the action. [source: Holland & Knight](https://www.hklaw.com/en/insights/publications/2025/05/an-update-on-climate-superfund-laws-and-climate-change-lawsuits)

**Legal Theory:**

These lawsuits allege that fossil fuel companies -- like tobacco companies before them -- knew their products caused harm, concealed that knowledge, and ran coordinated disinformation campaigns. The plaintiffs seek damages for climate-related costs: sea level rise, wildfires, extreme heat, flooding, and public health impacts.

[source: Climate in the Courts](https://www.climateinthecourts.com/big-oil-may-finally-have-to-face-trial-in-climate-deception-lawsuits-why-it-matters-and-what-comes-next/)

## Analysis

### What This Means

- **The science was never in doubt internally.** The industry's own researchers confirmed the link between fossil fuels and global warming in the 1970s. The public "debate" about climate science was manufactured.

- **The subsidies are indefensible.** Taxpayers provide ~$35 billion/year to an industry that earned record profits ($200+ billion combined for the five oil supermajors in 2022) and spends billions on lobbying and political contributions to maintain those handouts.

- **The lawsuits are the accountability mechanism.** With federal regulation weakened and Congress captured by industry money, the courts are the last remaining pathway for holding polluters financially responsible.

- **Greenwashing has replaced denial.** The industry no longer disputes climate science. Instead, it makes transition pledges it quietly walks back, while framing fossil fuels as permanent necessities. The strategy has shifted from "it's not real" to "you can't live without us."

- **State-owned companies are the biggest emitters** but the hardest to hold accountable. Investor-owned companies (ExxonMobil, Chevron, Shell, BP) are the primary targets of litigation and divestment because they operate within legal frameworks that allow lawsuits and shareholder action.

### Who Benefits vs. Who Pays

- **Who benefits:** Fossil fuel executives and shareholders, politicians receiving industry contributions, lobbyists, and PR firms. The top five oil supermajors alone earned over $200 billion in profit in 2022.

- **Who pays:** Taxpayers (through subsidies and disaster relief), communities near extraction and refining sites (disproportionately low-income and communities of color), coastal populations facing sea level rise, agricultural communities experiencing drought, and everyone breathing polluted air. The IMF estimates that global fossil fuel subsidies -- including unpaid environmental costs -- total $7 trillion per year.

### Check Your Bank & Retirement Investments (5 minutes)

Your bank and retirement fund may be financing fossil fuel expansion. Here's how to check:

- **Check your mutual funds:** Use the [Fossil Free Funds](https://fossilfreefunds.org/) tool by As You Sow to search any mutual fund or ETF for fossil fuel exposure
- **Check your bank:** JPMorgan Chase, Citi, Wells Fargo, and Bank of America are the top four banks financing fossil fuels globally ([Banking on Climate Chaos report](https://www.bankingonclimatechaos.org/))
- **Switch banks:** Consider moving accounts to a credit union or community bank that does not finance fossil fuel extraction
- **Ask your employer:** Request fossil-free options in your 401(k) plan -- many major providers now offer them

### Divest Your Personal Investments

If you manage your own investments:

- **Screen your portfolio** at [Fossil Free Funds](https://fossilfreefunds.org/) or [As You Sow](https://www.asyousow.org/)
- **Switch to fossil-free index funds** -- many track the S&P 500 minus fossil fuels with comparable returns
- **Move your IRA/Roth** to a platform offering fossil-free portfolios (Betterment, Wealthsimple, OpenInvest)

### File Shareholder Resolutions

If you own stock in fossil fuel companies (even through mutual funds), you have shareholder rights:

- Vote for climate disclosure resolutions at annual meetings
- Support Say on Climate proposals
- Contact your fund manager to vote for climate-aligned resolutions on your behalf
- [As You Sow](https://www.asyousow.org) helps coordinate shareholder advocacy

### Attend Public Comment Hearings

- **BLM lease sales** — Comment on oil and gas lease sales on public lands at [eplanning.blm.gov](https://eplanning.blm.gov)
- **State oil & gas commission hearings** — Attend when new drilling permits are proposed near communities
- **Utility rate cases** — When utilities seek rate increases for fossil fuel infrastructure, attend public utility commission hearings

### Support Climate Litigation

Over 30 lawsuits are holding fossil fuel companies accountable. You can help:

- Follow cases at [Climate Case Chart](http://climatecasechart.com/)
- Support **Earthjustice** and **Center for Climate Integrity** who litigate these cases
- Write letters to your state AG encouraging them to join multi-state climate lawsuits
