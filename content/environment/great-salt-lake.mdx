# Great Salt Lake Crisis

## Quick Summary

**Key Point:** The Great Salt Lake -- the largest terminal lake in North America -- has lost roughly 48% of its water volume since 1847 and currently sits at approximately 4,192 feet above sea level, nearly 6 feet below the minimum healthy level of 4,198 feet. Over 800 square miles of lakebed are now exposed, releasing dust containing arsenic and heavy metals toward the 2.5 million residents of the Wasatch Front. The lake supports 10-12 million migratory birds of 339 species annually and produces 40-45% of the world's brine shrimp.

Utah has invested over $300 million in conservation since 2022, but the lake remains critically low. Agricultural water diversions -- not climate change -- are the primary driver, consuming roughly 75% of the watershed's water, mainly for irrigating hay and alfalfa.

### Key Points

- The Great Salt Lake hit its lowest recorded elevation of 4,188.5 feet on December 17, 2022 -- down over 22 feet from its 1986 high of 4,211.6 feet [source: USGS](https://www.usgs.gov/media/before-after/great-salt-lake-comparison-1986-and-2022)
- As of April 2026, the lake sits at approximately 4,192.3 feet -- still nearly 6 feet below the minimum healthy level of 4,198 feet [source: Utah DNR Current Conditions](https://greatsaltlake.utah.gov/current-conditions)
- Over 54% of the lakebed is now exposed, roughly 800 square miles -- an area the size of Maui [source: BYU Great Salt Lake Study](https://pws.byu.edu/great-salt-lake)
- Three-quarters of consumed water in the watershed goes to agriculture, mainly irrigating hay and alfalfa for livestock feed [source: BYU Research](https://ballardbrief.byu.edu/issue-briefs/the-aridification-of-the-great-salt-lake)
- Only about 9% of the lake's decline is attributed to climate change; the rest is from human water diversions [source: BYU Great Salt Lake Study](https://pws.byu.edu/great-salt-lake)
- The lake contributes an estimated $1.9 billion annually to Utah's economy through mineral extraction, brine shrimp, and recreation, supporting over 7,700 jobs [source: USGS Economics](https://webapps.usgs.gov/gsl/characteristics/economics.html)
- The Great Salt Lake is designated a Western Hemisphere Shorebird Reserve Network Site of Hemispheric Importance -- one of only 17 such sites in the Western Hemisphere [source: FRIENDS of Great Salt Lake](https://www.fogsl.org/newsroom/item/905-great-salt-lake-celebrates-30-years-as-a-whsrn-site)

### [+] Health & Dust Risks

The exposed lakebed contains elevated concentrations of heavy metals deposited over decades of mineral extraction and natural accumulation. When winds blow across the dry playa, fine particulate dust carries these contaminants toward populated areas along the Wasatch Front.

**Contaminants found in lakebed dust:**

- **Arsenic** -- levels exceed EPA soil residential screening levels; linked to cancer, cardiovascular disease, and neurological damage [source: USGS](https://www.usgs.gov/programs/environmental-health-program/science/dust-great-salt-lake-dry-lakebed-a-possible-health)
- **Lithium** -- also exceeds EPA residential screening levels [source: USGS](https://www.usgs.gov/programs/environmental-health-program/science/dust-great-salt-lake-dry-lakebed-a-possible-health)
- **Lead** -- developmental toxin, especially dangerous for children [source: University of Utah](https://science.utah.edu/news/toxic-dust-hot-spots/)
- **Manganese, iron, copper** -- transition metals that are highly irritating to lung tissue [source: University of Utah](https://science.utah.edu/news/toxic-dust-hot-spots/)

**Health research status:**

- No formal evaluation of health impacts from Great Salt Lake dust has been completed due to data gaps [source: Utah DEQ](https://deq.utah.gov/air-quality/great-salt-lake-dust)
- Dose-level analysis has not been performed due to cost ($27,500 per monitoring site annually) [source: University of Utah](https://attheu.utah.edu/research/just-how-dangerous-is-great-salt-lake-dust-new-research-looks-for-clues/)
- Children are particularly vulnerable to dust-borne contaminants through ingestion and inhalation [source: ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2590332224002495)
- Research published in 2024 found that preserving lake water levels decreases ambient dust and reduces racial disparities in population exposure [source: ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2590332224002495)
- Utah scientists and community forums have raised growing concerns about dust exposure, particularly during high-wind events [source: KPCW](https://www.kpcw.org/state-regional/2025-07-29/utah-scientists-discuss-dangers-of-great-salt-lake-toxic-dust)

**Population at risk:** Approximately 2.5 million people live along the Wasatch Front, downwind of the exposed lakebed. Communities of color and low-income neighborhoods are disproportionately exposed.

### [+] Ecological Impact

**Migratory Birds:**

The Great Salt Lake is one of the most important migratory bird habitats in the Western Hemisphere. It provides irreplaceable stopover, breeding, and nesting habitat along the Pacific Flyway.

- **10-12 million birds** visit the lake annually, representing **339 species** [source: Audubon](https://www.audubon.org/news/our-work-great-salt-lake)
- **1.4 million shorebirds** use the lake during migration [source: FOGSL](https://www.fogsl.org/newsroom/item/905-great-salt-lake-celebrates-30-years-as-a-whsrn-site)
- **90% of North America's Eared Grebes** depend on the lake, each consuming up to 30,000 brine shrimp per day [source: Utah DWR](https://wildlife.utah.gov/gslep/wildlife/brine-shrimp.html)
- **500,000 Wilson's Phalaropes** stage at the lake, doubling their body weight on brine shrimp before migrating to South America [source: Audubon](https://www.audubon.org/news/governor-cox-declares-2021-year-shorebird-great-salt-lake)
- **250,000 American Avocets** -- over 30% of their global population [source: Audubon](https://www.audubon.org/news/our-work-great-salt-lake)

**Brine Shrimp & Ecosystem:**

Brine shrimp (Artemia) are the keystone species of the Great Salt Lake ecosystem. As the lake shrinks and salinity rises, both brine shrimp and brine fly populations approach critical thresholds.

- Great Salt Lake produces **40-45% of the world's brine shrimp eggs**, essential for global aquaculture [source: Utah DWR](https://wildlife.utah.gov/gslep/wildlife/brine-shrimp.html)
- The brine shrimp harvest is valued at **$10-60 million annually**, with 21 companies actively harvesting as of 2026 [source: Utah DWR Industries](https://wildlife.utah.gov/gslep/industries)
- Rising salinity is pushing brine shrimp and their microbial food sources toward -- and past -- survivability thresholds [source: Utah DWR Drought FAQ](https://wildlife.utah.gov/gslep/about/drought.html)
- Great Salt Lake produces **all primary magnesium in the U.S.** and **14% of the world's supply** [source: USGS Economics](https://webapps.usgs.gov/gsl/characteristics/economics.html)
- The lake leads the Western Hemisphere in production of sulfate of potash, a specialty crop fertilizer [source: Utah DWR Industries](https://wildlife.utah.gov/gslep/industries)

## Analysis

### What This Means

- **Public health emergency in slow motion:** Without formal health studies, 2.5 million residents are breathing dust of unknown toxicity during every wind event. The lack of data is itself a policy failure -- monitoring costs are a tiny fraction of the health costs if toxic exposure is confirmed.
- **Ecological collapse is not hypothetical:** Brine shrimp are already nearing salinity thresholds. If the keystone species collapses, the entire food web unravels -- affecting migratory bird populations across two continents.
- **Agricultural water reform is unavoidable:** With 75% of watershed water going to irrigate hay and alfalfa, no amount of residential conservation alone can solve the crisis. Willing-seller water leasing programs are promising but still small-scale.
- **Legislative progress is real but insufficient:** Utah has invested over $300 million and created a Great Salt Lake Commissioner. But the lake remains 6 feet below minimum healthy levels, and current programs acquire water measured in thousands of acre-feet while the lake loses nearly 1 million acre-feet per year.
- **Federal funding is critical:** The $50 million Bureau of Reclamation grant and $60 million wetlands settlement are significant, but ongoing federal support is uncertain under the current administration's environmental budget cuts.

### Who's Affected

- **2.5 million Wasatch Front residents** -- exposed to toxic dust during wind events, with children, elderly, and those with respiratory conditions at highest risk
- **Communities of color and low-income neighborhoods** -- disproportionately located in areas with highest dust exposure [source: ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2590332224002495)
- **Utah's agricultural sector** -- faces inevitable water use reductions through voluntary programs or regulation
- **Brine shrimp industry** -- 21 companies and thousands of jobs at risk as salinity levels threaten shrimp viability
- **Global aquaculture** -- depends on Great Salt Lake for 40-45% of the world's brine shrimp eggs
- **Migratory bird populations** -- 339 species and 10-12 million individual birds rely on the lake as irreplaceable Pacific Flyway habitat
- **Tourism and recreation** -- the lake's $1.9 billion annual economic contribution is directly threatened
- **2034 Winter Olympics** -- Governor Cox's charter ties lake restoration to Utah's international reputation

### Contact Utah State Legislators

Federal representatives handle national funding, but the most impactful Great Salt Lake legislation happens at the state level. Contact your Utah state legislators directly:

- **Find your state legislators:** [le.utah.gov](https://le.utah.gov/) -- Enter your address to find your state senator and representative
- **Key committees:** Natural Resources, Appropriations, and Agriculture Interim Committee
- **Ask them to:** Expand agricultural water leasing programs, fund dust health studies, and increase the Great Salt Lake Preservation Program budget beyond $2.75 million
- **Great Salt Lake Commissioner's Office:** [greatsaltlake.utah.gov](https://greatsaltlake.utah.gov/) -- Submit comments and track current initiatives

### Monitor the Lake Yourself
- **FRIENDS of Great Salt Lake** hosts monthly bird surveys and lakebed monitoring — volunteer at [fogsl.org](https://www.fogsl.org)
- **PurpleAir** ([purpleair.com](https://www.purpleair.com)) — Install an air quality sensor to track dust levels, especially during wind events from exposed lakebed
- **USGS real-time lake level data** ([waterdata.usgs.gov](https://waterdata.usgs.gov/monitoring-location/10010000/)) — Track elevation changes yourself

### Reduce Your Water Footprint
The lake crisis is a water use crisis. Personal conservation helps:
- **Flip Your Strip** — Replace park strips with water-wise landscaping. Many Utah cities offer rebates ($1-3/sq ft)
- **Fix leaks** — A running toilet wastes 200 gallons/day
- **Water lawns only 2x/week** — Follow Utah's outdoor watering guide at [slowtheflow.org](https://conservewater.utah.gov)
- **Choose low-water food** — Beef and dairy require the most irrigation water; alfalfa (the lake's biggest threat) is grown primarily for livestock feed

### Engage with State Government
- **Attend Utah Water Strategy Advisory Team meetings** — Public meetings happen quarterly ([waterstrategyadvisoryteam.utah.gov](https://waterstrategyadvisoryteam.utah.gov))
- **Comment on state water legislation** — Track bills at [le.utah.gov](https://le.utah.gov)
- **Contact your state legislators directly** — Find them at [house.utleg.gov](https://house.utleg.gov) and [senate.utah.gov](https://senate.utah.gov)
- **Support water metering ballot initiatives** — Secondary water metering is critical for accountability

## Sustained Actions

### Organizations to Support

- **FRIENDS of Great Salt Lake** ([fogsl.org](https://fogsl.org)) -- Founded in 1994, works on education, research, advocacy, and the arts to increase awareness and protection of the lake
- **Utah Rivers Council** ([utahrivers.org](https://utahrivers.org)) -- Runs the 4,200 Project to raise the lake back to a sustainable 4,200-foot level, and launched the Great Salt Lake Waterkeeper program in 2025
- **Great Salt Lake Collaborative** ([greatsaltlakenews.org](https://greatsaltlakenews.org)) -- Coalition of organizations providing public education and engagement about the lake
- **Audubon** ([audubon.org/great-salt-lake](https://www.audubon.org/news/our-work-great-salt-lake)) -- Monitors bird populations and advocates for habitat protection at the lake
- **The Nature Conservancy - Utah** ([nature.org](https://www.nature.org/en-us/get-involved/how-to-help/places-we-protect/the-great-salt-lake-shorelands-preserve/)) -- Manages the Great Salt Lake Shorelands Preserve and works on water conservation

### Ongoing Commitments

- **Conserve water at home:** Utah has among the highest per-capita water use in the nation. Fix leaks, reduce outdoor irrigation, replace lawns with water-wise landscaping. Every gallon saved in the watershed can reach the lake.
- **Attend Great Salt Lake Advisory Council meetings:** Open to the public, held every other month. [Meeting schedule and information](https://deq.utah.gov/great-salt-lake-advisory-council/meetings-great-salt-lake-advisory-council)
- **Monitor lake levels:** Track the lake's current elevation at [USGS Water Data](https://waterdata.usgs.gov/monitoring-location/USGS-10010000/) and the [Grow the Flow Lake Tracker](https://growtheflowutah.org/laketracker/)
- **Support agricultural water leasing:** Advocate for expanding programs that compensate farmers for voluntarily reducing water diversions rather than mandating cuts
- **Stay informed on legislation:** Follow the Utah Legislature's water bills each session at [le.utah.gov](https://le.utah.gov/) and through FRIENDS of Great Salt Lake's [legislative tracker](https://fogsl.org/advocacy-issues/legislative-session)
