# Methane Emissions

## Quick Summary

**Key Point:** Methane is a super-pollutant -- 80 times more potent than CO2 over 20 years -- and the fastest lever for slowing near-term warming. The oil and gas sector is the largest industrial source, with satellite data revealing emissions far higher than industry self-reports. In 2025, Congress repealed the methane waste emissions charge, EPA suspended enforcement of Biden-era methane rules, and compliance deadlines were pushed back 18 months. Meanwhile, MethaneSAT data documented massive, previously invisible leaks from oil and gas operations worldwide before the satellite was lost in June 2025.

Cutting methane is the single most effective short-term climate action available.

![Atmospheric methane concentration rising from 1,645 to 1,950 ppb since 1984](/environment/charts/methane-trend.png)

## Facts

### Why Methane Matters

Methane (CH4) is the second-largest contributor to human-caused climate change after CO2. While it stays in the atmosphere for only about 12 years (versus centuries for CO2), its heat-trapping potency is extreme:

- **80x more powerful** than CO2 over a 20-year period
- **30x more powerful** than CO2 over a 100-year period
- Responsible for roughly **30% of global warming** since pre-industrial times
- Atmospheric methane concentrations have more than doubled since 1750

Because methane is short-lived, reducing it produces rapid climate benefits -- cutting methane by 45% by 2030 could avoid 0.3 degrees Celsius of warming, buying critical time for longer-term CO2 reductions.

[source: UNEP Global Methane Assessment](https://www.unep.org/resources/report/global-methane-assessment-benefits-and-costs-mitigating-methane-emissions) | [source: IPCC AR6 Working Group I](https://www.ipcc.ch/report/ar6/wg1/)

### U.S. Methane Sources

| Source | Share of U.S. Methane Emissions |
|--------|-------------------------------|
| Oil and gas systems | ~30% |
| Enteric fermentation (livestock) | ~27% |
| Landfills | ~17% |
| Coal mining | ~6% |
| Wastewater treatment | ~3% |
| Other (rice, wetlands, etc.) | ~17% |

The oil and gas sector leaks methane through venting (intentional release), flaring (burning), and fugitive emissions (unintentional leaks from wells, pipelines, compressor stations, and processing plants). Satellite data consistently shows actual emissions are 50-100% higher than industry self-reports.

[source: EPA Greenhouse Gas Inventory](https://www.epa.gov/ghgemissions/inventory-us-greenhouse-gas-emissions-and-sinks) | [source: IEA Global Methane Tracker 2025](https://www.iea.org/reports/global-methane-tracker-2025/key-findings)

### Regulatory Rollbacks

**Methane Waste Emissions Charge Repealed:**
In March 2025, Congress used the Congressional Review Act to disapprove EPA's regulation implementing the Inflation Reduction Act's methane fee. The FY2025 reconciliation bill further postponed the charge for ten years. The fee was projected to raise $7.2 billion from 2025-2034 and incentivize companies to fix leaks.

**EPA Enforcement Suspended:**
An EPA memo stated enforcement actions "shall not shut down any stage of energy production or power generation" and that "enforcement and compliance will no longer focus on methane emissions from oil and gas facilities."

**Compliance Deadlines Extended:**
EPA finalized an 18-month delay for implementation of the 2024 methane standards. No annual emissions report under Subpart OOOOb will be due before November 30, 2026.

**Greenhouse Gas Reporting Under Review:**
EPA is reconsidering Biden-era regulations under Section 111 of the Clean Air Act and Subpart W of the Greenhouse Gas Reporting Program, potentially eliminating the primary federal mechanism for tracking methane emissions.

[source: Harvard EELP Tracker](https://eelp.law.harvard.edu/tracker/epa-voc-and-methane-standards-for-oil-and-gas-facilities-2/) | [source: EPA Methane Rule Delay](https://www.epa.gov/controlling-air-pollution-oil-and-natural-gas-operations/2025-interim-final-rule-extend-compliance) | [source: Vinson & Elkins Analysis](https://www.velaw.com/insights/congress-has-disapproved-the-epas-methane-tax-rule-what-happens-next/)

### [+] MethaneSAT: What Satellites Revealed

MethaneSAT, launched March 4, 2024 by the Environmental Defense Fund and New Zealand Space Agency, was the first satellite purpose-built to measure methane from oil and gas operations at basin scale. Before losing contact in June 2025, MethaneSAT:

- Surveyed 41 oil and gas basins across 25 countries
- Covered 50% of global onshore oil and gas production
- Revealed "massive, previously invisible" methane emissions
- Produced 10 peer-reviewed papers with many more in review
- Demonstrated that industry self-reported emissions significantly undercount actual leaks

The satellite's loss underscores the need for continued investment in methane monitoring. Other satellites (GHGSat, TROPOMI, EMIT) continue to provide data, and the EDF is exploring a replacement mission.

[source: MethaneSAT Project Updates](https://www.methanesat.org/project-updates/2025-was-year-highs-lows-and-hope-methanesat) | [source: Harvard Gazette - MethaneSAT](https://news.harvard.edu/gazette/story/2025/09/methane-tracking-satellite-lost-in-space-what-now/)

### [+] Global Methane Pledge

At COP26 in 2021, over 150 countries (including the U.S.) signed the Global Methane Pledge to reduce methane emissions 30% below 2020 levels by 2030. The IEA's Global Methane Tracker 2025 found that global methane emissions remain near record highs, with limited progress toward the pledge target.

The U.S. withdrawal from the Paris Agreement and rollback of domestic methane regulations undermines the credibility of the pledge and weakens international pressure on other major emitters.

[source: IEA Global Methane Tracker 2025](https://www.iea.org/reports/global-methane-tracker-2025/key-findings) | [source: Global Methane Pledge](https://www.globalmethanepledge.org/)

## Analysis

### What This Means

- Without the methane waste emissions charge, companies face no financial incentive to fix leaks that waste public resources
- Delayed compliance means 18+ months of additional uncontrolled emissions from oil and gas operations
- Eliminating the Greenhouse Gas Reporting Program would make it impossible to track whether emissions are increasing or decreasing
- Methane from the oil and gas sector alone contributes to thousands of premature deaths annually through ozone formation
- The economic value of wasted natural gas (leaked methane) is estimated at $2+ billion annually -- resources that belong to taxpayers on public lands

### Who's Affected

- **Frontline communities:** People living near oil and gas operations, compressor stations, and processing plants face elevated health risks from methane co-pollutants (benzene, formaldehyde, VOCs)
- **Everyone:** Methane drives near-term warming that intensifies extreme weather, wildfires, drought, and flooding
- **Taxpayers:** Methane vented from public lands represents lost royalty revenue -- billions of dollars over the next decade
- **Agricultural communities:** Warming-driven drought and extreme weather threaten crop yields and livestock
- **Global South:** Methane-driven warming accelerates climate impacts in the most vulnerable nations

### Report a Methane Leak (10 minutes)

If you smell natural gas (rotten egg odor) or see unusual venting or flaring:

1. **Immediate safety:** If you smell gas indoors, leave immediately and call 911
2. **Gas utility hotline:** Call your local gas company's emergency line (on your gas bill) -- they are required to investigate
3. **State oil and gas regulator:** Report suspected well leaks or venting to your state's oil and gas commission
4. **EPA reporting:** [echo.epa.gov/report-environmental-violations](https://echo.epa.gov/report-environmental-violations)
5. **Document:** Photos, video, GPS coordinates, date/time, and weather conditions

### Reduce Food Waste and Compost (ongoing)

Landfills are the third-largest U.S. methane source. Food waste decomposing in landfills generates methane for decades. You can reduce this:

1. **Plan meals** to reduce food waste -- the average American wastes ~30% of food purchased
2. **Compost:** Start a backyard compost bin or use a municipal composting program. Find your nearest program at [findacomposter.com](https://www.findacomposter.com)
3. **Support composting infrastructure:** Advocate for curbside composting programs with your city council
4. **Reduce meat consumption:** Livestock produce ~27% of U.S. methane. Even reducing meat by 1-2 meals per week makes a measurable difference

### Check Your Home for Gas Leaks (15 minutes)

Gas stoves, furnaces, and water heaters can leak methane inside your home:

1. **Soap bubble test:** Apply soapy water to gas connections -- bubbles indicate a leak
2. **Natural gas detector:** Electronic detectors (~$30-50) can identify leaks not detectable by smell
3. **Get a professional inspection:** Gas companies often provide free leak checks
4. **Consider induction:** When replacing a gas stove, induction cooktops eliminate indoor methane and combustion pollutants (NO2, CO, benzene)

## Sustained Actions

### Support Methane Monitoring

- **Fund independent monitoring:** Organizations like the [Environmental Defense Fund](https://www.edf.org), [Clean Air Task Force](https://www.catf.us), and [Earthworks](https://www.earthworks.org) conduct methane monitoring campaigns
- **Community monitoring:** Earthworks trains community members to use FLIR (forward-looking infrared) cameras to document invisible methane plumes -- [earthworks.org/issues/oil-and-gas-community-monitoring](https://www.earthworks.org/issues/oil-and-gas-community-monitoring/)
- **Support satellite monitoring:** Advocate for government investment in methane satellite programs following the loss of MethaneSAT

### Push for State and Local Action

Many states can adopt stronger methane standards than the weakened federal rules:

- **Colorado:** Has its own methane regulations for oil and gas -- a model for other states
- **New Mexico:** Adopted strong methane waste rules in 2021
- **California:** Mandatory methane monitoring and repair programs
- Contact your state legislators to support state-level methane regulations
- Push your city/county to adopt methane leak detection requirements for gas infrastructure
