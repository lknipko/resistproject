# Offshore Oil Drilling: Risks, Spills, and the Push to Expand

## Quick Summary

**Key Point:** The Trump administration is proposing to open **1.27 billion acres** of U.S. ocean to new oil and gas leasing — including the Atlantic and Pacific coasts that have been protected for decades. The outer continental shelf currently produces roughly **15% of U.S. domestic oil**. The Deepwater Horizon spill released **4.9 million barrels** of oil, killed **11 workers**, and cost BP more than **$65 billion** in damages and cleanup. Environmental groups and coastal communities have filed the first environmental lawsuits of the new administration to block the expansion.

Biden withdrew more than 625 million acres from new leasing in January 2025. Trump signed an executive order on day one to undo those protections. In March 2026, a **record-breaking $164 million** auction sold drilling rights to 1.3 million acres in the National Petroleum Reserve-Alaska. On **June 5, 2026**, a lease sale was held in the **Arctic National Wildlife Refuge**. The Trump administration also granted a **5-year lease extension** for the controversial Liberty offshore drilling project in the Arctic Ocean.

## Facts

### Current US Offshore Oil and Gas Production

The Bureau of Ocean Energy Management (BOEM) currently manages **2,073 active offshore leases** covering about 11.2 million acres. Offshore oil production reached record levels in 2025, totaling over **714 million barrels** for the year — the highest annual output on record. Offshore production accounts for roughly **15 percent** of U.S. domestic oil output.

The outer continental shelf is estimated to contain about **68.8 billion barrels of oil** and **229 trillion cubic feet of natural gas** yet to be discovered. [source: DOI/BOEM 11th Leasing Program Announcement](https://www.doi.gov/pressreleases/interior-launches-expansive-11th-national-offshore-leasing-program-advance-us-energy)

### The Trump Administration's Expansion Plan

On January 20, 2025, President Trump signed Executive Order 14148 directing the Department of Interior to reverse Biden's offshore drilling protections. DOI Secretary Doug Burgum subsequently issued a Secretary's Order titled "Unleashing American Offshore Energy," directing BOEM to terminate the Biden 2024–2029 National Outer Continental Shelf Oil and Gas Leasing Program and replace it with a new, expansive 11th program.

The proposed plan includes as many as **34 potential offshore lease sales** across 21 of 27 existing OCS planning areas, covering approximately **1.27 billion acres**, including areas along the Pacific coast and Atlantic coast that have been off-limits for decades. [source: Sabin Center for Climate Change Law](https://climate.law.columbia.edu/content/trump-signs-executive-order-expand-offshore-drilling-0)

### Biden's January 2025 Protections

Before leaving office, President Biden issued two presidential memorandums invoking authority under the Outer Continental Shelf Lands Act (OCSLA) to withdraw more than **625 million acres** of U.S. coastal ocean from new leasing. Areas protected included:
- The entire Atlantic coast
- The Pacific coast (California, Oregon, Washington)
- The eastern Gulf of Mexico
- Portions of the Northern Bering Sea in Alaska

[source: FactCheck.org — Biden Offshore Drilling Ban](https://www.factcheck.org/2025/01/how-trump-may-be-able-to-stop-bidens-ban-on-new-offshore-drilling/)

### The Legal Fight

A 2019 federal court ruling found that while the OCSLA grants presidents authority to withdraw ocean areas from leasing, it **does not grant authority to undo a prior president's withdrawal**. On February 19, 2025, a coalition of environmental groups led by Surfrider Foundation, Oceana, Center for Biological Diversity, and Greenpeace — represented by Earthjustice — filed the **first environmental lawsuit** against the new Trump administration, challenging the executive order as illegal.

[source: Earthjustice Press Release](https://earthjustice.org/press/2025/groups-file-first-environmental-lawsuit-vs-new-trump-administration-challenging-illegal-order-to-undo-ocean-protections-from-offshore-drilling)

### [+] Deepwater Horizon: The Cost of Offshore Drilling Failure

The Deepwater Horizon disaster on April 20, 2010 remains the largest marine oil spill in U.S. history and the benchmark for offshore drilling risk:

- **4.9 million barrels** of oil released (U.S. government estimate)
- **11 workers** killed in the initial explosion
- **$65 billion+** in total cleanup costs, fines, and penalties paid by BP as of 2018
- **$20.8 billion** consent decree — the largest environmental damage settlement in U.S. history (April 2016)
- **105,400 sea birds** killed
- **170,000 sea turtles** killed (including cleanup efforts)
- **51% decrease** in dolphins in Louisiana's Barataria Bay
- Gulf of Mexico dead zones and ecosystem disruptions persisting more than 15 years later

[source: EPA — Deepwater Horizon BP Gulf Oil Spill](https://www.epa.gov/enforcement/deepwater-horizon-bp-gulf-america-oil-spill) | [source: NOAA Fisheries — 10 Years Later](https://www.fisheries.noaa.gov/news/deepwater-horizon-10-years-later-10-questions)

### [+] Routine Harms Beyond Spills

Even without catastrophic spills, offshore oil operations cause ongoing ecological harm:

**Seismic Surveys:**
Seismic air gun surveys blast extremely loud noise into the ocean floor to detect oil deposits. These blasts:
- Can travel hundreds of miles through water
- Disrupt whale and dolphin communication, migration, mating, and feeding
- Can cause temporary or permanent hearing loss in marine mammals
- Require authorization from NOAA under the Marine Mammal Protection Act before any "take" is permitted

[source: Marine Mammal Commission — Offshore Energy and Marine Mammals](https://www.mmc.gov/priority-topics/offshore-energy-development-and-marine-mammals/offshore-oil-and-gas-development-and-marine-mammals/)

**Routine Operational Discharges:**
Offshore platforms legally discharge drilling muds, cuttings, produced water (brine mixed with oil and chemical residues), and other wastes into the ocean during normal operations.

**Decommissioning Failures:**
Thousands of offshore wells are in various stages of abandonment. Improperly decommissioned wells can leak oil for decades. The GAO has flagged the backlog of orphaned wells as a significant environmental liability.

### [+] Alaska Drilling Expansion

The Trump administration is specifically targeting Alaska for expanded drilling:

- **21 of 27 OCS planning areas** in the proposal include Alaska waters
- **ANWR coastal plain** leasing resumed under an Interior Department order
- **Cook Inlet** (southern Alaska) reopened for new lease sales
- **Beaufort Sea** and **Chukchi Sea** — critical habitat for polar bears, Pacific walrus, bowhead whales — targeted for new leasing

Alaska Native communities who depend on subsistence hunting of marine mammals strongly oppose expanded offshore drilling in their waters.

**2026 Arctic Lease Sales and Expansions:**

- **March 18, 2026:** National Petroleum Reserve-Alaska auction shattered records with winning bids totaling **$164 million** across 187 tracts covering 1.3 million acres [source: Alaska Public Media](https://alaskapublic.org/news/2026-03-18/164m-auction-for-drilling-rights-in-the-npr-a-sets-new-records)
- **June 5, 2026:** BLM held a lease sale in the **Arctic National Wildlife Refuge** Coastal Plain (1.56 million acres), though the sale drew minimal industry interest — just two bidders on five tracts covering about 70,000 acres [source: Alaska Beacon](https://alaskabeacon.com/2026/03/19/arctic-alaska-oil-and-gas-lease-sale-draws-record-bidding-despite-legal-clouds/)
- **November 2025:** Trump administration granted Hilcorp a **5-year lease extension** (through 2029) for the Liberty offshore drilling project in the Arctic Ocean, which the Biden administration had previously denied [source: Center for Biological Diversity](https://biologicaldiversity.org/w/news/press-releases/lawsuit-launched-to-challenge-liberty-offshore-drilling-lease-extensions-in-arctic-2026-02-09/)
- Environmental groups filed suit in February 2026 to challenge the Liberty lease extension

### Economics: Coastal Tourism vs. Offshore Oil

U.S. coastal tourism and recreation generate over **$60 billion annually** in economic activity. A single major spill can devastate coastal fishing and tourism economies for decades — as Gulf Coast communities can attest. Many Atlantic and Pacific coast states have long opposed offshore drilling precisely because their coastal economies far outweigh any potential oil revenue.

## Analysis

### What This Means

The Trump administration's offshore drilling push is the most aggressive expansion attempt in decades. Key implications:

- **Atlantic and Pacific coasts** face new drilling risk for the first time in decades
- The **legal battle** over Biden's withdrawal may ultimately be decided by Congress or the courts
- **Alaska communities and ecosystems** face the most immediate risk from new lease sales
- The **34-sale proposal** would lock in drilling commitments that could outlast the current administration
- **Routine commingling policy changes** could increase Gulf production by 100,000+ barrels/day immediately

### Who's Affected

- **Coastal communities**: From New England to Florida to California — tourism and fishing economies at risk
- **Alaska Native peoples**: Subsistence hunting and fishing communities directly in the path of new drilling
- **Marine mammals**: Whales, dolphins, sea turtles, seabirds throughout the Atlantic, Pacific, and Arctic
- **Coastal property owners**: Spill risk reduces property values; offshore rigs reduce scenic value
- **Commercial fishers**: Gulf of Mexico and Alaskan fisheries still recovering from prior spill impacts
- **Taxpayers**: Decommissioning liabilities and spill cleanup costs often fall to the public

### The Regulatory Capture Problem

The Minerals Management Service (MMS), the predecessor agency to BOEM and BSEE, became notorious for cozy relationships with industry — a dynamic the Deepwater Horizon commission identified as a contributing factor to the disaster. Regulatory rollbacks under the current administration raise similar concerns about whether safety oversight will keep pace with the new expansion.

### Submit Public Comments on BOEM Lease Sales

BOEM is required by law to accept public comments on new lease sales. When a lease sale is proposed, comment periods open at **regulations.gov**. Comments from the public — especially from coastal residents and businesses — are part of the official record and carry legal weight in court challenges.

- Find open BOEM comment periods: [regulations.gov](https://www.regulations.gov) (search "BOEM" or "offshore leasing")
- BOEM's official comment portal: [boem.gov](https://www.boem.gov)
- Submit substantive comments describing specific local economic, environmental, or community impacts

### Contact Your State Attorney General

Multiple states have indicated they will use legal tools to fight offshore drilling expansion. If your state has not yet filed legal challenges or joined the Earthjustice/Oceana lawsuit coalition:

- Contact your state AG's office and urge them to join litigation
- Contact your state governor's office
- Urge your state to pass legislation banning offshore drilling in state waters (many Atlantic and Pacific states have already done so)

### Volunteer With Coastal Monitoring Programs (Citizen Science)

Document baseline coastal ecosystem health before any drilling begins, and support the organizations gathering evidence:

- **SkyTruth** ([skytruth.org](https://skytruth.org)) — Monitors oil spills and drilling activity via satellite imagery; volunteers help analyze imagery and track lease activities
- **Surfrider Foundation Beach Cleanups** ([surfrider.org/act](https://www.surfrider.org/act)) — Over 800 local chapters organize beach cleanups that generate water quality and pollution data
- **CoastalWatch / NOAA Coastal Observation Programs** — Volunteer marine mammal spotting and ocean health monitoring in coastal communities

### Request FOIA Records on BOEM's Review Process

File a Freedom of Information Act request to document the administration's decision-making process for expediting lease sales:

- Submit to the Bureau of Ocean Energy Management at [boem.gov/foia](https://www.boem.gov/about-boem/foia-requests-and-reports)
- Request: "Communications between BOEM officials and oil and gas industry representatives regarding the 11th National Outer Continental Shelf Oil and Gas Leasing Program, from January 2025 to present"
- FOIA-obtained documents have been central to prior offshore drilling legal challenges

### Choose Energy Providers That Don't Profit From Offshore Drilling

If your state has an energy choice market, you may be able to switch to a supplier that sources 100% renewable electricity:

- Check [EnergySage](https://www.energysage.com) or your state's energy choice website for green power options
- Reducing demand for offshore oil (by switching to EVs, heat pumps, and renewable electricity) is a long-term economic signal to the industry
- Invest in community solar or residential storage to reduce your household's dependence on fossil-fuel-generated grid power
