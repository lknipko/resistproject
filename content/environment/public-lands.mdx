# Public Lands & Drilling Expansion

## Quick Summary

**Key Point:** Executive Order 14174, "Unleashing American Energy," revoked the moratorium on new oil and gas leasing on federal lands and waters, opened the Arctic National Wildlife Refuge to drilling, and directed review of all national monument designations for potential reduction. NEPA environmental review is being bypassed for energy projects through categorical exclusions. The Bureau of Land Management (BLM) has accelerated lease sales across the West.

Public lands belong to all Americans. What happens to them affects recreation, wildlife habitat, water quality, and the climate.

## Facts

### Executive Order 14174: Unleashing American Energy

Signed January 20, 2025, this order:
- Revoked the moratorium on new oil and gas leasing on federal lands and waters
- Directed Interior Department to resume oil and gas lease sales "immediately"
- Opened the Arctic National Wildlife Refuge (ANWR) Coastal Plain to drilling
- Directed review of all national monument designations under the Antiquities Act since 1996
- Ordered agencies to use categorical exclusions to bypass NEPA environmental review for energy projects
- Revoked Biden-era conservation and climate executive orders

[source: Executive Order 14174](https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2025/01/29/2025-02054/unleashing-american-energy)

### [+] Arctic National Wildlife Refuge

The Coastal Plain of ANWR is the biological heart of America's largest wildlife refuge -- 19.3 million acres of arctic wilderness in northeastern Alaska. It is calving habitat for the Porcupine caribou herd (approximately 218,000 animals), denning habitat for polar bears, and home to migratory birds from all 50 states.

The 2017 Tax Cuts and Jobs Act mandated two lease sales in the Coastal Plain. The first, held in January 2021, attracted minimal interest. The current administration has moved to expand the leasing program and waive environmental protections.

[source: U.S. Fish & Wildlife Service - Arctic Refuge](https://www.fws.gov/refuge/arctic) | [source: USGS Assessment of ANWR](https://pubs.usgs.gov/fs/2018/3001/fs20183001.pdf)

### [+] National Monument Reviews

The administration has directed review of 27+ national monuments designated since 1996 for potential reduction or elimination. Under the Antiquities Act (1906), presidents can designate national monuments, but whether they can reduce or eliminate them is legally disputed.

Monuments under review include:
- **Bears Ears** (Utah) -- 1.36 million acres, sacred to five tribal nations
- **Grand Staircase-Escalante** (Utah) -- 1.87 million acres
- **Northeast Canyons and Seamounts** (Atlantic Ocean) -- Marine national monument
- **Papahānaumokuākea** (Pacific Ocean) -- World's largest marine conservation area

[source: National Parks Conservation Association](https://www.npca.org/advocacy/monument-protection)

### [+] BLM Lease Sales

The Bureau of Land Management has accelerated oil and gas lease sales:
- Multiple quarterly lease sales held in 2025-2026 across Western states
- Reduced public comment periods on lease sale environmental assessments
- Minimum bid per acre remains at $10 (set in 1987, never adjusted for inflation)
- Royalty rates for onshore drilling remain at 12.5% (lowest in the developed world)

[source: BLM Oil and Gas Leasing](https://www.blm.gov/programs/energy-and-minerals/oil-and-gas/leasing)

### [+] NEPA Bypass

The National Environmental Policy Act (NEPA) requires environmental review before major federal actions. The administration has directed agencies to use "categorical exclusions" -- a NEPA bypass for actions deemed to have no significant environmental impact -- for energy projects including:
- Oil and gas drilling permits
- Pipeline approvals
- Mining permits on federal lands
- Renewable energy projects (including solar and wind)

[source: Federal Register - EO 14154](https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2025/01/29/2025-02054/unleashing-american-energy)

## Analysis

### What This Means

- Public lands that took decades to protect can be opened to extraction in months
- NEPA bypasses mean projects proceed without environmental impact assessment
- Revenue from federal oil and gas leasing is historically low ($10/acre minimum bids, 12.5% royalties) -- taxpayers subsidize extraction
- Reduced monuments could lose protections for cultural sites, archaeological resources, and habitats
- Arctic drilling poses risks to one of the last intact ecosystems on Earth

### Who's Affected

- **Tribal nations:** Many monuments protect sacred sites and ancestral lands
- **Outdoor recreation economy:** Public lands support $887 billion/year in economic activity and 7.6 million jobs
- **Wildlife:** Habitat fragmentation from drilling, roads, and infrastructure
- **Rural communities:** Air and water quality near drilling sites
- **All Americans:** These are publicly owned lands held in trust for current and future generations

### Comment on BLM Lease Sales

BLM posts upcoming lease sales on their website. You can comment during the environmental assessment period:
- [BLM Lease Sales](https://www.blm.gov/programs/energy-and-minerals/oil-and-gas/leasing/regional-lease-sales)
- Comments citing specific environmental concerns (water contamination, wildlife habitat, air quality) carry the most weight
