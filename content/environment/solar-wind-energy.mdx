# Clean Energy: Solar, Wind, and the Transition

## Quick Summary

**Key Point:** Solar and wind are the cheapest sources of new electricity for the 10th year in a row. Unsubsidized utility-scale solar costs $38-78/MWh, onshore wind $37-86/MWh -- both below new natural gas plants. Globally, 91% of new renewable projects are cheaper than fossil fuel alternatives. Wind and solar generated a record 17% of U.S. electricity in 2025, up from less than 1% in 2005.

The barriers to faster deployment are political, not economic. The One Big Beautiful Bill Act (signed July 2025) eliminates the residential solar tax credit after December 31, 2025, terminates clean vehicle credits after September 30, 2025, and phases out utility-scale solar and wind production tax credits for projects starting construction after July 4, 2026. Meanwhile, battery storage costs have dropped 27% year-over-year, and the U.S. installed a record 58 GWh of new battery storage in 2025.

## Facts

### Key Points

- **Cheapest power source:** Solar and wind are the least expensive new-build generation for the 10th consecutive year [source: Lazard LCOE+ 2025](https://www.lazard.com/research-insights/levelized-cost-of-energyplus-lcoeplus/)
- **Record generation:** Wind and solar produced 760,000 GWh in 2025 -- 17% of all U.S. electricity [source: EIA](https://www.eia.gov/todayinenergy/detail.php?id=67367)
- **Record deployment:** The U.S. added 63 GW of new generating capacity in 2025, led by 32.5 GW of solar [source: EIA](https://www.eia.gov/todayinenergy/detail.php?id=64586)
- **Battery storage boom:** 58 GWh of new U.S. battery storage installed in 2025 -- the largest single year on record [source: SEIA](https://seia.org/news/united-states-installs-58-gwh-of-new-energy-storage-in-2025/)
- **Jobs growth:** Renewable energy supports 596,100 U.S. jobs, with solar and wind accounting for 84% [source: DOE USEER 2025](https://www.energy.gov/articles/doe-report-shows-clean-energy-jobs-grew-more-twice-rate-overall-us-employment)
- **Tax credits eliminated:** The One Big Beautiful Bill Act phases out residential solar credits (end of 2025), EV credits (Sept 2025), and utility-scale solar/wind credits (July 2026) [source: Arnold & Porter Analysis](https://www.arnoldporter.com/en/perspectives/advisories/2025/07/from-ira-to-obbba-a-new-era-for-clean-energy-tax-credits)

### [+] Cost Revolution: Solar and Wind Beat Fossil Fuels

**Lazard's 2025 LCOE+ Report** (18th annual edition, June 2025) confirms that unsubsidized renewables remain the cheapest new-build power generation:

| Technology | LCOE ($/MWh) | Trend |
|-----------|-------------|-------|
| Utility-scale solar | $38-78 (avg $58) | Down 4% year-over-year |
| Onshore wind | $37-86 | Increased due to supply chain costs |
| Combined cycle gas | 10-year high | Rising from turbine shortages |
| Coal | Not competitive | Highest costs in decades |

[source: Lazard LCOE+ June 2025](https://www.lazard.com/research-insights/levelized-cost-of-energyplus-lcoeplus/) | [source: PV Tech - Lazard Solar LCOE](https://www.pv-tech.org/us-utility-scale-solar-pv-lcoe-tightens-to-us38-78-mwh-in-2025-lazard/)

**IRENA's Global Cost Analysis (2024 data):**

- 91% of newly commissioned renewable capacity delivered power cheaper than the cheapest new fossil fuel alternative
- Solar PV was 41% cheaper than the lowest-cost fossil fuel alternatives on average
- Onshore wind was 53% cheaper than the lowest-cost fossil alternatives
- Onshore wind: $0.034/kWh globally -- the cheapest new electricity source
- Solar PV: $0.043/kWh globally
- 582 GW of new renewable capacity in 2024 avoided $467 billion in fossil fuel costs

[source: IRENA Renewable Power Generation Costs 2024](https://www.irena.org/Publications/2025/Jun/Renewable-Power-Generation-Costs-in-2024) | [source: IRENA Press Release](https://www.irena.org/News/pressreleases/2025/Jul/91-Percent-of-New-Renewable-Projects-Now-Cheaper-Than-Fossil-Fuels-Alternatives)

**Cost reductions are expected to continue through 2030**, with solar installed costs potentially falling to $388/kW and onshore wind to $861/kW.

### [+] Jobs and Economic Growth

Clean energy employment is growing at more than twice the rate of overall U.S. employment:

**Current employment (2024 data):**
- **Solar:** 370,600 jobs (largest renewable energy employer)
- **Wind:** 133,000 jobs
- **Total renewable energy:** 596,100 jobs
- **Total energy sector:** 8.35 million jobs, with renewables comprising over 40%

[source: DOE 2025 U.S. Energy and Employment Report](https://www.energy.gov/sites/default/files/2025-08/National%20USEER_08282025.pdf)

**Growth trajectory:**
- Wind turbine service technician and solar PV installer are the two fastest-growing occupations in the U.S. for the third consecutive year
- Solar and wind employment projected to grow ~60% from 2022-2032
- Battery storage manufacturing projected for double or triple-digit employment growth by 2034

[source: Bureau of Labor Statistics via EESI](https://www.eesi.org/papers/view/fact-sheet-climate-jobs-2026)

**Threat to growth:** Policy reversals by the Trump administration are creating uncertainty. The Rhodium Group-MIT Clean Investment Monitor found that 110 clean energy projects representing at least $36 billion in announced investments were canceled as of September 2025.

[source: WRI Clean Energy Jobs Analysis](https://www.wri.org/insights/clean-energy-jobs-us-report-findings)

### [+] Grid Integration and Battery Storage

The biggest criticism of solar and wind -- intermittency -- is being solved by rapidly advancing battery storage technology.

**Battery storage milestones:**
- The U.S. installed a record **58 GWh** of new battery storage capacity in 2025 [source: SEIA](https://seia.org/news/united-states-installs-58-gwh-of-new-energy-storage-in-2025/)
- Global benchmark cost for 4-hour battery storage dropped **27% year-over-year** to $78/MWh in 2025 -- the lowest since tracking began in 2009
- Cost of utility-scale battery storage has fallen to **$65/MWh** as of December 2025
- Hybrid solar+storage projects deliver electricity at an average of **$57/MWh** globally

[source: NREL Cost Projections for Utility-Scale Battery Storage](https://docs.nrel.gov/docs/fy25osti/93281.pdf) | [source: BNEF via Carbon Credits](https://carboncredits.com/renewables-plus-storage-surge-as-battery-costs-drop-record-low-bnef-reports/)

**2026 outlook:**
- 86 GW of new U.S. capacity expected, with 26.3 GW from battery storage [source: EIA Electric Power Monthly](https://www.eia.gov/todayinenergy/detail.php?id=64586)
- Texas set to overtake California as the largest energy storage market
- U.S. deployments projected to reach 70 GWh/35 GW in 2026

[source: SunHub - U.S. Energy Storage Growth](https://news.sunhub.com/us-energy-storage-growth-2025-2026-outlook/)

**Grid reliability:** Modern grids increasingly use a combination of battery storage, demand response, improved transmission interconnections, and geographic diversity of renewable installations to maintain reliability. The Duck Curve -- the midday oversupply of solar -- is being addressed by pairing solar with 4-hour batteries that discharge during evening peak demand.

### [+] Policy Barriers and Tax Credit Status

The One Big Beautiful Bill Act (H.R. 1, signed July 4, 2025) accelerates the phaseout of most clean energy tax credits established by the Inflation Reduction Act of 2022.

**Credits already terminated (as of late 2025):**

| Credit | Section | Termination Date |
|--------|---------|-----------------|
| Clean Vehicle Credit | 30D | September 30, 2025 |
| Previously Owned Clean Vehicle | 25E | September 30, 2025 |
| Commercial Clean Vehicle | 45W | September 30, 2025 |
| Residential Clean Energy (rooftop solar) | 25D | December 31, 2025 |
| Energy Efficient Home Improvement | 25C | December 31, 2025 |

**Credits being phased out:**

| Credit | Section | Phase-out |
|--------|---------|-----------|
| Alternative Fuel Vehicle Refueling | 30C | After June 30, 2026 |
| New Energy Efficient Home | 45L | After June 30, 2026 |
| Clean Electricity Production Tax Credit | 45Y | No new construction after July 4, 2026; must be in service by Dec 31, 2027 |
| Clean Electricity Investment Tax Credit | 48E | No new construction after July 4, 2026; must be in service by Dec 31, 2027 |

[source: Arnold & Porter - From IRA to OBBBA](https://www.arnoldporter.com/en/perspectives/advisories/2025/07/from-ira-to-obbba-a-new-era-for-clean-energy-tax-credits) | [source: Congress.gov CRS Report](https://www.congress.gov/crs-product/IN12625) | [source: Pierce Atwood Analysis](https://www.pierceatwood.com/alerts/congress-phases-out-energy-tax-credits)

**What survived:** Carbon sequestration (45Q) and clean hydrogen (45V) tax credits have strong bipartisan support and remain largely intact. New foreign entity of concern (FEOC) restrictions bar certain foreign entities from accessing credits.

**State-level progress:** Despite federal rollbacks, 28 states plus D.C. have renewable portfolio standards. 23 states plus D.C. have requirements or goals for 100% renewable or clean electricity by 2050 or earlier. 16 states have RPS targets of at least 50%.

[source: NCSL State RPS and Goals](https://www.ncsl.org/energy/state-renewable-portfolio-standards-and-goals)

### [+] Utah Solar Potential

Utah has exceptional solar resources and growing renewable energy infrastructure:

**Solar resource:** Utah receives approximately 5.5-6.5 kWh/m²/day of solar irradiance -- among the best in the nation and comparable to parts of Spain and the Middle East. Southern Utah receives even higher irradiance.

[source: NREL National Solar Radiation Database](https://nsrdb.nrel.gov/)

**Current capacity:** Utah ranks 13th nationally in solar with 3,110 MW of installed capacity (as of early 2024), enough to power 586,000 homes. An additional 5,600 MW is projected by 2029.

[source: Pew - Utah Energy Mix](https://www.pew.org/en/research-and-analysis/fact-sheets/2025/03/utah-takes-steps-to-diversify-its-energy-mix)

**Utah Renewable Communities Program:** Approved by the Public Service Commission on March 4, 2026. Nineteen Utah communities -- including Salt Lake City, Park City, Ogden, Moab, and Millcreek -- have enrolled. Participating communities automatically enroll residents who are Rocky Mountain Power customers. Initial cost: $4/month per residential customer, with a low-income assistance fund for eligible customers to participate at no additional cost.

[source: Utah Renewable Communities](https://www.utahrenewablecommunities.org/) | [source: Utah Business](https://www.utahbusiness.com/press-releases/2026/03/30/new-renewable-energy-program-approved-19-communities/)

**Rocky Mountain Power's 2025 IRP:** PacifiCorp filed its 2025 Integrated Resource Plan on March 31, 2025. Environmental advocates have criticized the plan for delaying renewable energy investments until 2030 and prioritizing carbon-capture technology on existing coal plants. The Sierra Club noted that PacifiCorp did not model potential benefits of adding new solar, wind, and storage that still qualify for tax credits -- a potential billion-dollar missed opportunity.

[source: Sierra Club - PacifiCorp 2025 IRP](https://www.sierraclub.org/press-releases/2025/04/pacificorp-parent-company-rocky-mountain-power-files-lackluster-2025-energy) | [source: Utah Clean Energy - RMP Energy Plan](https://utahcleanenergy.org/unpacking-rocky-mountain-powers-updated-energy-plan/)

**Net metering in Utah:** Rocky Mountain Power offers net metering for customers with rooftop solar, though the export credit rate has been reduced in recent years. New customers with solar receive a lower export rate than those grandfathered under earlier programs.

[source: EnergySage - Rocky Mountain Power Net Metering 2026](https://www.energysage.com/local-data/net-metering/rocky-mountain-power/)

## Analysis

### What This Means

The economics of clean energy are settled: solar and wind are cheaper than building new fossil fuel plants in most U.S. markets. The cost advantage is widening as battery storage costs drop and fossil fuel infrastructure ages. The transition is happening because of market forces, not government mandates.

However, the federal policy environment is actively hostile to this transition. The OBBBA's elimination of clean energy tax credits removes critical financial incentives that were accelerating deployment. The residential solar tax credit elimination alone increases the cost of home solar by 30%, extending payback periods from ~7 years to 10+ years. Utility-scale developers face a hard deadline of July 4, 2026 to begin construction on projects that qualify for production tax credits.

The paradox: many of the clean energy investments created by the IRA are concentrated in Republican districts. An analysis found that over 60% of IRA-funded clean energy projects are in Republican-held congressional districts. Eliminating these credits threatens jobs and investment in the communities whose representatives voted for the repeal.

State-level action becomes even more critical. Twenty-eight states have renewable portfolio standards requiring utilities to increase renewable generation. Utah's new Renewable Communities Program offers a pathway for municipalities to pursue clean energy through Rocky Mountain Power, though the utility's own resource plan disappoints by delaying renewable investments.

### Who Benefits

- **Homeowners:** Solar panels still pay for themselves in most markets, even without the federal tax credit -- but the window for the 30% credit has closed. Those who installed before December 31, 2025 locked in significant savings
- **Rural communities:** Wind farms generate lease payments for landowners ($3,000-8,000/year per turbine) and property tax revenue for rural counties
- **Workers:** Clean energy jobs are growing at 2x the rate of overall employment. Solar installer and wind technician are the two fastest-growing occupations in the U.S.
- **Ratepayers:** As utilities add cheap solar and wind, electricity costs are moderated compared to volatile fossil fuel prices
- **Utah residents:** The state's exceptional solar resource means above-average returns on solar investment. The new Renewable Communities Program offers a low-cost entry point at $4/month

### Research Rooftop Solar for Your Home

Even without the federal tax credit, rooftop solar pays for itself in most U.S. markets:

- **Get free quotes:** [EnergySage](https://www.energysage.com) -- Compare solar installers and pricing (nonprofit marketplace)
- **Estimate your savings:** [Google Project Sunroof](https://sunroof.withgoogle.com) -- See your roof's solar potential using satellite data
- **Check Utah incentives:** [DSIRE Database - Utah](https://programs.dsireusa.org/system/program?state=UT) -- Database of state and local solar incentives
- **Understand net metering:** [Rocky Mountain Power Customer Generation](https://www.rockymountainpower.net/savings-energy-choices/customer-generation.html) -- Current net metering rates in Utah

**Key economics (2026, without federal credit):**
- Average residential system: $20,000-30,000 before incentives
- Payback period: 8-12 years (varies by location and electricity rates)
- System lifespan: 25-30+ years
- Still a strong long-term investment, especially as electricity rates rise

### Support Community Solar

If rooftop solar is not an option (renter, shading, roof condition), community solar programs let you subscribe to a share of a local solar farm:

- **Utah Renewable Communities Program:** If your municipality has enrolled, you will be automatically opted in. Check at [utahrenewablecommunities.org](https://www.utahrenewablecommunities.org/)
- **Rocky Mountain Power Subscriber Solar:** [rockymountainpower.net/subscriber-solar](https://www.rockymountainpower.net/savings-energy-choices/blue-sky-renewable-energy/subscriber-solar.html)
- **Community Solar Hub:** [communitysolaraccess.org](https://communitysolaraccess.org) -- Find community solar programs in your area

### Advocate at the State Level

State policies are now the primary driver of clean energy progress:
- **Attend Utah Public Service Commission hearings** on Rocky Mountain Power rate cases and resource plans
- **Support your city joining the Renewable Communities Program** -- Communities must adopt the ordinance by June 2, 2026 to participate in the first round
- **Push for stronger state renewable portfolio standards** -- Utah has no binding RPS; advocate for one through [utahcleanenergy.org](https://utahcleanenergy.org)
- **Oppose utility roadblocks to rooftop solar** -- Reduced net metering rates and standby charges discourage distributed generation

### Invest in Clean Energy

- **Choose renewable energy plans** from your utility (Blue Sky program through Rocky Mountain Power)
- **Install rooftop solar** if your home qualifies -- even without federal credits, it pays for itself
- **Purchase community solar subscriptions** if rooftop is not feasible
- **Support clean energy ballot initiatives** in your state and municipality
