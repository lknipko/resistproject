## Critical Issues

### Government Dismantlement & Power Grabs

**Supreme Court Expands Presidential Power** — In a historic end-of-term, the Court **overturned the 90-year-old *Humphrey's Executor* precedent** (*Trump v. Slaughter*, June 29, 6-3), letting the president fire FTC commissioners at will — threatening independence at the NLRB, FERC, MSPB and beyond. It did protect Fed Governor Lisa Cook from removal 5-4 the same day. The rulings hand the "unitary executive" theory its biggest victory yet.

- [LEARN: Independent Agencies & the Removal Power](/learn/independent-agencies)
- [ACT: Defend the Justice System](/act/defend-justice)

**DOGE Formally Sunset** — DOGE ceased operating as a centralized entity on its self-imposed **July 4, 2026** termination date. OMB Director Russ Vought told Congress there would be **no closing report**. Its dashboard claimed **$215 billion** in savings — far below Musk's $2 trillion target, and only ~30% backed by documented receipts. An estimated **200,000–260,000 federal workers** left through DOGE-linked firings, buyouts, and retirements.

- [LEARN: DOGE and Government Dismantlement](/learn/doge)
- [ACT: Protect Federal Services](/act/protect-federal-services)

**Schedule F: Civil Service Gutted** — On **June 3, EO 14410** reclassified ~8,000 career positions (97% GS-15/SES) into at-will "Schedule Policy/Career" status, stripping MSPB appeal rights and routing whistleblower complaints internally. NARFE, NTEU, and AFGE have all sued. **10,000+ federal lawyers** have quit government (1 in 5).

- [LEARN: Schedule F and the Administrative State](/learn/schedule-f)
- [ACT: Whistleblower Resources](/act/whistleblower)

**Project 2025 Implementation** — The Heritage Foundation's 920-page blueprint is being implemented across every federal agency. The *Slaughter* removal ruling, the Education Department's dismantling, and Schedule F all track its recommendations directly.

- [LEARN: Project 2025 Blueprint](/learn/project-2025)
- [ACT: Fight Project 2025](/act/fight-project-2025)

**Impoundment & Spending Workarounds** — A federal judge on **July 13 voided the collusive IRS "settlement"** that created a **$1.776 billion "anti-weaponization fund"** for Trump allies outside the appropriations process — sanctioning administration lawyers and referring officials for bar discipline. OMB is separately rewriting federal grant rules (2 CFR 200) to let agencies cancel awards that no longer match "agency priorities."

- [LEARN: Impoundment & Unconstitutional Fund Withholding](/learn/impoundment)
- [ACT: Demand Congressional Power of the Purse](/act/congressional-power)

### Immigration & Human Rights

**$70B ICE/CBP Funding Signed Into Law** — Trump signed the **$70 billion reconciliation bill** on **June 10** (House 214-212, Senate 52-47) funding ICE and CBP through the end of his term with few restrictions, ending the 115-day DHS funding standoff. ICE has since **tripled internal daily arrest quotas** to ~2,000/day — more than **10,000 arrested in five days** in early July, most with no criminal record.

- [LEARN: ICE Detention & Deportation Crisis](/learn/ice-detention-deportation)
- [ACT: ICE Detention Defense](/act/ice-detention-defense)

**ICE Shootings of Non-Target Civilians** — ICE officers **fatally shot two men who were "not the target"** of operations weeks apart — Lorenzo Salgado Araujo in Houston (**July 7**) and Johan Durán Guerrero in Biddeford, Maine (**July 13**), neither agent wearing a body camera. Mexico is filing criminal complaints over **17 nationals'** deaths; Colombia's president called Biddeford a "murder." HRW counts **52 deaths in ICE custody** in Trump's first 500 days.

- [LEARN: ICE Shootings & Use of Force](/learn/ice-agent-shootings)
- [ACT: ICE Detention Defense](/act/ice-detention-defense)

**Supreme Court Ends TPS for 350,000+** — In *Mullin v. Doe* (**June 25**, 6-3), the Court held that courts cannot review DHS's termination of Temporary Protected Status, clearing the way to end protections for **~350,000 Haitians and ~4,000 Syrians** — and up to **1.3 million** TPS holders from 17 countries.

- [LEARN: Temporary Protected Status Under Threat](/learn/temporary-protected-status)
- [ACT: Defend Immigrant Rights & ICE Accountability](/act/ice-detention-defense)

**Birthright Citizenship Upheld** — In *Trump v. Barbara* (**June 30**, 6-3, Roberts writing), the Supreme Court **struck down Trump's executive order** ending birthright citizenship, holding that children born in the U.S. to unlawfully or temporarily present parents "are citizens at birth." Trump urged Congress to legislate restrictions instead.

- [LEARN: Birthright Citizenship Challenge](/learn/birthright-citizenship)
- [ACT: Join Litigation](/act/join-litigation)

Separately, **101 retired judges** filed an ethics complaint against Blanche.

- [LEARN: Pardons & the Assault on Justice](/learn/pardons-justice-system)
- [ACT: Court Reform & Accountability](/act/court-reform)

**National Guard Occupation Extended to 2029** — The Pentagon **extended the D.C. National Guard deployment through January 2029** — over 5,000 troops at an estimated **$3 million/day** — amid ongoing litigation and a circuit split over domestic military deployments.

- [LEARN: Federal Law Enforcement Escalation](/learn/federal-law-enforcement)
- [ACT: Defend the Justice System](/act/defend-justice)

DOJ **refused further release** (3.5M pages produced, ~2.5M withheld) and is appealing. House Oversight subpoenaed billionaire Leon Black (who paid Epstein ~$158M); Blanche's AG confirmation hearing was dominated by the files.

### Press Freedom & Democratic Institutions

**DOJ Subpoenas Journalists** — On **July 11**, DOJ issued grand-jury subpoenas — delivered by agents to reporters' homes — to **four New York Times journalists** over reporting on the Qatari-donated jet. It came weeks after DOJ **withdrew subpoenas** against Washington Post and Wall Street Journal reporters (June 23) who fought them in court. The U.S. sits at **64th in global press freedom**, its lowest ranking ever.

- [LEARN: Press Freedom Under Attack](/learn/press-freedom)
- [ACT: Defend Press Freedom](/act/press-freedom-defense)

**Election Administration Under Pressure** — DOJ sent **all 50 states letters threatening criminal prosecution** over noncitizens on voter rolls (July 7, some given five days to respond). The White House **fired two Democratic Election Assistance Commission members** (July 10), disrupting the bipartisan body that certifies voting equipment before the midterms. On **July 16**, Trump gave a primetime address claiming China "stole" 220 million voter files — claims fact-checkers found unsupported.

- [LEARN: Election Security](/learn/election-security)
- [ACT: Protect Your Vote](/act/protect-your-vote)

**Voting Rights Narrowed Further** — The Supreme Court **declined to revive the private right to enforce VRA Section 2** in seven states (June 22) — where over 90% of such cases have historically been brought by private plaintiffs, not DOJ. Federal courts did block Trump's election executive order (proof-of-citizenship and mail-ballot provisions) in June.

- [LEARN: Voting Rights & Election Integrity](/learn/voting-rights)
- [ACT: Protect Your Vote](/act/protect-your-vote)

### Public Health & Environment

**Dismantling Public Health** — U.S. **measles cases neared 2,300** across 44 jurisdictions by mid-July — the elimination-status review pushed to November. **Sturgis Hospital (MI)** became the **4th hospital closure of 2026**, with 380+ rural hospitals at risk from Medicaid cuts. HHS is **overhauling the U.S. Preventive Services Task Force** (which sets no-cost screening coverage) and pushing antidepressant "deprescribing" guidance.

- [LEARN: Leaving the WHO & Dismantling Public Health](/learn/public-health)
- [ACT: Protect Public Health](/act/public-health)

**Medicaid Work Requirements Take Hold** — CMS's **June 1 rule** narrowed the "medically frail" exemption; **25 states and D.C. sued** (June 30). Nebraska, Montana, and Arkansas have begun enforcement ahead of the January 2027 deadline. CBO projects work requirements cut Medicaid **$344B** and cause **4.8 million** to lose coverage.

- [LEARN: OBBBA and Medicaid Changes](/learn/obbba-medicaid)
- [ACT: Medicaid Enrollment Defense](/act/medicaid-enrollment)

**Climate & Environment Rollback** — On **July 14**, the administration **gutted the Endangered Species Act's "harm" rule**, removing habitat destruction from the definition of harm (tribes sued within days). Clean-energy **wind and solar tax credits ended July 4** under OBBBA. The EPA is moving to **rescind drinking-water limits** for three PFAS "forever chemicals," and announced the **first-ever U.S. deep-sea mining lease sale** near American Samoa.

- [LEARN: Climate & Environment Rollback](/learn/climate-environment)
- [ACT: Defend the Environment](/act/environment)

**Our Home: Public Lands Under Siege** — On **July 13**, Trump **cut Bears Ears and Grand Staircase-Escalante national monuments by ~90%** (nearly 3 million acres), without tribal consultation. The **Colorado River** hit an all-time storage record low (July 12) and the **Great Salt Lake** sits within 3 feet of its record low amid toxic dust storms.

- [LEARN: Our Home — Environment & Public Lands](/ourhome)
- [ACT: Defend the Environment](/act/environment)

**US-Iran War Reignites; Hormuz Closed** — A **14-point ceasefire** signed at Versailles (**June 17**) **collapsed by July 8**; the U.S. has since carried out multiple nights of strikes on Bandar Abbas, and Iran has attacked **five Gulf states** and **declared the Strait of Hormuz closed** (July 12). Oil jumped **~9% to ~$78** as Hormuz traffic fell 60%. The Senate passed a **War Powers resolution 50-48** (June 24) directing withdrawal — the first time both chambers have done so — though it lacks force of law.

### Fiscal & Social Safety Net

**Student Loan Overhaul Takes Effect** — On **July 1**, SAVE, PAYE, and ICR **closed to new borrowers**, replaced by the Repayment Assistance Plan (RAP) and a Tiered Standard Plan; forgiveness after 2025 is now taxable. A new rule **narrowed Public Service Loan Forgiveness** eligibility — drawing a **21-state lawsuit** — and Parent PLUS borrowers lose PSLF access entirely.

- [LEARN: Student Loans](/learn/student-loans)
- [ACT: Student Loan Defense](/act/student-loan-defense)

**Social Security & Medicare Solvency** — The 2026 Trustees moved **Social Security's depletion date to Q4 2032** (78% of benefits payable after) and **Medicare's Hospital Insurance fund to 2033**. The 2027 COLA is estimated around **3.7–3.8%**.

- [LEARN: Social Security & Medicare](/learn/social-security-medicare)
- [ACT: Protect Social Security](/act/protect-social-security)

**Health Coverage Cliff** — Enhanced ACA marketplace subsidies **expired**, roughly **doubling premiums** for subsidized enrollees and dropping enrollment from 22.1M to 19.2M. A new **Medicare GLP-1 "Bridge"** offers Wegovy/Zepbound for a **$50/month** copay as of July 1.

- [LEARN: The ACA Marketplace Crisis](/learn/aca-marketplace)
- [ACT: Economic Relief Resources](/act/economic-relief)

## Guiding Principles

1. **Primary sources or nothing** — Federal Register, Congress.gov, court filings, .gov domains
2. **Facts and analysis separated** — Readers always know what's verified vs. interpreted
3. **Action over awareness** — Information without action is insufficient
4. **Credibility is paramount** — One bad source undermines everything
5. **Non-partisan framing** — Focus on rights and principles, not party politics
