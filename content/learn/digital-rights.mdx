# Digital Rights, Education, and the Courts

## Quick Summary

**Key Point:** In June 2025, the Supreme Court issued three rulings that used "protecting children" to restrict broader rights: *Paxton* (6-3) upheld mandatory age verification for websites, *Mahmoud v. Taylor* (6-3) required schools to let parents opt children out of LGBTQ+ curriculum, and *Skrmetti* (6-3) allowed states to ban gender-affirming care for minors under the lowest judicial scrutiny. Meanwhile, TikTok avoided a ban through a U.S. joint venture that gives the government algorithmic oversight of the platform used by 200+ million Americans.

### [+] Free Speech Coalition v. Paxton: Age Verification

**The Ruling (6-3, June 27, 2025):**

The Supreme Court upheld Texas HB 1181, which requires websites where more than one-third of content is "sexual material harmful to minors" to verify that visitors are 18 or older. The decision reversed decades of precedent that had applied strict scrutiny to online speech restrictions. [source: Supreme Court Opinion](https://www.supremecourt.gov/opinions/24pdf/23-1122_3e04.pdf)

**Key Holdings:**
- Applied **intermediate scrutiny** (not strict scrutiny), a significant downgrade in First Amendment protection for online speech
- Ruled the burden on adults to verify identity is "incidental" to the state's interest in protecting children
- Rejected arguments that age verification chills anonymous speech
- Distinguished this from the 2004 *Ashcroft v. ACLU* ruling that struck down a federal online age verification law

**Why This Matters Beyond Adult Content:**

More than 20 states have now passed age verification laws, and the SCREEN Act in Congress would create a federal requirement. The EFF warns this ruling "tramples free speech and undermines privacy" because the legal reasoning applies broadly: if states can require ID verification for adult content, the same logic could permit ID requirements for social media accounts, age-gating on political content, or tracking who accesses what content online. [source: EFF Analysis](https://www.eff.org/pages/supreme-courts-decision-age-verification-tramples-free-speech-and-undermines-privacy) | [source: Perkins Coie Analysis](https://perkinscoie.com/insights/blog/free-speech-coalition-v-paxton-what-supreme-courts-age-verification-decision-could)

### [+] Mahmoud v. Taylor: Educational Opt-Outs

**The Ruling (6-3, June 27, 2025):**

The Supreme Court ruled that Montgomery County, Maryland's refusal to allow parents to opt their elementary-school children out of lessons using LGBTQ+-inclusive storybooks violated the Free Exercise Clause. Justice Alito wrote the majority opinion. [source: Supreme Court Opinion](https://www.supremecourt.gov/opinions/24pdf/24-297_4f14.pdf)

**Background:**

In 2022, Montgomery County schools added LGBTQ+-inclusive children's books to supplemental language arts curriculum (pre-K through 5th grade). Initially, schools allowed parents to opt out. In March 2023, the district ended opt-outs "for any reason." Muslim, Catholic, and Orthodox Christian parents sued. [source: SCOTUSblog](https://www.scotusblog.com/2025/06/court-allows-parents-to-opt-their-children-out-of-school-lessons-involving-lgbtq-themes/)

**Key Holdings:**
- Government **substantially burdens** parents' religious exercise when it requires children to participate in instruction that violates family religious beliefs
- The majority found that teachers were "encouraged to reinforce" pro-LGBTQ+ viewpoints and "reprimand any children who disagree" -- making this more than neutral exposure
- Applied **strict scrutiny** to the school's opt-out refusal
- Schools cannot deny religious opt-out requests for inclusive curriculum

**Dissent (Sotomayor):**

Justice Sotomayor warned the ruling has "no discernable limits" and could subject school curriculum on patriotism, women's rights, interfaith marriage, meat consumption, and "immodest dress" to stringent judicial review whenever a parent raises a religious objection. [source: Congress.gov Analysis](https://www.congress.gov/crs-product/LSB11360)

**Implications:**
- Individual parents can veto inclusive education for their children on religious grounds
- Schools must create alternative instruction or excuse students
- May result in classroom segregation during certain lessons
- Empowers organized religious groups to challenge any curriculum element
- Shifts battles to local school boards, where advocacy matters most

### [+] United States v. Skrmetti: Gender-Affirming Care

**The Ruling (6-3, June 18, 2025):**

The Supreme Court upheld Tennessee's SB1, which bans puberty blockers, hormone therapy, and surgeries for gender-affirming care for minors. Chief Justice Roberts wrote the majority opinion. [source: Supreme Court Opinion](https://www.supremecourt.gov/opinions/24pdf/23-477_2cp3.pdf)

**Key Holdings:**
- Tennessee's ban classifies by **age** (adults can access these treatments) and **medical purpose** (puberty blockers are allowed for precocious puberty, just not for gender dysphoria) -- not by sex
- Because the law does not classify by sex, it triggers only **rational basis review** -- the lowest level of judicial scrutiny
- States have broad authority to regulate medical treatments for minors
- The Court declined to apply heightened scrutiny to laws affecting transgender individuals

**Dissent (Sotomayor, Jackson):**

The dissent argued the law plainly classifies by sex: whether a minor can receive puberty blockers depends entirely on whether the treatment aligns with or diverges from the sex assigned at birth. [source: KFF Analysis](https://www.kff.org/lgbtq/what-are-the-implications-of-the-skrmetti-ruling-for-minors-access-to-gender-affirming-care/)

**Current Impact:**
- **27 states** have enacted gender-affirming care bans for minors; 25 bans remain in effect as of mid-2025
- Effectively closes the federal constitutional path to challenge these bans
- Creates a patchwork where care is available in some states, banned in others
- Families seeking care must travel to states without bans
- Major medical organizations (AMA, AAP, Endocrine Society) oppose these bans as contrary to evidence-based medicine

### [+] TikTok U.S. Joint Venture

**The Deal (closed January 22, 2026):**

Instead of a total ban, TikTok was restructured as TikTok USDS Joint Venture LLC, with 80.1% U.S. and international ownership and ByteDance retaining a 19.9% minority stake. [source: TikTok Newsroom](https://newsroom.tiktok.com/announcement-from-the-new-tiktok-usds-joint-venture-llc) | [source: Deadline](https://deadline.com/2026/01/tiktok-sets-deal-for-majority-us-owned-joint-venture-1236694090/)

**Ownership Structure:**
- **Oracle** (15% stake, ~$2B) -- designated security partner, manages data audits and algorithm replication under U.S. jurisdiction [source: CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/11/oracle-tiktok-us-stake-outages.html)
- **Silver Lake** (15%) and **MGX** (Abu Dhabi, 15%) -- managing investors
- **ByteDance** (19.9% minority) -- retained to comply with federal law
- **Other U.S./international investors** -- remaining stake
- Led by CEO Adam Presser with Will Farrell as Chief Security Officer

**Implications:**
- The primary communication platform for 200M+ Americans is now under U.S. corporate and government algorithmic oversight
- Oracle is retraining the recommendation algorithm under U.S. jurisdiction -- raising questions about political content moderation
- Sets precedent for forced restructuring of foreign-owned social media on national security grounds
- Data of U.S. users will be stored and audited domestically

## Analysis

### The "Protect the Children" Pattern

A consistent theme connects *Paxton*, *Mahmoud*, and *Skrmetti*: the use of **child protection** as the legal mechanism to restrict broader rights.

| Case | "Child Protection" Claim | Right Restricted |
|------|--------------------------|------------------|
| *Paxton* | Protect minors from adult content | Online anonymity for adults |
| *Mahmoud* | Protect children from conflicting values | Inclusive public education |
| *Skrmetti* | Protect minors from medical decisions | Trans healthcare access |

By framing issues as child safety, courts apply lower scrutiny and permit restrictions that would be unconstitutional if applied to adults directly.

### The End of Online Anonymity

*Paxton* is particularly significant for civic organizing. With 20+ states now passing age verification laws and the federal SCREEN Act pending, the legal path to a "Real ID internet" is open. If states can require ID verification for adult content, the same logic permits:
- ID requirements for social media accounts
- Age-gating on political content deemed "inappropriate"
- Tracking of who accesses what content

Combined with the TikTok joint venture giving the U.S. government algorithmic oversight, the infrastructure for monitoring online dissent is expanding on multiple fronts.

### Who Is Affected

- **Trans youth and families**: 25 state bans in effect; must travel for care or go without
- **LGBTQ+ students**: Face classroom segregation when peers opt out; teachers must navigate complex logistics
- **Online activists**: Anonymity protections weakened by *Paxton*; 200M+ TikTok users now under government-overseen algorithm
- **Privacy advocates**: Age verification creates massive data collection by third-party vendors
- **School districts**: Must develop opt-out procedures or face litigation under *Mahmoud*
- **Medical providers**: Face criminal penalties in ban states for providing evidence-based care to trans minors
