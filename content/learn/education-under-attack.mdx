# The Dismantling of Public Education

## Quick Summary

Trump signed an executive order on March 20, 2025 directing the closure of the Department of Education, and the dismantling is now accelerating. On **July 16, 2026, the House Education and Workforce Committee advanced a 10-bill package** to permanently abolish the department and disperse its functions. The administration has already signed interagency agreements **moving special-education (IDEA) administration to HHS and education civil-rights enforcement to DOJ** (June), and plans to hand the department's headquarters building to the **Department of Energy**. The department has lost **53% of its lawyers** — the highest rate of any agency — and roughly half its staff (4,133 → ~2,000). The Office for Civil Rights **dismissed 90% of discrimination complaints**, resolving only 1% of cases. Meanwhile the **11th Circuit struck down Florida's "Stop WOKE Act"** higher-ed restrictions as unconstitutional (July 7). The **$1.7 trillion student-loan portfolio** is being transferred to Treasury, with major repayment changes that took effect **July 1, 2026** (see [Student Loans](/learn/student-loans)).

## Facts

### Key Points

- **Executive order directs closure of the Department of Education** (March 20, 2025). Trump ordered Education Secretary Linda McMahon to "take all necessary steps to facilitate the closure" of the department. The Department was created by Congress in 1979 and can only be abolished by Congress — requiring 60 Senate votes to overcome a filibuster [source: Executive Order](https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2025/03/improving-education-outcomes-by-empowering-parents-states-and-communities/)

- **H.R. 899 would terminate the Department by December 31, 2026.** The one-sentence bill introduced by Rep. Thomas Massie (R-KY) has 36 Republican cosponsors. It has not advanced past committee and would require 60 Senate votes [source: Congress.gov — H.R. 899](https://www.congress.gov/bill/119th-congress/house-bill/899)

- **Staff cut from 4,133 to ~2,000** (approximately 50% reduction). ~1,300 received RIF notices in March 2025, ~600 accepted voluntary separations earlier, and 466 additional RIF notices were sent in October 2025. A federal judge initially blocked the layoffs, but the **Supreme Court reversed (6-3) on July 14, 2025**, allowing terminations to proceed. Justice Sotomayor dissented: "When the executive publicly announces its intent to break the law, and then executes on that promise, it is the judiciary's duty to check that lawlessness, not expedite it." [source: NPR](https://www.npr.org/2025/03/12/nx-s1-5325854/trump-education-department-layoffs-civil-rights-student-loans) | [source: SCOTUSblog](https://www.scotusblog.com/2025/07/supreme-court-clears-the-way-for-trump-administration-to-massively-reduce-the-size-of-the-department-of-education/)

- **OCR dismissed 90% of civil rights complaints** between March and September 2025 (over 9,000 received). Resolved only **112 cases** in all of 2025 — just **1% of pending cases**, the fewest in at least 12 years. **Zero resolutions** in cases involving sexual harassment, sexual violence, racial harassment (including antisemitism and Islamophobia) despite 2,000+ pending cases. Disability discrimination resolutions dropped from 390 (2024) to 83 (2025). Current backlog: ~25,000 pending complaints including ~7,000 open investigations [source: GAO Report GAO-26-108320](https://www.gao.gov/products/gao-26-108320) | [source: Senate HELP Committee Report](https://www.sanders.senate.gov/wp-content/uploads/04.24.26-Justice-Denied-How-Trumps-Office-for-Civil-Rights-Reached-a-12-Year-Low-in-Protecting-Students-from-Discrimination_FINAL.pdf)

- **240+ OCR employees laid off** in March 2025, mostly attorneys investigating discrimination. 7 of 12 regional offices closed. GAO found the government spent **$38 million** paying 247 OCR staff who were on administrative leave and prohibited from working for ~9 months [source: GAO Report GAO-26-108320](https://www.gao.gov/products/gao-26-108320)

- **7.5 million students with disabilities** receive services under IDEA ($15.5 billion, flat-funded since 2023). The administration proposed consolidating 7 IDEA grant programs and canceled funding for dozens of special education programs including parent resource centers, teacher training, Braille training, interpreter training, and programs for deafblind individuals [source: Center for American Progress](https://www.americanprogress.org/article/the-trump-administrations-recent-special-education-layoffs-will-have-major-long-term-impacts-on-disabled-children-and-students/) | [source: CBPP](https://www.cbpp.org/blog/trump-administration-threatens-support-for-children-with-disabilities)

- **Title IX protections for LGBTQ+ students rescinded.** On April 6, 2026, OCR revoked portions of six Title IX resolution agreements protecting transgender students' bathroom access, preferred pronoun usage, and preventing LGBTQ+-related book removal. Biden-era Title IX regulations expanding protections were vacated nationwide by a federal court in January 2025 [source: Higher Ed Dive](https://www.highereddive.com/news/trump-education-department-rescinds-title-ix-pacts-protecting-lgbtq-students/816898/)

- **$1.7 trillion student loan portfolio** being transferred to Treasury in 3 phases (announced March 2026): Phase 1 — Treasury assumes defaulted debt collection; Phase 2 — non-defaulted loan operations; Phase 3 — FAFSA administration. No timeline set for Phases 2-3. Schools struggling to implement OBBBA financial aid changes with compressed timelines [source: NPR](https://www.npr.org/2026/03/19/nx-s1-5753906/student-loans-trump-treasury)

- **$6.8 billion in education grants frozen** on June 30, 2025 — one day before scheduled release. Funds were released July 25 after bipartisan congressional pressure and a lawsuit by 14 school districts [source: NPR](https://www.npr.org/2025/07/01/nx-s1-5453457/trump-school-funding-grants)

- **35 ED programs have FY2026 funds still "unallocated"** by OMB as of May 2026, meaning ED cannot spend congressionally approved funds [source: EdWeek](https://www.edweek.org/policy-politics/trump-holds-back-2-billion-for-education-grants-what-will-happen-next/2026/05)

- **Federal Tax Credit Scholarship Program** created by OBBBA (effective January 1, 2027). Taxpayers receive up to $1,700 tax credit for contributions to Scholarship Granting Organizations. Funds can be used for private school tuition, tutoring, and educational technology. States must opt in [source: Congress.gov CRS](https://www.congress.gov/crs-product/R48724)

### [+] The Executive Order and Legal Authority

**What the order does:**

Executive Order "Improving Education Outcomes by Empowering Parents, States, and Communities" (March 20, 2025) directs the Education Secretary to "take all necessary steps to facilitate the closure of the Department of Education and return authority over education to the States and local communities."

The order acknowledges it must act "to the maximum extent appropriate and permitted by law" — because the Department was created by Congress through the Department of Education Organization Act (1979) and can only be abolished by an act of Congress.

**Why only Congress can abolish ED:**

- The Department was established by Public Law 96-88 (signed by President Carter, 1979)
- Abolishing it requires legislation passing both chambers — 60 Senate votes to overcome a filibuster
- H.R. 899 (Rep. Massie) has only 36 Republican cosponsors and has not advanced

**What the administration is doing instead:**

Rather than waiting for congressional action, the administration is functionally dismantling the department through:
- Mass layoffs (50% staff reduction)
- Transferring programs to other agencies (10 interagency agreements)
- Freezing or withholding congressionally appropriated funds
- Collapsing enforcement offices (OCR)
- Moving the $1.7T student loan portfolio to Treasury

[source: Executive Order](https://www.whitehouse.gov/presidential-actions/2025/03/improving-education-outcomes-by-empowering-parents-states-and-communities/) | [source: White House Fact Sheet](https://www.whitehouse.gov/fact-sheets/2025/03/fact-sheet-president-donald-j-trump-empowers-parents-states-and-communities-to-improve-education-outcomes/)

### [+] Civil Rights Enforcement Collapse

**The Office for Civil Rights (OCR)** enforces federal civil rights laws in education, including Title VI (race), Title IX (sex), Section 504 (disability), and the Americans with Disabilities Act.

**By the numbers:**

| Metric | Before | After |
|--------|--------|-------|
| OCR staff | 575 | ~280 (7 of 12 regional offices closed) |
| Complaints received (Mar-Sep 2025) | 9,000+ | 90% dismissed without review |
| Resolution agreements (2025) | — | 112 (1% of pending cases, 12-year low) |
| Sexual harassment/violence resolutions | Routine | Zero |
| Racial harassment resolutions | Routine | Zero |
| Disability resolutions | 390 (2024) | 83 (2025) |
| Pending complaints | — | ~25,000 (including ~7,000 investigations) |
| Cost of paid-leave staff | — | $38 million (247 staff, ~9 months) |

**What this means for students:** Discrimination complaints in schools — from sexual harassment to disability access to racial discrimination — have effectively nowhere to go at the federal level. Students and families filing complaints are overwhelmingly likely to have them dismissed without review.

[source: GAO Report GAO-26-108320](https://www.gao.gov/products/gao-26-108320) | [source: 19th News](https://19thnews.org/2026/02/student-civil-rights-cases-dismissed-trump-education-department/) | [source: Senate HELP Committee Report](https://www.sanders.senate.gov/wp-content/uploads/04.24.26-Justice-Denied-How-Trumps-Office-for-Civil-Rights-Reached-a-12-Year-Low-in-Protecting-Students-from-Discrimination_FINAL.pdf)

### [+] Impact on Students with Disabilities

**7.5 million students** (15% of the student population) receive special education services under the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA).

**IDEA funding:** $15.5 billion — level-funded since 2023, meaning an effective cut when adjusted for inflation. The federal government has never met its original commitment to fund 40% of the excess cost of educating students with disabilities; it currently covers about 13%.

**Programs defunded or targeted:**

- Community parent resource centers ($33 million)
- Special education teacher training programs
- Braille training for visually impaired students
- Interpreter training for deaf/hard-of-hearing students
- Programs for deafblind individuals
- FY2026 proposal would consolidate 7 IDEA grant programs into one allocation

**Enforcement gap:** With OCR disability resolutions dropping from 390 (2024) to 83 (2025), students with disabilities have far less recourse when schools violate IDEA requirements.

[source: Center for American Progress](https://www.americanprogress.org/article/the-trump-administrations-recent-special-education-layoffs-will-have-major-long-term-impacts-on-disabled-children-and-students/) | [source: CBPP](https://www.cbpp.org/blog/trump-administration-threatens-support-for-children-with-disabilities) | [source: ABA — OCR gaps put IDEA at risk](https://www.americanbar.org/groups/diversity/disabilityrights/news/ocr-enforcement-gaps-put-students-idea-rights-at-risk/)

### [+] Title IX Rollbacks

**Biden-era regulations vacated:** A federal judge in Kentucky vacated the entire 1,500-page Biden Title IX regulation nationwide (January 2025). The regulation had expanded protections for LGBTQ+ students, required schools to address off-campus harassment, and added protections for pregnant and parenting students.

**Resolution agreements rescinded (April 6, 2026):** OCR revoked portions of six Title IX resolution agreements that protected LGBTQ+ students, including:
- Transgender students' bathroom access
- Use of preferred pronouns
- Prevention of LGBTQ+-related book removal from school libraries

**Who is affected:** Approximately 1.4 million LGBTQ+ students in U.S. public schools, plus millions on college campuses. Transgender students are disproportionately impacted.

[source: NBC News — Judge scraps Biden Title IX rules](https://www.nbcnews.com/nbc-out/out-politics-and-policy/judge-scraps-bidens-title-ix-rules-reversing-expansion-protections-lgb-rcna187052) | [source: Higher Ed Dive — ED rescinds Title IX pacts](https://www.highereddive.com/news/trump-education-department-rescinds-title-ix-pacts-protecting-lgbtq-students/816898/)

### [+] Impact on Low-Income Students

**Title I** sends approximately **$18 billion** annually to schools serving disadvantaged communities. If eliminated, an estimated **180,000 teaching positions** could be lost, affecting **2.8 million students**.

**FY2026 proposals:**
- House Appropriations Committee advanced a bill cutting Title I by 26% (~$4.7 billion reduction)
- Trump budget proposed eliminating all funding ($1.3 billion) for English language learners and migrant students
- Would combine 18 funding streams (rural schools, civics education, at-risk youth, students experiencing homelessness) from $6.5 billion down to $2 billion
- Congress ultimately rejected the deepest cuts and maintained level funding

**Funding freeze (summer 2025):** The administration froze $6.2-6.8 billion in education grants on June 30, 2025 — one day before scheduled release. An 84-word email to states said grants weren't "in accordance with the President's priorities." Funds released July 25 after bipartisan pressure and a lawsuit by 14 school districts.

**Ongoing withholding (May 2026):** 35 ED programs have most or all FY2026 appropriations listed as "unallocated" — ED doesn't have OMB permission to spend congressionally approved funds.

[source: NPR — Trump budget cuts](https://www.npr.org/2025/06/02/nx-s1-5420677/trump-budget-education-department-financial-aid) | [source: EdWeek — Trump holds back $2 billion](https://www.edweek.org/policy-politics/trump-holds-back-2-billion-for-education-grants-what-will-happen-next/2026/05) | [source: Center for American Progress — Title I elimination impact](https://www.americanprogress.org/article/project-2025s-elimination-of-title-i-funding-would-hurt-students-and-decimate-teaching-positions-in-local-schools/)

### [+] Student Loan Transfer to Treasury

In March 2026, ED signed an interagency agreement to transfer the **$1.7 trillion federal student loan portfolio** to the Department of Treasury in three phases:

1. **Phase 1:** Treasury assumes responsibility for collecting on defaulted debt
2. **Phase 2:** Treasury takes over non-defaulted loan operations
3. **Phase 3:** Treasury takes over FAFSA administration

No timeline has been set for Phases 2 and 3.

**Pell Grant shortfall:** The program faces a $5.5 billion shortfall by end of FY2026 and $11.5 billion by FY2027 (per CBO). OBBBA added $10.5 billion in mandatory funding for FY2026, temporarily averting a crisis.

**FAFSA chaos:** Schools are struggling to implement sweeping OBBBA changes with compressed timelines. Financial aid professionals warn of potential delays in aid disbursement for the 2026-27 award year.

[source: NPR — Student loans to Treasury](https://www.npr.org/2026/03/19/nx-s1-5753906/student-loans-trump-treasury) | [source: Inside Higher Ed — Pell Grant shortfall](https://www.insidehighered.com/news/government/student-aid-policy/2026/02/13/cbo-pell-grant-facing-115b-shortfall)

## Analysis

### What This Means

The Department of Education is being functionally eliminated without a congressional vote. The administration cannot legally abolish the department — that requires legislation — so it is dismantling it from the inside: gutting staff, collapsing enforcement, transferring programs, and withholding funds Congress already approved.

The collapse of OCR enforcement is particularly consequential. For the first time in decades, students who face discrimination in schools — sexual harassment, disability discrimination, racial harassment — have effectively no federal recourse. The backlog of 25,000 complaints and 7,000 open investigations represents real students whose civil rights violations are going unaddressed.

The student loan transfer to Treasury raises fundamental questions about accountability. Treasury has no experience administering financial aid, and the transition creates massive potential for disruption to 45 million borrowers during a period when OBBBA is simultaneously overhauling repayment programs.

### Who's Affected

- **50 million** K-12 public school students — from potential Title I cuts, reduced oversight, and weakened civil rights enforcement
- **7.5 million students with disabilities** — from IDEA oversight gutting, special education program defunding, and collapsed OCR enforcement
- **1.4 million LGBTQ+ students** — from rescinded Title IX protections and resolution agreements
- **2.8 million students** in Title I schools — at risk if funding is eliminated or reduced
- **19 million higher education students** — from student loan transfer chaos, FAFSA disruptions, and Pell Grant uncertainty
- **45 million student loan borrowers** — from portfolio transfer to Treasury and OBBBA repayment changes
- **180,000 teaching positions** at risk if Title I is eliminated
- **Parents and families** filing discrimination complaints — 90% dismissal rate means complaints go nowhere
