# ICE Shootings & Use of Force

## Quick Summary

As ICE tripled its internal arrest quotas in 2026, immigration agents have **fatally shot multiple people who were not the targets** of their operations — often without body cameras and with contested official accounts. In **Houston on July 7**, an officer killed construction worker **Lorenzo Salgado Araujo, 52**; acting ICE Director David Venturella later confirmed he "was not a target." Six days later, in **Biddeford, Maine (July 13)**, an officer killed **Johan Durán Guerrero, 25**, a work-authorized father of a 3-year-old — again confirmed not to be the subject of the warrant. These follow the January killings of Renée Good and Alex Pretti in Minneapolis. Mexico is filing **criminal complaints over 17 nationals' deaths**, and Colombia's president called the Biddeford killing a "murder."

### Key Points

- Agents in both the Houston and Biddeford shootings were **not wearing body cameras**, leaving contested accounts
- Human Rights Watch documents **52 deaths in ICE custody** over Trump's first 500 days — more than double the prior term's rate [source: HRW](https://www.hrw.org/report/2026/06/25/dying-in-detention/rising-deaths-in-an-expanding-us-immigration-detention-system)
- ICE separately **rescinded a rule requiring investigation of deaths** within 30 days of a detainee's release, drawing a 22-state challenge [source: Virginia Mercury](https://virginiamercury.com/2026/07/07/virginia-joins-multistate-push-against-ice-rollback-on-detainee-death-reporting/)

## Analysis

### What This Means

The recurrence of fatal shootings of non-targets — paired with the absence of body cameras and expanding arrest quotas — points to a systemic use-of-force problem, not isolated incidents. The international response (criminal complaints, presidential condemnations) is escalating the diplomatic stakes.

### Who's Affected

- **Immigrant communities** facing aggressive enforcement regardless of criminal history
- **Bystanders and U.S. citizens** caught in operations
- **U.S. diplomatic relationships** with Mexico, Colombia, and others whose nationals have been killed
