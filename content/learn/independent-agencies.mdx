# Independent Agencies & the Removal Power

## Quick Summary

For 90 years, *Humphrey's Executor v. United States* (1935) protected the heads of independent agencies — the FTC, NLRB, FEC, and others — from being fired by the president without cause, insulating them from partisan pressure. On **June 29, 2026**, the Supreme Court **overturned that precedent** in *Trump v. Slaughter* (6-3), holding that the president may remove FTC commissioners at will. The ruling validates Trump's 2025 firing of Commissioner Rebecca Slaughter and hands the "unitary executive" theory — a centerpiece of Project 2025 — its biggest victory yet. In a separate 5-4 holding the same day (*Trump v. Cook*), the Court carved out the Federal Reserve, keeping for-cause protection for its governors.

### Key Points

- The ruling calls into question removal protections at the **NLRB, FERC, MSPB, and other multimember agencies** [source: Venable LLP](https://www.venable.com/insights/publications/2026/06/supreme-court-overrules-humphreys-executor)

- Independent agencies were designed with bipartisan, fixed-term structures to keep regulation insulated from election-cycle politics

## Analysis

### What This Means

Removing for-cause protection lets a president reshape "independent" regulators — antitrust, labor, energy, communications — to match the administration's priorities in real time. Critics warn this collapses a structural check that has governed the administrative state since the New Deal.

### Who's Affected

- **Regulated industries and consumers** — enforcement can now swing sharply with each administration
- **Agency staff** whose leadership can be replaced at will
- **The balance of power** between Congress (which created these agencies) and the executive
