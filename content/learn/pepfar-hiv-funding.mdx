# The PEPFAR Funding Cliff

## Quick Summary

PEPFAR — the President's Emergency Plan for AIDS Relief — has been credited with saving more than 25 million lives since 2003. In 2026 it faces a **funding cliff**: after **USAID was formally dissolved on July 1** and its functions folded into the State Department, roughly **120 CDC-administered PEPFAR awards serving more than 8.7 million HIV patients** are set to **expire by September 30, 2026** with no replacement mechanism in place. Mozambique, Tanzania, and South Africa — where the U.S. ended HIV-program funding on June 19 — are among the hardest hit. A CDC official described the situation as "the end of PEPFAR."

- The administration says ~85% of former USAID programming has been cut since 2025
- Disruption to Red Sea and Strait of Hormuz shipping from the Iran war is compounding aid-delivery costs

## Analysis

### What This Means

A lapse in continuous HIV treatment doesn't just stall progress — interrupted antiretroviral therapy leads to drug resistance, new infections, and deaths. The September 30 deadline is a hard cliff unless Congress or the State Department creates a bridge.

### Who's Affected

- **8.7 million HIV patients** dependent on PEPFAR-funded treatment
- **Frontline clinics and health workers** in the hardest-hit countries
- **Global public health**, as interrupted treatment fuels resistant strains
