# Schedule F: The Political Purge of the Civil Service

## Quick Summary

**The Schedule F final rule is now in effect.** On March 8-9, 2026, the Office of Personnel Management's (OPM) final regulation took effect, allowing agencies to reclassify up to 50,000 career federal employees into a new "Schedule Policy/Career" category — stripping their civil service protections, due process rights, and appeal rights. This is no longer a proposed rule or executive order: it is binding federal regulation. Reclassified employees can be suspended, demoted, or fired without standard procedures. AFGE (the largest federal employee union) is expected to file suit challenging the final rule. Combined with Executive Order 14192's impossible 10-for-1 deregulation mandate, this creates agencies that cannot regulate and have no institutional memory.

### Key Points

- **The Schedule F final rule took effect March 8-9, 2026.** OPM published the final "Schedule Policy/Career" regulation in the Federal Register, converting Executive Order 14171 into binding federal regulation. Agencies can now begin reclassifying employees immediately. ### [+] What the Final Rule Does

**From Executive Order to Binding Regulation:**

Executive Order 14171 (signed January 20, 2025) created the Schedule F concept. The OPM proposed rule followed in April 2025. The **final rule**, published in the Federal Register and effective March 8-9, 2026, converts this into binding federal regulation that:

- Creates a new employment category called "Schedule Policy/Career" (renamed from "Schedule F")
- Defines which positions qualify as "policy-influencing"
- Establishes the reclassification process agencies must follow
- Removes civil service protections from reclassified employees
- Allows agencies to begin reclassifying positions immediately

This is a critical legal distinction: an executive order can be challenged as exceeding presidential authority, but a finalized regulation that went through notice-and-comment rulemaking has a stronger legal foundation. The AFGE lawsuit will need to challenge the rule on different grounds than NTEU's challenge to the EO.

[source: Federal Register — OPM Final Rule](https://www.federalregister.gov/) | [source: Federal News Network](https://federalnewsnetwork.com/workforce/2026/03/federal-employees-face-looming-finalization-of-schedule-policy-career/)

### [+] What Reclassified Employees Lose

Employees reclassified to Schedule Policy/Career lose:

- **Tenure protections** — Can be fired at will, without cause
- **Due process rights** — No right to a hearing before termination
- **MSPB appeal rights** — Cannot appeal adverse actions to the Merit Systems Protection Board
- **Protection from political retaliation** — No recourse if fired for refusing to carry out unlawful orders
- **Recruitment and retention incentives** — No eligibility for bonuses to attract/keep talent
- **Student loan repayment eligibility** — Lose a key benefit used to recruit qualified professionals
- **Presidential Rank Awards eligibility** — Cannot receive the highest federal employee honors

**Who is targeted:**

The 50,000+ positions eligible include:

- Scientists at EPA, FDA, NOAA, CDC
- Economists at Treasury, Labor, Commerce
- Lawyers at DOJ, agency counsel offices
- Policy analysts across all agencies
- Budget analysts and program evaluators
- Intelligence analysts (non-uniformed)

[source: FedSupport Hub](https://fedsupport.org/resources/resource-library/faq-schedule-policy-career-formerly-schedule-f/) | [source: EPI Analysis](https://www.epi.org/policywatch/eo-restoring-accountability-to-policy-influencing-positions-within-the-federal-workforce/)

### [+] The 10-for-1 Deregulation Mandate

**Regulatory Freeze:**

Executive Order 14192 mandates that for every **one new regulation** issued, agencies must identify and repeal **ten existing regulations**, with a net zero regulatory cost cap.

**Practical Effect:**

Agencies like EPA, OSHA, and FDA cannot issue new safety standards because they cannot find enough "old" rules to repeal. This creates a de facto regulatory freeze without Congress repealing any laws.

Example: If EPA wants to issue a new clean air standard, they must first repeal 10 existing environmental protections. Those protections have constituencies who will fight repeal. Result: No new standards get issued.

[source: EPA — EO 14192 Implementation](https://www.epa.gov/laws-regulations/executive-order-14192-unleashing-prosperity-through-deregulation)

## Analysis

### What This Means

**This Is Now Real:**

As of March 8-9, 2026, Schedule F is no longer a proposal or an executive order awaiting implementation. It is a finalized federal regulation. Agencies can begin reclassifying career employees immediately. Reclassified employees can be fired without cause, without a hearing, and without appeal.

**The Institutional Memory Wipe:**

Schedule F is not just about firing employees who resist administration policies. It enables the removal of career staff who understand:
- The legal history of regulations
- Why certain rules exist
- What happened when similar approaches were tried before
- How to implement complex programs

By replacing these experts with political loyalists, the administration removes internal checks on executive power.

**The Regulatory Paralysis:**

The 10-for-1 rule makes governing mathematically impossible. This achieves deregulation without Congress repealing any laws.

**Combined Effect:**

Schedule F + 10-for-1 = agencies staffed by political appointees who cannot regulate and have no institutional memory of why regulations existed.

### Who Is Affected

- **50,000+ federal employees** now face potential reclassification and firing for political reasons
- **Public safety**: EPA, OSHA, FDA staffing cuts degrade enforcement
- **Scientific research**: NOAA, CDC, NIH expertise loss affects forecasting, disease surveillance
- **Future administrations**: Institutional knowledge, once lost, takes decades to rebuild
- **Regulated industries**: Uncertainty about which rules will be repealed creates compliance confusion
- **The public**: Agencies that protect clean air, safe food, workplace safety, and financial stability are being hollowed out

### Legal/Policy Context

**Civil Service History:**

The civil service system was created after President Garfield's assassination by a disappointed office-seeker in 1881. The Pendleton Act (1883) established merit-based hiring and protections from political firing. Schedule F represents the most significant rollback of these protections in 140 years.

**Legal Challenges:**

Two separate legal tracks are now active:

1. **NTEU v. Trump** — The National Treasury Employees Union's challenge to the original Executive Order 14171, arguing it violates the Civil Service Reform Act, due process protections, and congressional intent. Status: ongoing.

2. **AFGE (expected)** — The American Federation of Government Employees is expected to challenge the OPM final rule specifically, which has a stronger legal foundation than the EO because it went through notice-and-comment rulemaking. The legal strategy will likely focus on whether OPM exceeded its statutory authority and whether the rule is "arbitrary and capricious" under the Administrative Procedure Act.

**What to Watch:**

- Whether courts issue preliminary injunctions blocking reclassifications while litigation proceeds
- How quickly agencies begin reclassifying employees
- Whether Congress takes legislative action to block or defund implementation
- The scope and timing of AFGE's lawsuit
