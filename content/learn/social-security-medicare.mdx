# Social Security & Medicare: The Solvency Crisis

## Quick Summary

The OBBBA has **accelerated Social Security insolvency to 2032** and Medicare insolvency by a year, according to the Committee for a Responsible Federal Budget. Medicare's Hospital Insurance Trust Fund is now projected to be exhausted by **2040** — down from 2052 projected in March 2025. Without new legislation, OMB must issue a sequestration order to reduce Medicare spending by an estimated **$45 billion** for FY2026. Social Security insolvency would trigger an automatic **28% benefit cut** for all 70.6 million recipients — a typical retired couple faces an **$18,400/year cut**. The OBBBA's senior deduction reduces trust fund revenue by **~$30 billion/year** but is temporary (expires 2028) and only benefits 46% of senior filers — not the permanent "no tax on Social Security" that was promised. DOGE cut SSA by **~7,500 employees** (13%) to a **60-year staffing low**. **47 field offices** are slated for closure. Disability determination takes **236 days**; over **1 million people** wait on appeals. Eliminating the taxable maximum would fix **67%** of the long-range shortfall — but every year of inaction makes the fix more painful.

## Facts

### Key Points

- **Social Security trust fund insolvent by 2032.** The CBO's February 2026 baseline projects the OASI (Old-Age and Survivors Insurance) Trust Fund will be depleted in FY2032 (beginning October 2031). At that point, benefits would be automatically cut **28%** across the board — by law, only incoming payroll tax revenue can be paid out. The 2025 Trustees Report projected 2033; CBO moved it up a year due to OBBBA revenue losses [source: CBO February 2026 Social Security Baseline](https://www.cbo.gov/system/files/2026-02/51308-2026-02-socialsecurity.pdf) | [source: SSA 2025 Trustees Report](https://www.ssa.gov/oact/TR/2025/II_A_highlights.html)

- **Medicare lost 12 years of solvency in under one year.** The CBO now projects Medicare's Hospital Insurance (Part A) trust fund will be depleted by **2040** — down from 2052 before the OBBBA. Starting in 2040, Medicare Part A benefits face automatic cuts beginning at **8%** and climbing to **10% by 2056** [source: Fortune — Trump Erased 12 Years of Medicare Funding](https://fortune.com/2026/02/23/how-trump-wiped-out-12-years-of-medicare-funding-cbo-one-big-beautiful-bill/) | [source: KFF — Medicare Financing FAQ](https://www.kff.org/medicare/issue-brief/faqs-on-medicare-financing-and-trust-fund-solvency/)

- **OBBBA senior deduction: promise vs. reality.** The One Big Beautiful Bill Act created a $4,000 standard deduction for individuals 65+ ($8,000 for couples), effective 2025–2028. The White House claimed 88% of retirees would pay no tax on benefits. The Tax Policy Center found only **46% of senior tax-filing households** actually benefit. Cost: **$91 billion** in reduced revenue over 4 years — directly reducing Social Security and Medicare trust fund income by ~$30B/year. The deduction is **temporary** and is **not** a permanent elimination of Social Security benefit taxation [source: CRFB — OBBBA Would Accelerate Insolvency](https://www.crfb.org/blogs/obbba-would-accelerate-social-security-medicare-insolvency) | [source: IRS — OBBBA Senior Deduction](https://www.irs.gov/newsroom/one-big-beautiful-bill-act-tax-deductions-for-working-americans-and-seniors)

- **DOGE gutted SSA to a 60-year staffing low.** SSA lost ~7,500 employees (~13% of workforce) — the largest staffing cut in SSA history. **47 field offices** slated for closure, plus 6 of 10 regional offices. One staff member now serves **1,480 beneficiaries** (3x the 1967 ratio). An estimated **2 million additional annual trips** will be forced on seniors for in-person assistance. A DOGE employee signed an agreement to share sensitive Social Security data with an outside political advocacy group [source: EPI — What Is DOGE Doing to Social Security](https://www.epi.org/blog/what-is-doge-doing-to-social-security/) | [source: AFGE — 1 Employee per 1,480 Beneficiaries](https://www.afge.org/article/due-to-doge-cuts-1-ssa-employee-is-expected-to-serve-1480-beneficiaries/)

- **Disability determination: 236 days.** Average wait for initial disability decisions is **236 days**; appeals average **277 days**. Over **1 million people** are waiting on a disability appeal [source: Senate Democrats — DOGE Cutting SSA to the Bone](https://www.democrats.senate.gov/newsroom/press-releases/doge-is-cutting-the-social-security-administration-to-the-bone)

- **A typical retired couple faces an $18,400/year cut** at insolvency. The average retired worker would lose **$580/month** ($6,960/year). The average disabled worker would lose **$456/month** ($5,477/year) [source: CRFB — Social Security Turns 90](https://www.crfb.org/blogs/social-security-turns-90-its-racing-towards-insolvency)

- **Multiple reform bills pending.** The Social Security Expansion Act (S.770) would apply payroll tax to earnings above $250,000 and increase benefits. The Medicare and Social Security Fair Share Act would set a $400,000 threshold and apply to investment income. SSA estimates that eliminating the taxable maximum entirely would fix **67% of the long-range actuarial shortfall** [source: Congress.gov — S.770](https://www.congress.gov/bill/119th-congress/senate-bill/770) | [source: SSA Solvency Proposals](https://www.ssa.gov/oact/solvency/index.html)

- **70.6 million Americans** currently receive Social Security benefits. **69.7 million** are enrolled in Medicare. **180 million workers** pay into the system. Every American under 60 faces a potential 28% benefit cut without reform [source: SSA Monthly Statistical Snapshot](https://www.ssa.gov/policy/docs/quickfacts/stat_snapshot/) | [source: SSA 2026 COLA Fact Sheet](https://www.ssa.gov/news/en/cola/factsheets/2026.html)

### [+] How OBBBA Accelerated the Crisis

**The Senior Deduction (Section of OBBBA):**

- Creates a $4,000 standard deduction for individuals 65+ ($8,000 for couples), effective 2025–2028
- Phases out at modified AGI over $75,000 ($150,000 for joint filers)
- **Cost:** $91 billion in reduced federal revenue over 4 years (Joint Committee on Taxation)
- Revenue from taxation of Social Security benefits flows directly into the OASI and HI trust funds
- Reducing this revenue by ~$30B/year accelerated both Social Security and Medicare insolvency

**What was promised vs. what was delivered:**

| Promise | Reality |
|---------|---------|
| "No tax on Social Security" (permanent) | Temporary deduction (expires 2028) |
| Benefits 88% of retirees (White House) | Benefits 46% of senior tax filers (Tax Policy Center) |
| Protects Social Security | Accelerated insolvency by ~1 year (SS) and 12 years (Medicare) |

[source: CRFB — OBBBA Would Accelerate Insolvency](https://www.crfb.org/blogs/obbba-would-accelerate-social-security-medicare-insolvency)

**Social Security Fairness Act (signed January 5, 2025):**

Repealed the Windfall Elimination Provision (WEP) and Government Pension Offset (GPO), benefiting 2.8 million public employees (teachers, firefighters, police) with up to $587/month increases. Cost: $195 billion over a decade. Advanced Social Security insolvency by approximately 6 months.

[source: SSA — Social Security Fairness Act](https://www.ssa.gov/benefits/retirement/social-security-fairness-act.html)

**Medicaid cuts (indirect impact on seniors):**

The OBBBA's $793 billion to $1.02 trillion in Medicaid cuts over 10 years are devastating for seniors because Medicaid pays for **61% of all long-term care** in the US and 70%+ of Home and Community-Based Services. Low-income seniors who rely on both programs face a double squeeze: reduced Social Security and reduced Medicaid.

[source: Center for American Progress](https://www.americanprogress.org/article/the-truth-about-the-one-big-beautiful-bill-acts-cuts-to-medicaid-and-medicare/)

### [+] Pending Reform Legislation

**Revenue-focused bills:**

- **Social Security Expansion Act (S.770):** Applies payroll tax to earnings above $250,000 (currently capped at $184,500). Increases benefits. Combines OASI and DI into single trust fund [source: Congress.gov — S.770](https://www.congress.gov/bill/119th-congress/senate-bill/770)

- **Medicare and Social Security Fair Share Act:** Sets $400,000 threshold for Social Security payroll tax, also applying to investment income. Closes pass-through business loophole for Medicare taxes

**Benefit-focused bills:**

- **Social Security Emergency Inflation Relief Act (S.3078):** Emergency payments to SS, SSI, railroad retirement, and veterans benefit recipients [source: Congress.gov — S.3078](https://www.congress.gov/bill/119th-congress/senate-bill/3078/text)

- **Social Security and Medicare Lock-Box Act (H.R.1221):** Prevents trust fund reserves from being invested in anything other than U.S. obligations [source: Congress.gov — H.R.1221](https://www.congress.gov/bill/119th-congress/house-bill/1221/all-info)

- **We Can't Wait Act of 2026** (Senators Collins & Hassan, bipartisan): Addresses the 5-month disability insurance waiting period

**Expert reform frameworks (Penn Wharton Budget Model):**

| Option | Revenue Changes | Benefit Changes | GDP Impact by 2060 |

| Option C (Benefit) | +1.0pp payroll tax | PIA reduction + retirement age to 69 | +6% GDP |

SSA estimates that **eliminating the taxable maximum entirely** would fix **67% of the long-range actuarial shortfall**.

## Analysis

### What This Means

Social Security and Medicare are the two largest and most popular federal programs, relied on by over 70 million Americans each. The gap between political rhetoric — "not cut one penny" — and actual policy outcomes is staggering. The OBBBA's senior deduction was marketed as fulfilling a campaign promise to eliminate taxes on Social Security. In reality, it's a temporary measure that benefits fewer than half of senior tax filers while accelerating the very insolvency that will trigger automatic benefit cuts.

**2032 is only 6 years away.** Every year of inaction narrows the options. Fixing the shortfall through revenue alone (lifting the payroll tax cap) becomes more expensive each year. Fixing it through benefit cuts becomes more painful. The nonpartisan SSA actuaries have published a full menu of reform options — the solutions exist. What's missing is political will.

The DOGE staffing crisis adds an administrative dimension to the policy crisis. Even if benefits are preserved on paper, they're meaningless if people can't access them — if disability determinations take 236 days, if field offices close, if phone calls go unanswered 76% of the time.

### Who's Affected

- **70.6 million Social Security recipients** — face automatic 28% cut by 2032 without reform
- **69.7 million Medicare enrollees** — face automatic cuts starting 2040
- **7.1 million disabled workers on SSDI** — face both benefit cuts and 236-day determination waits
- **2.8 million public employees** — benefited from Fairness Act but face same insolvency cliff
- **180 million workers** paying into the system — paying for benefits they may receive at 72 cents on the dollar
- **Every American under 60** — faces a retirement with significantly reduced benefits unless Congress acts
- **Low-income seniors** — hit hardest: depend most on Social Security (provides 90%+ of income for lowest-income quintile) and most on Medicaid for long-term care
