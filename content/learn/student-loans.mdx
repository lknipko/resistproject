# Student Loan Crisis: The OBBBA Overhaul

## Quick Summary

**The overhaul took effect July 1, 2026.** The One Big Beautiful Bill Act (P.L. 119-21) slashed **$300 billion** from federal financial aid — the most significant rewrite in a generation. As of July 1, **SAVE, PAYE, and ICR are closed to new borrowers**, replaced by just two options: the income-driven **Repayment Assistance Plan (RAP)** and a fixed **Tiered Standard Plan**; existing SAVE/PAYE/ICR borrowers must transition by July 1, 2028. **Grad PLUS loans are discontinued** and **Parent PLUS borrowing is capped** — and Parent PLUS borrowers taking loans after July 1 **lose PSLF access entirely**, since RAP (their only IDR option) doesn't count toward it. IDR forgiveness is now **taxable** (the exemption expired January 1, 2026), creating tax "bombs" averaging **$5,800–$10,000**. A new final rule **narrowing PSLF's "qualifying employer"** definition — to exclude organizations found to have a "substantial illegal purpose" — also took effect July 1, drawing **three lawsuits, including one from a coalition of 21 states and D.C.** Up to **13 million borrowers** could face default by the end of 2026. The **$1.7 trillion** federal loan portfolio is being transferred from the Education Department to the Treasury Department.

## Facts

### Key Points

- **SAVE plan eliminated — 7.5 million borrowers affected.** The 8th Circuit Court of Appeals issued a final ruling on March 9, 2026 effectively ending the SAVE (Saving on a Valuable Education) plan. On March 10, the Department of Education confirmed it cannot implement SAVE. Starting July 1, servicers will issue notices instructing borrowers to exit SAVE. Borrowers who fail to choose a new plan within 90 days will be automatically enrolled in the Standard Plan. Upwards of half of all SAVE enrollees had $0 monthly payments — they could go from paying nothing to hundreds of dollars per month. A borrower earning $40,000/year would have paid ~$40/month under SAVE but pays ~$132/month under RAP [source: U.S. Dept. of Education — Next Steps for SAVE Borrowers](https://www.ed.gov/about/news/press-release/us-department-of-education-announces-next-steps-borrowers-enrolled-unlawful-save-plan) | [source: CNBC — 7 Million Borrowers Face SAVE Deadline](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/30/student-loan-borrowers-deadline-save.html)

- **Grad PLUS loans discontinued for new borrowers starting July 1, 2026.** Previously, graduate students could borrow up to the full cost of attendance with no cap. Over 440,000 graduate students per year could be forced to turn to private loans. The average borrower who replaces their entire Grad PLUS loan with a private option would pay an additional **$10,885 in interest**. Current borrowers get up to 3 additional academic years under old terms [source: Saving for College — Grad PLUS Loans Ending](https://www.savingforcollege.com/article/grad-plus-loan-changes-2026) | [source: Protect Borrowers — OBBBA Deep Dive](https://protectborrowers.org/resource/deep-dive-the-obbba-law-makes-paying-for-college-more-expensive-and-risky/)

- **Parent PLUS loans capped at $20,000/year, $65,000 lifetime.** Between 29% and nearly half of Parent PLUS borrowers may be forced to private loans. **Critical deadline: Parents who have NOT consolidated into a Direct Consolidation Loan by June 30, 2026 permanently lose access to ALL income-driven repayment plans.** After consolidation, borrowers must enroll in ICR before July 1, 2028 or lose IDR eligibility permanently [source: Student Loan Borrowers Assistance — Parent PLUS](https://studentloanborrowerassistance.org/do-you-have-parent-plus-loans-act-now-to-lower-your-payments-before-options-disappear/) | [source: CNBC — Parent PLUS Repayment Deadline](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/01/student-loan-parent-plus-borrowers-deadline.html)

- **IDR forgiveness is now taxable.** The American Rescue Plan Act exemption expired January 1, 2026. Forgiveness after 20–30 years of IDR payments is now treated as taxable income. Average forgiven balance (~$49,000) could produce a federal tax liability of **$5,800–$10,000**. Six-figure balances could exceed $20,000 in taxes. PSLF forgiveness remains tax-free [source: Bankrate — IDR Forgiveness Taxable 2026](https://www.bankrate.com/loans/student-loans/idr-student-loan-forgiveness-becomes-taxable-in-2026/) | [source: NASFAA — Student Loan Forgiveness Taxable in 2026](https://www.nasfaa.org/news-item/37947/Welcome_to_2026_Some_Student_Loan_Forgiveness_Is_Now_Taxable)

- **PSLF preserved but new employer rule.** A new "substantial illegal purpose" rule allows the Department of Education to disqualify employers from PSLF if they engage in "illegal activities" such as "aiding and abetting violations of federal immigration laws." Cities of Boston, Chicago, San Francisco, and Albuquerque have sued. For new borrowers after July 1, 2026, RAP is the only PSLF-qualifying IDR plan. New Parent PLUS loans have no path to PSLF [source: ABA — PSLF Final Rule](https://www.americanbar.org/advocacy/governmental_legislative_work/publications/washingtonletter/november-25-wl/pslf-final-rule-1125wl/) | [source: Brookings — PSLF Past, Present, Future](https://www.brookings.edu/articles/the-past-present-and-future-of-the-public-service-loan-forgiveness-program/)

- **13 million borrowers could face default by end of 2026.** CFPB received a record 22,900 student loan complaints (July 2024–June 2025). Roughly one-quarter of all federal borrowers may face default [source: Protect Borrowers — Student Loan Default Cliff](https://protectborrowers.org/falling-off-the-student-loan-default-cliff/)

- **$1.7 trillion loan portfolio being transferred to Treasury.** In March 2026, the Department of Education signed an interagency agreement to transfer the entire federal student loan portfolio to the Department of Treasury in three phases: (1) defaulted debt collection, (2) non-defaulted loan operations, (3) FAFSA administration. No timeline set for phases 2 and 3 [source: NPR — Student Loans Moving to Treasury](https://www.npr.org/2026/03/19/nx-s1-5753906/student-loans-trump-treasury)

- **"Do No Harm" institutional accountability:** For the first time, Congress linked student loan eligibility to verified post-graduation earnings. Programs must show graduates' median earnings exceed typical high school graduates (undergrad) or bachelor's-degree holders (grad). Programs failing 2 of 3 consecutive years lose loan eligibility. Implementation begins 2027; loss of eligibility begins July 2028. Only ~1.8% of students are currently in failing programs [source: Brookings — How OBBBA Reshapes Student Lending](https://www.brookings.edu/articles/how-obbba-reshapes-student-lending/)

### [+] SAVE Plan Elimination

**What was SAVE?**

The Saving on a Valuable Education (SAVE) plan was the most generous income-driven repayment plan ever offered by the federal government. It capped payments at 5% of discretionary income for undergraduate loans (10% for graduate), protected the first 225% of the federal poverty level from counting as income, and provided government interest subsidies so balances wouldn't grow. Forgiveness came after 20 years (undergrad) or 25 years (grad).

**What happened:**

- The 8th Circuit Court of Appeals issued a final ruling on March 9, 2026 vacating the SAVE plan, finding the Biden administration exceeded its statutory authority in creating it
- On February 27, 2026, a federal judge dismissed the Department of Education's attempted settlement that would have preserved some SAVE features
- 7.5 million borrowers were enrolled in SAVE at the time of the ruling

**The 90-day transition window (July 1 – ~September 30, 2026):**

- Starting July 1, federal loan servicers will issue notices instructing borrowers to exit SAVE
- Borrowers have 90 days to choose a new plan: RAP, IBR (for pre-July 2026 loans), or Tiered Standard
- Borrowers who don't act will be auto-enrolled in the Standard Repayment Plan or Tiered Standard Plan
- Payment shock is real: half of SAVE enrollees were paying $0/month

[source: 8th Circuit SAVE Ruling](https://www.acainternational.org/news/federal-appeals-court-issues-final-ruling-vacating-save-plan/)
[source: Student Loan Borrowers Assistance — SAVE Plan Ending](https://studentloanborrowerassistance.org/the-save-plan-is-ending-what-borrowers-in-save-need-to-know/)

### [+] Grad PLUS Loans Discontinued

**Effective July 1, 2026:** Grad PLUS loans are discontinued for new borrowers.

**New graduate borrowing limits:**

| Category | Annual Limit | Lifetime Aggregate |
|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| Graduate programs | $20,500 | $100,000 |
| Professional programs (law, medicine) | $50,000 | $200,000 |
| Combined lifetime cap (all federal loans excl. Parent PLUS) | — | $257,500 |

**Legacy provision:** Current Grad PLUS borrowers get up to 3 additional academic years (or program completion, whichever comes first) to continue borrowing under the old terms. Existing loans do NOT count toward the new lifetime limits.

**Financial impact:** The average Grad PLUS borrower who replaces their entire loan with a private market option would pay an additional **$10,885 in interest** due to higher rates and less favorable terms.

[source: Harvard SFS — Key Changes from OBBBA](https://sfs.harvard.edu/2025-changes-federal-student-loans)
[source: Inside Higher Ed — Grad PLUS Borrowers Face Caps](https://www.insidehighered.com/news/government/student-aid-policy/2026/04/22/some-grad-plus-borrowers-could-face-loan-caps)

### [+] Parent PLUS Loan Caps

**Effective July 1, 2026:**

- Annual cap: **$20,000 per dependent student** (previously up to cost of attendance)
- Lifetime cap: **$65,000 per dependent student** (regardless of whether one or more parents borrow)

**CRITICAL DEADLINE — Parent PLUS consolidation:**

Parents who have NOT consolidated into a Direct Consolidation Loan **by June 30, 2026** permanently lose access to ALL income-driven repayment plans (ICR, IBR, RAP). The Department of Education recommends applying no later than April 1, 2026 (processing takes 4–6 weeks) — that recommended deadline has passed, but late applicants should apply immediately at StudentAid.gov. After consolidation, borrowers must enroll in ICR before July 1, 2028 or lose IDR eligibility permanently.

**Financial impact:** Between 29% and nearly half of Parent PLUS borrowers may be forced to turn to private loans. The average Parent PLUS borrower who exceeds the $65,000 cap would pay an additional **$4,608 in interest**.

[source: NASFAA — Parent PLUS Borrowers Must Act](https://www.nasfaa.org/parent_plus_borrowers_to_keep_income-driven_repayment_access_loan_forgiveness_eligibility)
[source: NerdWallet — Parent PLUS Forgiveness Deadline](https://www.nerdwallet.com/student-loans/learn/parent-plus-forgiveness-deadline)

### [+] New Repayment Plans: RAP and Tiered Standard

**Repayment Assistance Plan (RAP) — Available July 1, 2026**

RAP is the ONLY income-driven repayment plan for loans disbursed on or after July 1, 2026.

- **Payment formula:** Rate = min(10%, floor(AGI / $10,000) + 1)%. Monthly payment = (AGI × Rate) / 12
- **Example:** $45,000 AGI → floor(45000/10000) + 1 = 5% rate → $45,000 × 5% / 12 = **$187.50/month**
- **Minimum payment:** $10/month (for AGI under $10,000)
- **$50 deduction per dependent** from monthly payment
- Government subsidizes any uncovered interest and provides up to $50 toward principal for on-time payments
- Balance will not grow as long as required monthly payments are made
- **Forgiveness after 360 qualifying payments (30 years)** — up from 20–25 years under previous plans
- Qualifies for PSLF (120 payments / 10 years)
- **Parent PLUS loans are NOT eligible** for RAP

**Tiered Standard Plan — Available July 1, 2026**

- Fixed payments (not income-driven) over 10, 15, 20, or 25 years depending on total balance
- No income recertification required
- No forgiveness timeline
- No income-based payment adjustments

[source: CRS — The Repayment Assistance Plan (RAP)](https://www.congress.gov/crs-product/IF13075)
[source: NerdWallet — What Is RAP](https://www.nerdwallet.com/student-loans/learn/what-is-the-new-repayment-assistance-plan-rap-for-student-loans)

### [+] PSLF Changes

Public Service Loan Forgiveness itself is **preserved** — 120 qualifying payments while working full-time for a government or nonprofit employer.

**Key changes:**
- RAP counts as a qualifying repayment plan for PSLF
- For new borrowers after July 1, 2026, RAP is the **only PSLF-qualifying IDR plan**
- PSLF forgiveness remains **federally tax-free**
- **New "substantial illegal purpose" rule:** The Department of Education can disqualify employers from PSLF if they engage in "illegal activities" such as "aiding and abetting violations of federal immigration laws"
- New Parent PLUS loans disbursed on or after July 1, 2026 have **no path to PSLF**
- Cities of Boston, Chicago, San Francisco, and Albuquerque have **sued** the Trump administration over the employer eligibility changes

[source: Dept. of Education — PSLF Final Rule](https://www.ed.gov/about/news/press-release/us-department-of-education-announces-final-rule-public-service-loan-forgiveness-protect-american-taxpayers)
[source: Student Loan Lawyer — PSLF Changes 2026](https://www.tateesq.com/learn/pslf-changes-2026)

### [+] IDR Forgiveness Now Taxable

The American Rescue Plan Act exemption that made IDR forgiveness tax-free expired **January 1, 2026**. IDR forgiveness (after 20–25 years under existing plans, or 30 years under RAP) is now treated as **taxable income**.

**The math:**
- Average forgiven IDR balance (~$49,000) → federal tax liability of **$5,800–$10,000**
- Six-figure forgiven balances (common for graduate borrowers) → tax bills exceeding **$20,000**
- PSLF forgiveness remains **tax-free**

**Mitigation options:**
- **IRS insolvency exclusion** (Form 982): If your total liabilities exceed your total assets at the time of forgiveness, the forgiven amount may be excludable
- **Offer in compromise** (Form 656): Negotiate a reduced tax payment with the IRS
- **Payment plan** (Form 9465): Spread the tax bill over time

[source: Bankrate — IDR Forgiveness Taxable 2026](https://www.bankrate.com/loans/student-loans/idr-student-loan-forgiveness-becomes-taxable-in-2026/)

### [+] Institutional Accountability ("Do No Harm")

For the first time, Congress has linked broad student loan eligibility to verified post-graduation earnings:

- **Undergraduate programs** must show graduates' median earnings (4 years post-completion) exceed those of typical high school graduates
- **Graduate programs** must exceed median bachelor's-degree-holder earnings
- Programs failing for **2 of 3 consecutive years** lose student loan eligibility
- Undergraduate certificate programs are exempt
- Only ~1.8% of students are currently in programs that fail the test
- **Implementation:** Notifications begin 2027, loss of eligibility begins July 2028

[source: American University — OBBBA Accountability Standard](https://www.american.edu/spa/peer/one-big-beautiful-bill-acts-new-accountability-standard.cfm)

## Analysis

### What This Means

The OBBBA student loan overhaul represents the most significant rewrite of federal student aid since the creation of income-driven repayment plans. The direction is clear: shifting the cost of higher education from the federal government back to students and families.

**For current borrowers:** The SAVE elimination creates an immediate crisis. Millions of borrowers who had manageable (or zero) payments face a sudden increase with the 90-day clock ticking. The taxability of IDR forgiveness adds a financial surprise that will hit borrowers decades from now. And the Parent PLUS consolidation deadline is a trap — borrowers who miss it face permanently higher payments with no recourse.

**For future students:** Graduate education becomes dramatically more expensive. The Grad PLUS elimination means medical students, law students, and PhD candidates must turn to private lenders at higher rates. The Parent PLUS caps mean families at schools with tuition above $20,000/year must find alternative funding. The net effect is to make higher education more stratified by wealth.

**The default crisis:** With up to 13 million borrowers potentially facing default by end of 2026, the combination of SAVE elimination, payment shock, and inadequate servicer capacity could produce the largest student loan default event in history.

### Who's Affected

- **7.5 million SAVE borrowers:** Face immediate payment increases — many from $0/month to hundreds of dollars
- **440,000+ graduate students per year:** Must turn to private loans at higher rates after Grad PLUS ends
- **All Parent PLUS borrowers:** Face caps and a critical June 30 consolidation deadline
- **45 million total student loan borrowers:** Affected by the IDR tax change, Treasury transfer, and servicer disruptions
- **Future students:** Face higher costs, fewer federal options, and more reliance on private debt
- **Public service workers:** PSLF employer rule creates uncertainty about eligibility at government and nonprofit employers
