# Trump Accounts: The Shift to Asset-Based Welfare

## Quick Summary

**Key Point:** Trump Accounts are tax-advantaged savings accounts for children, created by Section 70204 of the One Big Beautiful Bill Act (OBBBA). Every child born 2025-2028 is eligible for a one-time $1,000 federal seed contribution, and any child under 18 can receive up to $5,000/year in family contributions with tax-deferred growth. Contributions open July 4, 2026 via trumpaccounts.gov. While the $1,000 is free money worth claiming, the program's structure overwhelmingly benefits wealthy families who can max out contributions -- a family contributing $5,000/year plus employer matches could accumulate $135,000+ by age 18, while a low-income family gets roughly $3,400 from the seed alone.

### How Trump Accounts Work

**Two Levels of Eligibility:**

1. **Trump Account (any child under 18):** Any child with a Social Security number who is under 18 at the end of the election year can have a Trump Account opened. For 2026 elections, the child must have been born after December 31, 2008. [source: IRS Form 4547 Instructions](https://www.irs.gov/instructions/i4547)

2. **$1,000 Pilot Program (newborns only):** The one-time $1,000 federal seed contribution is available only to U.S. citizens born between January 1, 2025 and December 31, 2028. [source: IRS Proposed Regulations](https://www.irs.gov/newsroom/treasury-irs-issue-proposed-regulations-on-how-to-open-initial-trump-accounts-under-the-one-big-beautiful-bill)

**Account Structure:**

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| **Seed Funding** | $1,000 one-time federal contribution (newborns 2025-2028 only) |
| **Family Contributions** | Up to $5,000 annually (after-tax dollars, tax-deferred growth); limit indexed starting 2028 |
| **Employer Contributions** | Up to $2,500 annually per child (tax-free to employee, deductible for employer) |
| **Investment Options** | Restricted to low-fee index funds (S&P 500 or similar), no leverage, max 0.1% expense ratio |
| **Lock-Up Period** | Funds cannot be withdrawn until child turns 18 |
| **Conversion** | At age 18, account converts to traditional IRA |

[source: IRS Summary](https://www.irs.gov/newsroom/one-big-beautiful-bill-provisions) | [source: KPMG Proposed Regulations](https://kpmg.com/us/en/taxnewsflash/news/2026/03/tnf-proposed-regulations-guidance-regarding-trump-accounts-and-contribution-pilot-program.html)

### How to Open an Account

1. **File IRS Form 4547** ("Trump Account Election") -- can be filed with your 2025 tax return (due April 15, 2026) or separately at any time
2. **Open a custodial account** at a qualifying financial institution
3. **Online portal:** [trumpaccounts.gov](https://trumpaccounts.gov/) expected to go live mid-2026
4. **Contributions begin July 4, 2026** -- no deposits can be made before this date

[source: IRS Form 4547 Instructions](https://www.irs.gov/instructions/i4547) | [source: Brownstein Analysis](https://www.bhfs.com/insight/baby-steps-treasury-issues-first-set-of-proposed-regulations-for-trump-accounts/)

### [+] Corporate Integration

Major employers have begun offering Trump Account contributions as employee benefits:
- Dell has pledged additional contributions for employees' children in specific zip codes
- Other companies are including employer matches in benefits packages
- Employer contributions (up to $2,500/child/year) are tax-free to the employee and deductible for the employer, making this an attractive recruiting tool

## Analysis

### What This Means

**The Policy Shift:**

Trump Accounts represent a fundamental philosophical change in how the government approaches child welfare:

| Traditional Safety Net | Asset-Based Welfare |
|------------------------|---------------------|
| Protect from risk (health coverage, food assistance) | Facilitate risk-taking (investment) |
| Immediate needs | Long-term wealth building |
| Universal access | Favors those who can contribute |

**The Wealth Gap Problem:**

While the $1,000 seed grant reaches all eligible newborns equally, the account structure heavily favors wealthy families:

| Family Income | Likely Outcome |
|---------------|----------------|
| **High income** | Max out $5,000/year + employer match = $135,000+ principal over 18 years + compound growth |
| **Middle income** | Partial contributions when possible |
| **Low income** | Only $1,000 seed + modest growth = perhaps $2,000-3,000 at age 18 |

Over 18 years with investment returns, the gap between wealthy and low-income children's accounts could exceed $200,000.

**The Ideological Swap:**

The OBBBA simultaneously:
- **Cut** Medicaid (social insurance protecting against health risk)
- **Created** Trump Accounts (private asset accumulation)

This is not coincidental. It represents a deliberate shift from collective risk-pooling to individual asset ownership as the model for government support.

### Who Is Affected

- **All newborns (2025-2028)**: Eligible for the $1,000 seed grant
- **Low-income families**: May miss claiming the benefit due to paperwork barriers; cannot afford additional contributions
- **Wealthy families**: Gain a new tax-advantaged vehicle for intergenerational wealth transfer
- **Unbanked families**: Face barriers opening custodial accounts
- **Employers**: New benefit option for recruiting/retention

### What You Should Do

**Claim the $1,000 if eligible:**

Regardless of your views on the policy, if you have a child born 2025-2028, claim the free money:

1. File IRS Form 4547 with your 2025 tax return (due April 15, 2026) or separately
2. Open a custodial account at a qualifying financial institution
3. Wait for activation information from Treasury (expected May 2026)
4. Contributions open July 4, 2026

**Even $1,000 grows:** At 7% annual return over 18 years, $1,000 becomes approximately $3,400. It's free money -- take it.

**For older children:** Even without the $1,000 seed, a Trump Account offers tax-deferred growth on up to $5,000/year. If your child is under 18, consider whether this vehicle makes sense for your family. [source: Saving for College Guide](https://www.savingforcollege.com/article/how-to-open-trump-account-irs-form-4547)
